## Exercise: Causatives (Kausativa)

Form the causative of the following verb and participle forms. The causative is typically formed with the suffix *-aya-* and often involves a strengthening of the root vowel (Guna or Vriddhi).

### 1) With Guna-level (high-stage) root
1. **arhanti** -> *arhayanti* (they cause to be worthy)
2. **prāpya** -> *prāpayya* (having caused to reach)
3. **kupyati** -> *kopayati* (he enrages)
4. **kṛṣati** -> *karṣayati* (he causes to pull/plow)
5. **gantum** -> *gamayitum* (to cause to go)
6. **roditi** -> *rodayati* (he makes [someone] cry)
7. **muhyati** -> *mohayati* (he confuses/deludes)

### 2) With Vriddhi-level (lengthened-stage) root
1. **atti** -> *ādayati* (he feeds / causes to eat)
2. **charati** -> *chārayati* (he causes to walk/behave)
3. **bhavati** -> *bhāvayati* (he causes to be / manifests / meditates)
4. **mriyate** -> *mārayati* (he kills / causes to die)
5. **śṛṇoti** -> *śrāvayati* (he causes to hear / recites)
6. **tiṣṭhati** (root *sthā*) -> *sthāpayati* (he causes to stand / establishes)

### 3) Special Causatives
1. **duh** -> *dūṣayati* (he spoils/corrupts)
2. **pā** (drink) -> *pāyayati* (he causes to drink)
3. **pā** (protect) -> *pālayati* (he protects / rules)
4. **han** -> *ghātayati* (he causes to kill)

## Sentences & Compounds (Causative Usage)

Translate and analyze. Note the relationship between the causative and the simple action:
*Causative: Rāma makes the servant carry the load -> Simple: The servant carries the load.*

1. शत्रुजयाय क्षत्रियो ब्राह्मणेन हरिहरं याजयित्वारीन्योत्स्यते ॥१॥
   To conquer the enemies, the Kṣatriya, having caused a Brahmin to perform a sacrifice to Hari and Hara (*yajayitvā*), will fight the enemies.
   *(Simple action: The Brahmin sacrifices to Hari and Hara.)*
2. गुरुर्बालान्वेदमध्याप्य गृहं गतः ॥२॥
   The teacher, having taught (*adhyāpya*) the Veda to the boys, went home.
   *(Simple action: The boys study the Veda.)*
3. गर्भगृहे देवीप्रतिमा दर्श्यते ॥३॥
   In the inner sanctuary, the image of the goddess is shown (*darśyate* - causative passive).
   *(Simple action: The image is seen.)*
4. पापाद्मोक्षार्थेन सुगत आर्यजनानार्यसत्यानि बोधयति ॥१०॥
   For the sake of liberation from evil, the Buddha (*Sugata*) causes noble people to understand (*bodhayati*) the Noble Truths.
   *(Simple action: Noble people understand the Noble Truths.)*
