## 58.1. Primary Dual Endings (*dvivacanam*)

| Person | Parasmaipada | Ātmanepada (Thematic) | Ātmanepada (Athematic) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1st** | **-vas** | **-vahe** | **-vahe** |
| **2nd** | **-thas** | **-īthe** | **-āthe** |
| **3rd** | **-tas** | **-īte** | **-āte** |

## 58.2. Dual of Thematic Present Classes (Classes 1, 4, 6, 10 & Causatives)

Before *-vas* and *-vahe*, the thematic vowel *-a-* is lengthened to **-ā-**.

*   **Class 1 (bhū)**: *bhavāvas / bhavāvahe* (1st), *bhavethas / bhavethe* (2nd), *bhavatas / bhavete* (3rd).
*   **Class 4 (nṛt)**: *nṛtyāvas / nṛtyāvahe* (1st), *nṛtyathas / nṛtyethe* (2nd), *nṛtyatas / nṛtyete* (3rd). ![](/images/lekt5802.jpg)
*   **Class 6 (tud)**: *tudāvas / tudāvahe* (1st), *tudathas / tudethe* (2nd), *tudatas / tudete* (3rd).
*   **Class 10 / Causatives (cur)**: *corayāvas / corayāvahe* (1st). ![](/images/lekt5803.jpg)

## 58.3. Dual of the Simple Future (*lṛṭ*)

Following the thematic pattern:
*   *dāsyāvas / dāsyāvahe* (from *dā* "to give").
*   *bhaviṣyāvas / bhaviṣyāvahe* (from *bhū* "to be").

## 58.4. Dual of the Passive
*   *tudyāvahe* (1st), *tudyethe* (2nd), *tudyete* (3rd).

## 58.6. The *iṣ*-Aorist (Sigmatic Aorist #5)

Used for **seṭ** roots (those taking the connecting vowel *-i-*).

**Formation**: **Augment + Root + i + s + Athematic Secondary Endings**

The 2nd and 3rd person singular Parasmaipada end in **-īs** and **-īt** respectively.

### Endings Table
| Person | Parasmaipada | Ātmanepada |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| | **Sing. / Plur.** | **Sing. / Plur.** |
| **1st** | **-iṣam / -iṣma** | **-iṣi / -iṣmahi** |
| **2nd** | **-īs / -iṣṭa** | **-iṣṭhās / -idhvam** (or *-iḍhvam*) |
| **3rd** | **-īt / -iṣur** | **-iṣṭa / -iṣata** |

### Root Grades:
*   Roots with a short vowel + single consonant: **Guṇa** in both P and Ā. (e.g., *budh* → **abodhiṣam**).
*   Roots with final vowel: **Vṛddhi** in P, **Guṇa** in Ā. (e.g., *pū* → **apāviṣam / apaviṣi**).
*   Roots in *-ar, -al* and roots *vad* (say), *vraj* (go): **Vṛddhi** in P.

### Paradigms:
*   **pū (9U, "to purify")**: *apāviṣam* (1st P), *apāviṣur* (3rd pl. P); *apaviṣi* (1st Ā), *apaviṣata* (3rd pl. Ā).
*   **budh (1U, "to recognize")**: *abodhiṣam* (1st), *abodhiṣma* (1st pl.), *abodhis* (2nd sg.), *abodhon* (3rd sg.).

### Special Rules:
*   **grah** (to seize) always takes long **-ī ṣ-**: *agrahīṣam*.
*   **jan** (to be born) and **dīp** (to shine) can take the root-aorist passive form in the 3rd per. sg. Ā: **ajani**, **adīpi**.
