# Lesson 8

## 8.1. On the Formation of Nouns

In traditional Indian grammar, a distinction is made between the suffixes used to form nominal stems:

*   kṛt-suffixes (कृत्) (primary suffixes): derive nominal stems, absolutives, and infinitives from verbal roots (dhātu m. = धातु) and verbal stems
*   taddhita-suffixes (तद्धित) (secondary suffixes): are appended to nominal stems and indeclinables to form new nominal stems or indeclinables

Schema:

Root + kṛt-suffix » nominal stem, etc. + taddhita-suffix » new nominal stem, etc.

Nominal stem + case ending » syntactically functional noun

## 8.2. Classification of Nominal Suffixes

*   kṛt (kṛdanta) = कृत् (कृदन्त) 
    *   kārakakṛt कारककृत् » nomina agentis (denoting an agent who performs that which is signified by the root)
    *   kṛtikṛt कृतिकृत् » nomina actionis (denoting the action expressed by the root) or abstract nouns
        
*   taddhita 
    *   viśeṣyataddhita विशेष्यतद्धित » Adjectives
    *   bhāvārthakataddhita (tanmātrataddhita) भावार्थकतद्धित (तन्मात्रतद्धित) » Abstract nouns
    *   tadvattaddhita (तद्वत्तद्धित) » possessive (e.g., -mant -मन्त्, -vant -वन्त्)
    *   tolanataddhita (atiśāyanataddhita) तोलनतद्धित (अतिशायनतद्धित) » intensification (comparative, superlative), comparison
    *   pūraṇataddhita पूरणतद्धित » Ordinal numbers (first, etc.)
    *   vibhaktitaddhita विभक्तितद्धित » in place of case endings (e.g., -tas -तस्, -tra -त्र)
    *   abhūtatadbhāva (cvitaddhita) अभूततद्भाव (च्वितद्धित) » (cvi-formations च्वि, -sāt -सात्)

Classification according to ablaut:

*   guṇya गुण्य: high-grade formation
*   na guṇya न गुण्य : low-grade formation
*   vṛddhyarha वृद्ध्यर्ह : lengthened-grade formation

## 8.3. Some Important Nominal Suffixes

### 8.3.1. -a m. (kṛt)

The kṛt suffix *-a* forms masculine (less frequently neuter) nouns denoting an action or a state indicated by the root; it also occasionally forms adjectives or nouns expressing the agent (*kartṛ*) of the action denoted by the verbal root. For a short penultimate (= vowel before a consonant on which the root ends) or a final root vowel, the *guṇa* (strengthening) or *vṛddhi* (extension) grade is usually substituted.

Examples:

| --- | --- |
| ji 1 P "to conquer"  
जि | jaya m. "winning, victory"  
जय |
| muh 4 P "to be confused"  
मुह् | moha m. "confusion, delusion, error"  
मोह |
| krudh 4 P "to be angry"  
क्रुध् | krodha m. "anger"  
क्रोध |
| kup 4 P "to be angry"  
कुप् | kopa m. "anger"  
कोप |
| lubh 4 P "to desire"  
लुभ् | lobha m. "desire"  
लोभ |
| labh 1 Ā "to obtain"  
लभ् | lābha m. "obtaining, gain"  
लाभ |
| sṛj 6 P "to release, to emanate"  
सृज् | sarga m. "release, emanation, creation" (regarding word sandhi j » g, see later)  
सर्ग |
| śru 5 P "to hear"  
श्रु | śrava m. "hearing"  
श्रव |

### 8.3.2. -ana n. (kṛt)

The kṛt suffix *-ana* primarily forms neuter nouns denoting an action, a state, or the means/instrument through which the action indicated by the root or a verbal stem is brought about. For a short penultimate or a final vowel of the root, the *guṇa* grade is usually substituted.

Examples.

| Root | \+ -ana (k．t) |
| --- | --- |
| gam 1 P "to go"  
गम् | gamana n. "going"  
गमन |
| nī 1 U "to lead"  
नी | nayana n. "(the instrument of leading, i.e.) eye"  
नयन |
| śru 5 P "to hear"  
श्रु | śravaṇa n. "(instrument of hearing =) ear"  
श्रवण |
| kṛ 8 U "to do"  
कृ | kāraṇa n. "(that by which something is done, i.e.) cause, reason"  
कारण |
| bhū 1 P "to become"  
भू | bhavana n. "becoming, arising"  
भवन |
| dṛś 4 P "to see"  
दृश् | darśana n. "seeing, perspective, philosophical system, appearance, specifically: Darśan"  
दर्शन |

### 8.3.3. -tra n. (kṛt)

The kṛt suffix *-tra* forms (mostly) neuter nouns denoting the means or instrument through which the action indicated by the root is brought about. Short penultimates and final vowels of the root are replaced by the *guṇa* grade.

Examples:  
 

| Root | \+ -tra (kṛt) |
| --- | --- |
| nī 1 U "to lead"  
नी | netra n. "(means of leading =) eye"  
नेत्र |
| śru 5 P "to hear"  
श्रु | śrotra "(instrument of hearing =) ear"  
श्रोत्र |
| man 4 Ā "to think"  
मन् | mantra m. (!) "(instrument of thought:) incantation, 'magical' formula (Mantra)"  
मन्त्र |
| tan 8 U "to stretch/expand"  
तन् | tantra n. "web/loom chain"  
तन्त्र |

### 8.3.4. -ti f. (kṛt)

The kṛt suffix *-ti* forms feminine nouns which generally express the action or the state indicated by the root. The form of the root is in the zero grade.

Examples:  
 

| Root | \+ -ti (kṛt) |
| --- | --- |
| śru 5 P "to hear"  
श्रु | śruti f. "hearing, the Veda"  
श्रुति |
| smṛ 1 P "to bring to mind"  
स्मृ | smṛti f. "recollection, memory, tradition, mindfulness"  
स्मृति |
| nī 1 U "to lead"  
नी | nīti f. "leading, guidance, conduct"  
नीति |
| sṛj 6 P "to emanate"  
सृज् | sṛṣṭi f. "emanation, creation"  
सृष्टि |
| dṛś 4 P "to see"  
दृश् | dṛṣṭi f. "gaze, face, way of seeing" (regarding word sandhi, see later)  
दृष्टि |
| gam 1 P "to go"  
गम् | gati f. "gait, course, destination of going" (from \*gm » ga + -ti)  
गति |
| man 4 Ā "to think"  
मन् | mati f. "thinking, thought, opinion" (from \*mn » ma + -ti)  
मति |

\* Note: \* before a form indicates that this form does not occur in Sanskrit but has been theoretically reconstructed as a prerequisite for a specific formation. The zero grade of *gam* is *gəm* = \*gm; the *m* is replaced by *a* as a so-called nasal sonans $\rightarrow$ *ga*; the same applies to *man* $\rightarrow$ *ma*.

### 8.3.5. -tva n., -tā f. (taddhita)

The taddhita suffixes *-tva* (n.) and *-tā* (f.) form abstract nouns from nouns. The form of the underlying nominal stem remains unchanged.

Examples:  
 

| Nominal stem | \+ -tva n. (taddhita) | \+ -tā f. (taddhita) | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| guru 3 "heavy, worthy, m. master"  
गुरु | gurutva n.  
गुरुत्व | gurutā f.  
गुरुता | "heaviness, worthiness, being a teacher (the essence or nature of a teacher)" |
| brāhmaṇa m. "Brahmana"  
ब्राह्मण | brāhmaṇtva n.  
ब्राह्मणत्व | brāhmaṇatā f.  
ब्राह्मणता | "being a Brahmana, that which makes a Brahmana a Brahmana, essence/nature of a Brahmana" |
| deva m. "celestial, god"  
देव |   | devatā f.  
देवता | "deity" |

Both of these formations can practically be applied to any noun. They are very frequent in scholarly Sanskrit works.

## 8.4. The 8th Present Class (tanādi = तनादि = "tan etc.")

Stem Formation:

* Strong stem: Root + -o-
* Weak stem: Root + -u- (before a vowel: -v-)

Example:

tan 5 U तन् "to stretch"

> 3. sg. P tan-o-ti तनोति
> 
> 3. pl. P tan-v-anti तन्वन्ति
> 
> 3. sg. Ā tan-u-te तनुते
> 
> 3. pl. Ā tan-v-ate तन्वते

Note: Regarding the debate as to whether tan is not actually a root of the 5th present class — *tn » ta + no- — cf. Thumb-Hauschild, Handbook of Sanskrit II, 265.

The most significant root belonging to the 8th present class is kṛ 8 U "to do, to make". Its conjugation is quite irregular:

kṛ 8 U "to do, to make"

> 3. sg. P kar-o-ti करोति
> 
> 3. pl. P kur-v-anti कुर्वन्ति
> 
> 3. sg. Ā kur-u-te कुरुते
> 
> 3. pl. Ā kur-v-ate कुर्वते
