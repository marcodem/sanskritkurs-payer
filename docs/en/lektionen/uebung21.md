# Exercise 21

## Exercise

A) Dissolve the following compounds in Sanskrit and translate them:

1. **anādikālikasaṃsāraḥ**: The cycle of rebirths (saṃsāra) without beginning and without a fixed duration.
2. **anādimadhyāntaḥ**: Without beginning, middle, and end.
3. **mahāmaitrīkaruṇācittaḥ**: Possessing a heart (citta) full of great friendliness (maitrī) and compassion (karuṇā).
4. **sarvahatāndhakāraḥ**: He who has destroyed darkness everywhere.

B) Translate:

1. मृतं दहन्नग्निः सतीमपि दहति ॥१॥
   The fire that burns the dead also burns the faithful wife (*satī*).
2. सद्गुरुर्महाकविस्तोत्रैर्महादेवं स्तौति ॥२॥
   The good teacher praises the Great God (*Mahādeva*) with the hymns of great poets.
3. महान्ति फलान्यदन्तो बाला जलमापि पिबन्ति ॥३॥
   The boys, eating large fruits, also drink water.
4. पुजां कुर्वञ्जनो यजते च स्तौति च देवताम् ॥४॥
   While performing worship (*pūjā*), the man sacrifices to and praises the deity.
5. गुरूपनीतनरो द्विजः ॥५॥
   A man initiated [into the Veda] by a teacher is a Twice-born (*dvija*).
6. जितक्रोधो घ्नन्तमप्यरिं न द्वेष्टि । क्रोधजितस्तु द्वेष्टि ॥६॥
   One who has conquered anger does not hate even a killing enemy; but one conquered by anger does hate.
