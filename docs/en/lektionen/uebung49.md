Bāṇa (7th cent. AD): Kādambarī, ed. M. R. Kale, 1968, p. 35f.

Questions of King Śūdraka of Vidiśā to the parrot Vaiśampāyana:

नरपतिरब्रवीत् । आस्तां तावत्सर्वमेवेदम् । अपनयतु नः कुतूहलम् । आवेदयतु भवानादितः प्रभृति कार्त्न्येनात्मनो जन्म कस्मिन्देशे । भवान्कथं जातः । केन वा नाम कृतम् । का माता । कस्ते पिता । कथं वेदानामागमः । कथं शास्त्राणां परिचयः । कुतः कलाः समासादिताः । किं जन्मान्तरानुस्मरणमुत वरप्रदानम् । अथवा विहंगवेषधारी कश्चिच्छन्नं विवससि । क्व वा पूर्वमुषितम् । कियद्वा वयः । कथं पञ्जरबन्धः । कथं चाण्डालहस्तगमनम् । इह वा कथमागमनमिति ।

वैशम्पायनस्तु स्वयमुपजातकुतूहलेन सबहुमानमवनि्पतिना पृष्टो मुहूर्तमिव ध्यात्वा सादरमब्रवीत् । देव मतीयं कथा । यदि कौतुकमाकर्ण्यताम् ॥

> The king spoke: "Let all this be as it is! Relieve our curiosity! Sir! Tell us from the very beginning in its entirety: In which country was your birth? How were you born? By whom was your name given? Who is your mother? Who is your father? How did you attain the Vedas? How did you become familiar with the textbooks (shastras)? From where were the arts acquired? Is it a memory of a previous birth or the granting of a boon? Or are you some hidden being dwelling in the guise of a bird? Where did you live before? How old are you? How were you caught in a cage? And how did you come into the hands of a Cāṇḍāla? Or how did you come here?"
> 
> Vaiśampāyana, however, who had been asked with great respect by the lord of the earth with spontaneously arisen curiosity, reflected for a moment and then spoke with reverence: "Your Majesty, this is a long story. If you are interested, let it be heard!"
