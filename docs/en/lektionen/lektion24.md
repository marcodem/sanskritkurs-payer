# Lesson 24

## 24.1. The Dative (*caturthī* = "fourth case-ending")

1. To express the purpose or intention ("in order to") of an action, one can use a noun with a *kṛt* suffix (denoting an action or state) in the **Dative** (*caturthī*) instead of using the Infinitive (*tumun*).

Example:
Instead of:
*   *rāmo guruvacanaṃ śrotuṃ gataḥ.*
One can say:
*   *rāmo guruvacanasya śravaṇāya gataḥ.*
OR:
*   *rāmo guruvacanaśravaṇāya gataḥ.*
= "Rāma went in order to hear the speech of the teacher."

![](/images/lekt2404.jpg)
Fig.: *bālā guruśravaṇāya gatāḥ* (Children gone to hear the teacher).

2. The Dative (*caturthī*) can also designate the object of an implied infinitive of purpose:
Example:
*   *phalebhya grāmaṃ gacchati* = "He goes to the village for the purpose of fruits = He goes to the village to get fruits."

Similarly, it expresses the purpose of a thing or an action:
Example:
*   *yajñāyānnam* = "Food for the purpose of a sacrifice = Food for a sacrifice."

![](/images/lekt2401.jpg)
Fig.: *yajñāyānnam* (Food for sacrifice). "Annakut" celebrations.

3. The Dative (*caturthī*) also designates the person or thing for whom the object of an action is intended (indirect object; question: "to whom?"). (Note, however, verbs that take a double accusative!).
Example:
*   *rāmāya phalaṃ dadāti* = "He gives a fruit to Rāma."

![](/images/lekt2402.jpg)
Fig.: *bālā balāya rakṣābandhanaṃ dadāti* (A girl gives a rakhi to a boy).

4. The Dative is used with certain verbs and adverbs, similar to the English "to/for":
Examples:
*   **krudh**: *devo narebhya krudhyati* = "The god is angry with the men."
*   **alam** (adverb): "enough, sufficient, equal to": *alaṃ yodho yodhāya* = "one warrior is equal to [a match for] another warrior."

![](/images/lekt2403.jpg)
Fig.: *alaṃ yodho yodhāya*. Rāma and Rāvaṇa in battle.

## 24.2. Formation of the Dative (*caturthī*)

The regular endings of the dative are:
*   Singular: **-e**; for vowel-ending feminine stems: **-ai**.
*   Plural: Dative and Ablative (*pañcamī*): **-bhyas**.

### 24.2.1. Consonantal Stems

| | Dative Singular | Dative Plural |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **yajant** | *yajate* (*yaja-t-e*) | *yajadbhyas* (from *yaja-t-bhyas*) |
| **mahānt** | *mahate* | *mahadbhyas* |
| **paśumant** | *paśumate* | *paśumadbhyas* |
| **guṇavant** | *guṇavate* | *guṇavadbhyas* |

### 24.2.2. Interrogative Pronoun (*kim*)

| | Masculine / Neuter | Feminine |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Dative Singular** | *kasmai* | *kasyai* |
| **Dative Plural** | *kebhyas* | *kābhyas* |

### 24.2.3. Demonstrative Pronouns

| | | tad | etad | idam |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Masc. / Neut.** | **Dat. sg.** | *tasmai* | *etasmai* | *asmai* |
| | **Dat. pl.** | *tebhyas* | *etebhyas* | *ebhyas* |
| **Feminine** | **Dat. sg.** | *tasyai* | *etasyai* | *asyai* |
| | **Dat. pl.** | *tābhyas* | *etābhyas* | *ābhyas* |

### 24.2.4. Vocalic Stems

*   **Masc. / Neut. in -a: deva**
    - Dat. sg.: *devāya*
    - Dat. pl.: *devebhyas*
*   **Masculine in -i: kavi**
    - Dat. sg.: *kavaye*
    - Dat. pl.: *kavibhyas*
*   **Masculine in -u: paśu**
    - Dat. sg.: *paśave*
    - Dat. pl.: *paśubhyas*
*   **Feminine in -ā: devatā**
    - Dat. sg.: *devatāyai*
    - Dat. pl.: *devatābhyas*
*   **Feminine in -ī: devī**
    - Dat. sg.: *devyai*
    - Dat. pl.: *devībhyas*
*   **Feminine in -i: śruti**
    - Dat. sg.: *śrutaye* OR *śrutyai* (as *kavi* or *devī*)
    - Dat. pl.: *śrutibhyas*
*   **Feminine in -u: dhenu**
    - Dat. sg.: *dhenave* OR *dhenvai*
    - Dat. pl.: *dhenubhyas*

## 24.3. Sandhi of Final *-ai* and *-au*

*   **-ai** before an initial vowel is usually replaced by **-ā** (rarely by *-āy*).
*   **-au** before a vowel is usually replaced by **-āv** (rarely by *-ā*).

Example:
*   *tasmai + agnaye* → *tasmā agnaye* = "to this fire."

## 24.4. Vocabulary

*   **alam** (adverb): enough, sufficient, equal to; with Dative: enough for, equal to; with Instrumental: enough with, cease from. e.g., *alaṃ krodhena* = "enough with the anger = cease from anger!"
*   **kṛtam**: similarly used with Instrumental: *kṛtaṃ krodhena* = "done with the anger."
*   **alam** + **kṛ** 8U (*alaṃkaroti*): to decorate, to adorn.
    *   **alaṃkāra** m.: ornament, figure of speech (in poetics).
    ![](/images/lekt2405.jpg)
    Fig.: *alaṃkāraḥ* (ornament).
*   **hetu** m.: motive, occasion, cause, reason; *hetunā, hetos, hetave* with Genitive or as the second member of a compound = "for the sake of, because of."
*   **pratimā** f.: image, likeness, idol.
    ![](/images/lekt2406.jpg)
    Fig.: *devīpratimā*. Hampi, Karnataka.
*   **vṛt + pra** 1Ā (*pravartate*): to take place, happen, arise.
    From *vṛt*:
    *   **vṛtti** f.: conduct, activity, lifestyle.
    *   **vṛtta** n.: behavior, conduct.
*   **abhi** (preverb): towards, near, against, regarding.
*   **ni** (preverb): downwards, in, back.
*   **ā** (prep/postp): with Ablative or after Accusative: up to, since.
*   **atas** (indeclinable): from there, then, therefore.
*   **adhyakṣa** m.: overseer, supervisor, department head; eyewitness.
*   **indriya** n.: power, sense organ.
*   **ūha** m.: consideration, deliberation, reasoning.
    *   **apoha** m.: negation (*apa* + *ūha*).
    *   **ūhāpoha** m.: discussion of pros and cons.
    ![](/images/lekt2407.jpg)
    Fig.: *ūhāpohaḥ*.
*   **aupakārika** 3 f. *-ī*: useful.
*   **kupya** n.: forest product; non-precious metal.
*   **khyā** 2P (*khyāti*, PPP *khyāta*): to see, become visible; to name, explain.
    *   *khyā + ā*: to tell; **ākhyāna** n.: story, narrative.
    ![](/images/lekt2408.jpg)
    Fig.: *ākhyānam*.
    *   *khyā + sam*: to count; **saṃkhyā** f.: counting, number; one of the six philosophical systems (Sāṃkhya).
*   **grahaṇa** n.: seizing, grasping.
*   **caula** n.: tonsure ceremony (*saṃskāra*) in the 3rd year.
*   **tattva** n.: true essence, truth, reality (*tad* + *tva* = "that-ness").
*   **svasti** f.: well-being, success (*su* + *asti* = "it is well").
*   **namas** n.: bow, respect, greeting. Greeting: *namo namaḥ*.
    *   *namas + kṛ* 8 (*namaskaroti*): to bow, venerate, greet.
    ![](/images/lekt2411.jpg)
    Fig.: The poet Jayadeva bows to Viṣṇu. Manuscript of the Gītagovinda.
*   **svāgata** n.: welcome (*su-ā-gata*).
*   **tṛṇa** n.: blade of grass.
*   **punar** (adverb): again, back, but.

1. The goddess to whom one has not sacrificed is angry with the people.
2. He releases the cow into the village.
3. That's enough now = Enough with patience.
4. This is good (*hita, sukha*) for a Brahmin.
5. Respect (*namas*) be to Śiva! Respect be to Śrī Gaṇeśa!
   ![](/images/lekt2409.jpg)
   Fig.: *śrīgaṇeśāya namaḥ*.
6. Goodbye! (= Well-being (*svasti*) to you!)
7. This fruit is enough to eat.
8. A fighter is equal to (*śakta*) the [other] fighter.
9. Even Viṣṇu does not surpass (*pra-bhū* + Dat.) Śiva.
10. After I have bowed (*namaskṛ*) to the three sages... He bows to Narasiṃha (Dat.).
    ![](/images/lekt2410.jpg)
    Fig.: *naro narasiṃhāya namaskaroti*.
11. Welcome (*svāgatam*) to you. Welcome to the queen.
12. I wish you well-being (*kuśala*) = Well-being to you!
13. He does not consider him as [worth even] a blade of grass.
14. A fruit is enough for eating, and water for drinking.
15. Goodbye! (Neo-Sanskrit: *punardarśanāya*).
