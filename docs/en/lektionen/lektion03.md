# Lesson 3

## 3.1. Nominative singular = prathamā ekavacanam = प्रथमा एकवचनम् (Continued)

### 3.1.1. Nominative Singular in -s (Continued)

The following nominal stems ending in a vowel also form the nominative singular with -s:

*   Feminine in -i: e.g., śruti f. "hearing" -- Nom. sg.: śrutis = श्रुतिस्
*   Feminine in -u: e.g., dhenu f. "milch cow" -- Nom. sg.: dhenus = धेनुस्

### 3.1.2. Nominative singular without ending

The following form the nominative singular without an ending:

*   Feminine in -ā: e.g., devatā f. "deity" -- Nom. sg.: devatā = देवता
*   Feminine in -ī: e.g., devī f. "goddess" -- Nom. sg.: devī = देवी

Note: Additionally, there exist monosyllabic feminine root nouns in -ī whose nominative singular ends in -s: e.g., dhī f. "thought" -- Nom. sg.: dhīs = धीस्; likewise, lakṣmī लक्ष्मी f. "Lakṣmī" (as well as tarī = तरी f. "boat" and tantrī = तन्त्री f. "string") irregularly form the nominative singular in -s: lakṣmīs = लक्ष्मीस्.

## 3.2. Formation of Feminines from Masculines (Adjectives and Nouns)

| Masculines in -a:
*   Feminine in -ā: e.g., kṣatriya m. » kṣatriyā f. "Kṣatriya woman" = क्षत्रिया
*   Feminine in -ī: e.g., brāhmaṇa m. » brāhmaṇī f. "Brahmin woman" = ब्राह्मणी

No definitive rule can be established for the distribution of feminines in -ā or -ī, although Indian grammarians provide a number of rules: see, e.g., M. R. Kale: A higher Sanskrit grammar. -- Delhi, 1969. -- § 305 - 336.

 |
| --- |
| Masculines in -u:

*   Feminine in -ī: e.g., sādhu m. » sādhvī f. (good, holy woman) = साध्वी

-u- is replaced by the corresponding semi-vowel -v- before a vowel, hence -u-ī » -vī

## 3.3. Nominative plural = prathamā bahuvacanam = प्रथमा बहुचनम्

The ending of the nominative plural (prathamā bahuvacanam) in the masculine and feminine is -as.

*   Masculine in -a: deva- + -as » devās = देवास्
*   Feminine in -ā: devatā- + -as » devatās = देवतास्
*   Masculine in -i: kavi- » strengthened grade: kave- + -as » kavayas = कवयस्
*   Feminine in -i: śruti- » strengthened grade: śrute- + -as » śrutayas = श्रुतयस्
*   Feminine in -ī: devī- + -as » devyas = देव्यस्
*   Masculine in -u: guru- » strengthened grade: guro- + -as » guravas = गुरवस्
*   Feminine in -u: dhenu- » strengthened grade: dheno- + -as » dhenavas = धेनवस्

Note: $e$ is the strengthened grade (*guṇa*) of $i$, and $o$ is the strengthened grade of $u$; $ay$ is the representative of $e$ before vowels, $av$ is the representative of $o$ before vowels; $y$ is the semivocalic representative of $i$ or $\bar{i}$ before vowels.

## 3.4. Vowel Sandhi

Simple vowels, which differ either not at all or only in their length, "merge" into the corresponding long vowel:

*   \-a / -ā + a- / ā- » -ā-
*   \-i /-ī + i- / ī- » -ī-
*   \-u / -ū + u- / ū-  » -ū-
*   (-ṛ + ṛ-  » -ṝ-)

e.g.

devatā + annapūrṇā » devatānnapūrṇā "Annapūrṇā is a goddess" = देवतान्नपूर्णा

> (Annapūrṇā is the goddess of food and cooking; she is regarded as an incarnation of Pārvatī, the consort of Śiva.)

devī + indrāṇī » devīndrāṇī "Indrāṇī is a goddess" = देवीन्द्राणी

> (Indrāṇī is the consort of the god Indra.)

Simple vowels — except for -a / -ā — are replaced by their corresponding semi-vowel when preceding dissimilar vowels:

*   \-i / -ī before a- / ā- / u- / ū-  ... / au » -y
*   \-u / -ū before a- / ā- / i- / ī- / ṛ-  ... / au » -v
*   \-ṛ before a- / ā- / i- / ī- / u- / ū-  ... / au » -r

(!! There are exceptions to this rule, particularly regarding dual forms !!)

e.g.

devī + umā » devy umā "Umā is a / the goddess" = देव्युमा

> (Umā is a name for Pārvatī, the consort of Śiva)

\-a / -ā before a dissimilar vowel:

*   \-a / -ā + i- / ī » -e-
*   \-a / -ā + ṛ- » -ar-
*   \-a / -ā + e- / ai- » -ai-
*   \-a / -ā + o- / au- » -au-

e.g.

śūdrā + itarā » śudretarā "Itarā is a Śūdra woman" = शूद्रेतरा

## 3.5. Sentence Sandhi of -ās

-ās is replaced by -ā before all voiced sounds.

e.g.

dvijās + vaiśyāḥ » dvijā vaiśyāh "Vaiśyas are the twice-born" = द्विजा वैश्याः

## 3.6. Vocabulary

Learn the following words:

śruti f. = श्रुति: hearing, eternal tradition (designation for the Vedās and Brāhmaṇas)

smṛti f. = स्मृति: recollection, memory, meditative recollection = mindfulness, tradition (antonym to śruti). Comprises:

*   the six vedāṅgas n. = वेदाङ्ग
    1.  śikṣā f. = शिक्षा: correct pronunciation
    2.  chandas n. = छन्दस्: meter
    3.  vyākaraṇa n. = व्याकरण: grammar
    4.  nirukta n. = निरुक्त: etymology, word explanation
    5.  jyotiṣa n. = ज्योतिष: astronomy, calendrical science
    6.  kalpa m. = कल्प: ritual/ceremonial
*   śrautasūtra n. = श्रौतसूत्र: treatises for the performance of the great sacrifices
*   gṛhyasūtra n. = गृह्यसूत्र: treatises for the ceremonies and sacrifices of daily life
*   dharmasūtra n. = धर्मसूत्र and dharmaśāstra n. = धर्मशास्त्र: treatises on law and custom (right conduct)
*   the great epics mahābhārata n. = महाभारत and rāmāyaṇa n. = रामायण
*   the purāṇas n. = पुराण
*   nītiśāstra n. = नीतिशास्त्र: treatises on worldly wisdom

smṛti is also a particular designation for treatises on dharma

dhenu f. = धेनु: (milch) cow

paśu m. = पशु: domesticated livestock, cattle (collective)

devatā f. = देवता: deity (abstract and concrete)

brāhmaṇī f. = ब्राह्मणी: female Brāhmaṇa

kṣatriyā f. = क्षत्रिया: female Kṣatriya

kṣatriyī f. = क्षत्रियी: wife of a Kṣatriya

vaiṣyā f. = वैश्या: female Vaiśya

śūdrā f. = शूद्रा: female Śūdra

śūdrī f. / śūdrāṇī f. = शूद्री शूद्राणी: wife of a Śūdra

devī f. = देवी: goddess, especially Durgā f. = दुर्गा, the consort of Śiva = शिव

![](/images/lekt0303.jpg)  
Fig.: Durgā = दुर्गा, Orissa (ଓଡ଼ିଶା)

sādhvī f. = साध्वी: feminine of sādhu

gurvī f. = गुर्वी: feminine of guru

asmitā f. = अस्मिता: "I-am-ness", i.e., the (false) belief: "I am the one who sees," etc.

ānvīkṣikī f. = आन्वीक्षिकी: philosophy (the science that arrives at its conclusions through logically correct reasoning)

upekṣā f. = उपेक्षा: disregard, equanimity

karuṇā f. = करुणा: compassion, empathy

muditā f. = मुदिता: joy, especially sympathetic joy (the opposite of envy)

## 3.7. Exercise

A) Convert the following sentences into the plural:

1.  dvijo brāhmaṇaḥ = द्विजो ब्राह्मणः
2.  dvijaḥ kṣatriyaḥ = द्विजः क्षत्रियः
3.  dvijo vaiśyaḥ = द्विजो वैश्यः
4.  gurur brāhmaṇaḥ = गुरुर्ब्राह्मणः
5.  sādhur guruḥ = साधुर्गुरुः
6.  guruḥ kaviḥ = गुरुः कविः
7.  sādhvī brāhmaṇī = साध्वी ब्राह्मणी
8.  devatā guruḥ = देवता गुरुः
9.  paśur dhenuḥ = पशुर्धेनुः
10.  gurvī sādhvī = गुर्वी साध्वी

B) Form nominal phrases by insertion:

1.  śrutis ... (veda) = श्रुतिस् ... वेद
2.  paśus ... (dhenu) = पशुस् ... धेनु
3.  devī ... (durgā, umā, indrāṇī) = देवी ... दुर्गा, उमा, इन्द्राणी
4.  devatā ... (mīnākṣī, annapūrṇā) = देवता ... मीनाक्षी, अन्नपूर्णा
5.  śūdrā ... (itarā) = शूद्रा ... इतरा

C) Convert into the feminine:

1.  gurur brāhmaṇaḥ = गुरुर्ब्राह्मणः
2.  sādhur guruḥ = साधुर्गुरुः
3.  kṣatriyaḥ sādhuḥ = क्षत्रियः साधुः

D) Translate into Sanskrit:

1.  Umā is a goddess.
2.  The Veda is śruti.
3.  The female teachers are goddesses.
4.  Milk cows are domesticated animals.
5.  Poets are teachers.
6.  Kṣatriyas are twice-born.
7.  The holy men are Śūdras.
