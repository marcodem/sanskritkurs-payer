# Lesson 27

## 27.1. Use of the Locative (*saptamī* f. = "seventh case-ending")

The **Locative** (*saptamī*) answers the questions:
*   Where?
*   When?
*   Under what circumstances?
*   With regard to what?

1. For the question "Where?", the Locative designates the space in (at, on, among) which the agent (*kartṛ*) or the object of an action is located, or where the action itself takes place.
> *nagare* = "in the city"

![](/images/lekt2701.jpg)
Fig.: Jaipur city, 1875.

2. For the question "When?", the Locative designates the point in time or the external circumstances:
> *tasmin kāle* = "at that time, during that time"

This usage includes the so-called **Locativus Absolutus**:
If the action or state of one person or thing determines the time at which—or the circumstances under which—ANOTHER person or thing performs an action or is in a certain state, then the word designating the first person or thing is put into the Locative along with its modifier (which is often a participle).

> *evaṃ gacchati kāle* = "while time passes thus..."
> *tasmin gate* = "after he had gone..."

Sometimes the agent is not explicitly named:
> *evaṃ sati* = "it being so," "since this is the case."

The Locativus Absolutus must be translated according to the context as "while" (e.g., with a present participle), "after," "when" (e.g., with a PPP), "since," "because," "although," "if," etc.

**Note**: In the *Absolutive* (*ktvā/lyap*), the agent of the absolutive and the main verb must be identical. In the *Locativus Absolutus*, the agent of the locative construction and the agent of the main verb must be different.

![](/images/lekt2702.jpg)
Fig.: *evaṃ gacchati kāle uṣṭraḥ svapiti* (In this way, while time passes, the camel sleeps).

3. The Locative is also used for the question "With regard to what?":
> *asti tasya putre snehaḥ* = "He has love (*sneha* m.) for his son."

![](/images/lekt2703.jpg)
Fig.: *asti tasya putre snehaḥ*.

4. In traditional lexicons and commentaries, the Locative is used to indicate the meaning of a word:
> *bhūū sattāyām* = "the root *bhū* in the sense of 'being-ness' (*sat-tā*)." (Dhātupāṭha 1.1)

## 27.4. A Verse with all Singular Declension Forms of *Rāma*

![](/images/lekt2708.jpg)
*rāmo rājamaṇiḥ sadā vijayate rāmaṃ rameśaṃ bhaje |*
*rāmeṇābhihatā niśācaracamū rāmāya tasmai namaḥ |*
*rāmānnāsti parāyaṇaṃ parataraṃ rāmasya dāso 'smyaham |*
*rāme cittalayaḥ sadā bhavatu me bho rāma māmuddhara ||*

**Translation:**
1. **Nom**: Rāma, the jewel (*maṇi* m.) among kings (*rājan* m.), always (*sadā*) triumphs.
2. **Acc**: I love (*bhaj* 1U) Rāma, the lord and husband (*īśa* m.) of Ramā (= Lakṣmī).
3. **Inst**: Rāma has killed the army (*camū* f.) of the night-wanderers (= demons).
4. **Dat**: To this Rāma be veneration!
5. **Abl**: There is no refuge (*parāyaṇa* n.) better (*paratara*) than Rāma.
6. **Gen**: I (*aham*) am (*asmi*) a servant of Rāma.
7. **Loc**: In Rāma be always the tranquility (*laya* m.) of my (*me*) heart.
8. **Voc**: O Rāma, deliver (*uddhara*) me (*mām*)!

## 27.5. Vocabulary

*   **vas** 1P (*vasati*, Fut. *vatsyati*, Pass. *uṣyate*, PPP *uṣita*, Inf. *vastum*): to stay, dwell (with Loc. of the person one lives with).
    *   **vastu** n.: seat, place; reality, real thing, object.
    *   **vastutas** (adv.): in reality, really.
*   **vas** 2Ā (*vaste*, Fut. *vasiṣyate*, PPP *vasit*, Inf. *vasitum*): to put on (clothes), wear.
    *   **vastra** n.: garment, clothing, fabric.
    ![](/images/lekt2706.jpg)
    Fig.: *vastrāṇi* (clothes).
*   **vas** 6 (*ucchati*!): to shine, light up.
    *   **vasu** n.: wealth, treasure, property.
    *   **vasanta** m.: "shining" = Spring (March–May).
    ![](/images/lekt2707.jpg)
    Fig.: *vasantaḥ* (Spring).
*   **hṛ** 1U (*harati*, Fut. *hariṣyati*, Pass. *hriyate*, PPP *hṛta*, Inf. *hartum*): to hold, carry; to take away, rob.
    *   **hara** 3: taking away; m.: "The Destroyer" = epithet of Śiva.
    *   **hari** 3: blond, yellow-green; m.: epithet of Viṣṇu.
    *   **harihara** m.: Viṣṇu and Śiva united as a single deity.
    ![](/images/lekt2704.jpg)
    Fig.: *hariharaḥ*. (Left: Viṣṇu, Right: Śiva).
