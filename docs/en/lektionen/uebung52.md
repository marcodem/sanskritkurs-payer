# Exercise 52

1. Manusmṛti 4, 159–161

यद्यत्परवशं कर्म  
ततद्यत्नेन वर्जयेत् ।  
यद्यदात्मवशं तु स्यात्  
ततत्सेवेत यत्नतः ॥१५९॥

> Every act that depends on another's will, one should avoid with effort; but whatever depends on one's own will, that one should pursue with effort.

सर्वं परवशं दुःखं  
सर्वमात्मवशं सुखम् ।  
एतद्विद्यात्समासेन  
लक्षणं सुखदुःखयोः ॥१६०॥

> All that depends on another's will is suffering; all that depends on one's own will is happiness. This should be known in brief as the characteristic of happiness and suffering.

यत्कर्म कुर्वतो ऽस्य स्यात्  
परितोषो ऽन्तरात्मनः ।  
तत्प्रयत्नेन कुर्वीत  
विपरीतं तु वर्जयेत् ॥१६१॥

> Whatever act, when performed, results in the satisfaction of the inner self, that one should do with effort; but the opposite one should avoid.

2. Manusmṛti 2, 6: On the sources of Dharma

वेदो ऽखिलो धर्ममूलम्  
स्मृतिशीले च तद्विदाम् ।  
आचआरश्चैव साधूनाम्  
आत्मनस्तुष्टिरेव च ॥६॥

> The root of Dharma is:
> 
> *   the whole Veda
> *   the tradition and the conduct of those who know it
> *   the behavior of the good
> *   and the satisfaction of one's soul.

3. Kauṭilīya Arthaśāstra 1, 7, 2–7: On Artha, Kāma, and Dharma in the life of a prince

एवं वश्येन्द्रियः परस्त्रीद्रव्यहिंसाश्च वर्जयेत्, स्वप्नं लौल्यमनृतमुद्धतवेषत्वमनर्थ्यसंयोगमधर्मसंयुक्तमनर्थसंयुक्तं च व्यवहारम् ।२। धर्मार्थाविरोधेन कामं सेवेत, न निःसुखः स्यात् ।३। समं वा त्रिवर्गमन्योन्यानुबद्धम् ।४। एको ह्यत्यासेवितो धर्मार्थकामानामात्मानमितरौ च पीदयति ।५। अर्थ एव प्रधान इति कौटिल्यः ।६। अर्थमूलौ हि धर्मकामाविति ।७।

> Thus, having his senses under control, he should avoid other men's wives, other's property, and violence; and avoid sleep, fickleness, lies, vanity, useless companionship, and unjust or useless business. He should enjoy pleasure (kāma) without it being in conflict with Dharma and purposeful conduct (artha); he should not be joyless. Or he should pursue all three goals of life (trivarga) equally, as they are bound together. For if any one of the three—Dharma, Artha, or Kāma—is pursued excessively, it oppresses the soul and the other two. Kauṭilya says that Artha (purposeful conduct/wealth) is the most important. For Dharma and Kāma are rooted in Artha.

4. Aśvaghoṣa (2nd cent. AD): Buddhacarita 14: Buddha's liberating insight

ततो मारबलं जित्वा  
धैर्येण च शमेन च ।  
परमार्थं विजिज्ञासुः  
स दद्ध्यौ ध्यानकोविदः ॥१॥

> Then, after conquering the army of Māra with firmness and tranquility, he, skilled in meditation, wishing to fully know the highest truth and reality, meditated.

सर्वेषु ध्यानविधिषु  
प्राप्य चैश्वर्यमुत्तमम् ।  
सस्मार प्रथमे याम  
पूर्वजन्मपरंपराम् ॥२॥

> Having attained the highest mastery in all methods of meditation, he remembered in the first watch of the night the continuous succession of his previous births.

अमुत्राहमयं नाम  
च्युतस्तस्मादिहागतः ।  
इति जन्मसहस्राणि  
सस्मारानुभवन्निव ॥३॥

> "There I was so-and-so by name, having departed from there I have come here"—thus he remembered thousands of births as if he were experiencing them.

स्मृत्वा जन्म च मृत्युं च  
तासु तासूपपत्तिषु ।  
ततः सत्त्वेषु कारुण्यम्  
चकार करुणात्मकः ॥४॥

> Having thus remembered birth and death in those various existences, he, whose nature is compassion, felt compassion for all beings.

कृत्वेह स्वजनोत्सर्गम्  
पुनरन्यत्र च कृत्वा ।  
अत्राणः खलु लोको ऽयम्  
परिभ्रमति चक्रवत् ॥५॥

> Truly, this world is without protection and wanders around like a wheel: it releases its creatures here and then again there.

इत्येवं स्मरतस्तस्य  
बभूव नियतात्मनः ।  
कदलीगर्भनिःसारः  
संसार इति निश्चयः ॥६॥

> While he, of resolute heart, was thus remembering, the conviction arose: "The course of rebirths (samsara) is without core or essence, like the interior of a banana plant."

द्वितीye त्वागते यामे  
सो ऽद्वितीयपराक्रमः ।  
दिव्यं लेभे परं चक्षुः  
सर्वचक्षुष्मतां वरः ॥७॥

> But when the second watch had come, he, whose power is like none other, obtained the highest divine eye, he, the best of all who have eyes.

ततस्तेन स दिव्येन  
परिशुद्धेन चक्षुषा ।  
ददर्श निखिलं लोकम्  
आदर्श इव निर्मले ॥८॥

> Then, with that completely pure divine eye, he saw the entire world as if in a flawless mirror.

सत्त्वानां पश्यतस्तस्य  
निकृष्टोत्कृष्टकर्मणाम् ।  
प्रच्युतिं चोपपत्तिं च  
ववृधे करुणात्मता ॥९॥

> As he saw the passing away and arising of beings who had bad or good karma, compassion grew in his heart.

इमे दुष्कृतकर्माणः  
प्राणिनो यान्ति दुर्गतिम् ।  
इमे ऽन्ये शुभकर्माणः  
प्रतिष्ठन्ते त्रिविष्टपे ॥१०॥

> "These living beings, who have committed evil deeds, go to a bad destination; these others, who have good karma, are established in the heaven [of Indra]."
