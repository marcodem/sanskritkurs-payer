# Lesson 37

## 37.1. The Adverb (*kriyāviśeṣaṇam*)

In Sanskrit, adverbs are formed:
1.  Through case forms of nouns and pronouns.
2.  With the help of special suffixes and endings.

In the first case, the line between an active case usage and an adverb is fluid. Some adverbs are fossilized ancient case forms where the corresponding noun is no longer used.

## 37.2. Adverbial Use of Case Forms

### 37.2.1. Accusative (*dvitīyā*)
The accusative can express manner, duration, or direction.
*   **From Nouns**: *kāmam* (at wish), *artham* (for the sake of).
*   **From Adjectives (Neuter)**: *sukham* (easily, happily), *nityam* (always), *sādhu* (properly, well).
*   **From Pronouns (Neuter)**: *tad* (then, therefore), *yad* (if, that).

### 37.2.2. Instrumental (*tṛtīyā*)
*   **From Nouns**: *arthena* (for the sake of), *kṣaṇena* (in a moment, immediately).
*   **From Adjectives**: *dūreṇa* (from afar), *uccais* (loudly, highly).

### 37.2.3. Dativ (*caturthī*)
Rarely used, usually for purpose: *arthāya* (for the sake of).

### 37.2.4. Ablative (*pañcamī*)
Expresses reason ("why?") or origin ("from where?").
*   **From Nouns**: *balāt* (forcibly).
*   **From Adjectives**: *dūrāt* (from afar).
*   **From Pronouns**: *kasmāt* (why?), *akasmāt* (unexpectedly).

### 37.2.5. Genitive (*ṣaṣṭhī*)
Rarely used for "after how much time?": *cirasya* (after a long time).

### 37.2.6. Locative (*saptamī*)
Expresses circumstance or location: *dūre* (afar), *-arthe* (for the sake of), *-kṛte* (for the sake of).

## 37.3. Archaic Case Forms as Adverbs

*   **jātu**: at all, ever (originally "by birth").
*   **tūṣṇīm**: silently.
*   **evam**: thus, so.

## 37.4. Pronominal Adverbs formed with Suffixes

The suffixes **-tas** (ablative), **-tra** (locative), **-thā/-tham** (manner), and **-dā** (time) are added to pronominal stems.

| Suffix | Stems: tad | a- (idam) | yad | kim (ku) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **-tas** (Abl.) | *tatas* (thence) | *itas / atas* (hence) | *yatas* (whence) | *kutas* (why? whence?) |
| **-tra** (Loc.) | *tatra* (there) | *atra* (here) | *yatra* (where) | *kutra / kva* (where?) |
| **-thā/-tham** | *tathā* (thus) | | *yathā* (as) | *katham* (how?) |
| **-dā** (Time) | *tadā* (then) | | *yadā* (when) | *kadā* (when?) |

**Other Suffixes:**
*   **-vat**: Added to any noun to mean "like, in the manner of" (e.g., *putravat* - like a son).
*   **-dhā**: Expresses "-fold" (e.g., *dvidhā* - twofold) or "in such a manner".

## 37.5. Adverbial Compounds

### 37.5.1. Compounds with Adverbial members
Example: *suciram* (very long time).

### 37.5.2. Adverbial Bahuvrīhis
Example: *rakṣārtham* (for the sake of protection).

### 37.5.3. *Avyayībhāva* Compounds
An *Avyayībhāva* is a compound where the first member is an indeclinable (preposition, particle) and the second is a noun that takes the Accusative Neuter Singular ending.
*   **pratyagni**: towards the fire (*agniṃ prati*).
*   **yathāśakti**: according to one's power (*śaktim anatītya*).
*   **abhimukham**: towards the face (*mukham abhi*).
*   **yathākāmam**: as desired (*yathā kāmaḥ*).

## 37.6. Syntax

With verbs like **as, bhū, kṛ**, a predicative adverb can be used instead of an adjective.
Example: *tūṣṇīṃ babhūva* (he became silent).

## 37.7. Indefinite Pronouns

Indefinite pronouns are formed by adding **-cid, -cana,** or **api** to the case forms of the interrogative *kim*.
*   *kaścid = kaścana = ko 'pi*: someone, anyone.
*   *kasyacid*: of someone.

## 37.8. Interrogative Sentences

Interrogative sentences can be:
1.  **Direct (Word) Questions**: Ask for specific information (*who, what, where*).
    - *ko grāmaṃ gacchati?* (Who goes to the village?)
    - *kva rāmo vasati?* (Where does Rāma live?)
    - *kiṃ bālā adhīyīran?* (What should children study?)
    ![](/images/lekt3704.jpg)
2.  **Sentence (Yes/No) Questions**:
    - Identified by context, verb-first order, or particles like **api**, **kim**, **kaccid**.
    - *api satyam?* (Is it true?)
    - *kiṃ śaknoti?* (Can he?)
    - *kaccit kṣatriyeṇa śatravo jitāḥ?* (Has the warrior defeated the enemies?)
