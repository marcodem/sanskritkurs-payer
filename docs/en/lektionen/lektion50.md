# Lesson 50

## 50.1. Nominal Formation: Regular Comparison

Regular comparison in Sanskrit is formed using the *taddhita* suffixes:
*   **-tara** (feminine: **-tarā**) for the **Comparative**.
*   **-tama** (feminine: **-tamā**) for the **Superlative**.

These suffixes are attached to the **masculine stem**. For nouns with stem gradation, they are attached to the **weak stem** (specifically the form used before the Locative plural ending *-su*).

![](/images/lekt5007.jpg)
Fig.: *lokasyottamo giriḥ* (The highest mountain in the world)

### Meaning and Usage:
1.  **Comparative (-tara)**:
    *   Without a compared object: Means "rather," "very," or "too." (e.g., *śucitara* 3 = "rather pure," "too pure").
    *   With a compared object: The object compared stands in the **Ablative** (*pañcamī*), meaning "than."
    *   Example: *devadattād rāmo dhīmattaraḥ* = "Rāma is more intelligent than Devadatta."

2.  **Superlative (-tama)**:
    *   Without a compared object: Means "extremely" or "very." (e.g., *sattama* 3 = "extremely good").
    *   With a compared object: The object compared stands in the **Genitive** (*ṣaṣṭhī*), meaning "among" or "of."
    *   Example: *dvijānāṃ sattamaḥ* = "The best among the twice-born."

These suffixes can also be attached to nouns, indeclinables, and even verbal forms:
*   **gajatama** m.: "the best elephant" (a title of the Buddha).
*   **gotama** m.: "the greatest ox" or "one richest in cattle."
*   **ut** (up) → **uttara** 3 (higher) → **uttama** 3 (highest).

When attached to a **verbal form**, the suffixes take the adverbial forms **-tarām** and **-tamām**:
*   *pacatitarām* = "he/she cooks better."
*   *pacatitamām* = "he/she cooks best."

## 50.2. Vocabulary

*   **dhruva** 3: firm, eternal, unchanging.
*   **niṣeka** m.: sprinkling, fertilization, ceremony of conception.
*   **paṇḍita** 3: wise, learned, scholarly.
*   **man + ava** 4Ā (*avamanyate*, PPP *avamat*): to despise, disregard.
*   **mantrin** m.: advisor, minister.
    ![](/images/lekt5001.jpg)
*   **rahas** n.: secret, solitude.
*   **riṣ** 1P/4P (*riṣati / riṣyati*, PPP *riṣṭa*): to be harmed, fail.
*   **lup** 6U (*lumpati*, PPP *lupta*): to break, destroy.
*   **vidhi** m.: rule, fate, destiny. (from *vi-dhā*).
*   **vṛṣ** 1P (*varṣati*, PPP *vṛṣṭa*): to rain.
    ![](/images/lekt5002.jpg)
*   **samyak** (adv.): rightly, truly, properly; completely.
*   **āditya** m.: sun; (pl.) a class of Vedic deities.
    ![](/images/lekt5005.jpg)
*   **sarva** 3: every, all. (Declines like *yad*, except Nom./Acc. sg. neut. *sarvam*).
*   **vai**: (particle) truly, indeed, but.
*   **iha**: here, in this world; now.
*   **kalpa** m.: ritual, custom, sacred rule; aeon (*kalpa*).
*   **kalyāṇa** 3: beautiful, auspicious.
    ![](/images/lekt5003.jpg)
*   **ku-**: (prefix) bad, evil.
    ![](/images/lekt5004.jpg)
*   **cakṣ** 2Ā (*caṣṭe*, 2.pl.Ā *caḍḍhve*): to see. (Only used in present).
    *   **cakṣ + pra** 2Ā (*pracaṣṭe*): to tell, relate; to call.
*   **deśa** m.: place, country, region.

## 50.3. Exercise

A) For review, the following verse contain all singular forms of **guru** m.:
*gurur eva gatir guruṃ eva bhaje guruṇaiva sahāsmi namo gurave |*
*na guroḥ paramaṃ śiśur asmi guror matir asti gurau mama pāhi guro ||*
![](/images/lekt5008.jpg)

B) Translate:
1.  *yena asya pitaro yātā yena yātāḥ pitāmahāḥ | tena yāyāt satāṃ mārgam tena gacchan na riṣyate || 1 ||* (Manusmṛti 4, 178)
2.  *kuvivāhaiḥ kriyālopair vedān adhyayanena ca | kulāny akulatāṃ yānti brāhmaṇātikrameṇa ca || 2 ||* (Manusmṛti 3, 63)
3.  *saṃtuṣṭo bhāryayā bhartā bhartrā bhāryā tathaiva ca | yasminn eva kule nityam kalyāṇaṃ tatra vai dhruvam || 3 ||* (Manusmṛti 3, 60)
4.  *svādhyāye nityayuktaḥ syād daive caiveha karmaṇi | daive karmaṇi yukto hi bibhartīdaṃ carācaram || 4 ||*
5.  *agnau prāstāhutiḥ samyag ādityam upatiṣṭhate | ādityāj jāyate vṛṣṭir vṛṣṭer annaṃ tataḥ prajāḥ || 5 ||* (Manusmṛti 3, 75-76)
6.  *heyaṃ duḥkham anāgatam || 6 ||*
7.  *draṣṭṛdṛśyayoḥ saṃyogo heyahetuḥ || 7 ||* (Yogasūtra 2, 16-17)
8.  *na kiṃcid avamanyeta sarvasya śṛṇuyān matam | bālasyāpy arthavad vākyaṃ upayuñjīta paṇḍitaḥ || 8 ||* (Arthaśāstra 1, 15)
9.  *upanīya tu yaḥ śiṣyaṃ vedam adhyāpayed dvijaḥ | sakalpaṃ sarahasyaṃ ca tam ācāryāṃ pracaṣṭate || 9 ||*
10. *ekadeśaṃ tu vedasya vedāṅgāny api vā punaḥ | yo 'dhyāpayati vṛttyarthaṃ upādhyāyaḥ sa ucyate || 10 ||*
11. *niṣekādīni karmāṇi yaḥ karoti yathāvidhi | saṃbhāvayati cānnenas a vipro gurur ucyate || 11 ||* (Manusmṛti 2, 140-142)
