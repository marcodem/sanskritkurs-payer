# Lesson 10

## 10.1. The Passive Sentence

| Schema: (Agent - *kartṛ* - in the Instrumental (*tṛtīyā*)) - (Direct Object - *karman* - in the Nominative) - Passive Verbal Form |
| :--- |

e.g.,
*   *brāhmaṇena deva ijyate* = ब्राह्मणेन देव इज्यते = "A Brahmin venerates a god with a sacrifice" (literally: "A god is venerated by a Brahmin with a sacrifice.")

This sentence is completely (!) identical in meaning to:
*   *brāhmaṇo devaṃ yajati / yajate* = ब्राह्मणो देवं यजति / यजते

| In a passive sentence, the agent (*kartṛ*) stands in the **Instrumental** (*tṛtīyā* f. "third case-ending"), and the direct object (*karman* n.) stands in the **Nominative** (*prathamā* f.). |
| :--- |

Passive sentences in which the agent is not designated often have an impersonal meaning ("one"):
e.g.,
*   *ijyate* = इज्यते = "One sacrifices" (literally: "it is venerated with a sacrifice").

| Even if the corresponding active sentence would contain an accusative (*dvitīyā*) of the goal, this can be put into the nominative (*prathamā*) in the passive sentence: |
| :--- |

The active sentence *rāmo grāmaṃ gacchati* = रामो ग्रामं गच्छति = "Rāma goes to the village" corresponds to the passive sentences:
*   *rāmeṇa grāmaṃ gamyate* = रामेण ग्रामं गम्यते OR alternatively *rāmeṇa grāmo gamyate* = रामेण ग्रामो गम्यते

| Intransitive verbs (verbs without a direct object) frequently have passive constructions, especially in polite requests: |
| :--- |

e.g.,
*   *praviśyatām* = प्रविश्यताम् = "May it be entered = Please enter / Come in!"
*   *niṣadyatām* = निषद्यताम् = "May it be sat down = Please sit down / Please take a seat."

Passive constructions are extremely common in Sanskrit—especially in spoken Sanskrit. In fact, the passive is often easier to form than the various present stems.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Sanskrit passive sentences should generally NOT be rendered as passive sentences in English or German, as the passive in European languages often has a very different stylistic function.

## 10.2. The Instrumental = *tṛtīyā* f. = तृतीया = "third case-ending"

The regular endings of the Instrumental (*tṛtīyā*):
*   Singular: **-ā**
*   Plural: **-bhis**

Regular formations:

| | Instrumental singular | Instrumental plural |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Feminine in -i: śruti** | *śruty-ā* श्रुत्या | *śruti-bhis* श्रुतिभिस् |
| **Feminine in -ī: devī** | *devy-ā* देव्या | *devī-bhis* देवीभिस् |
| **Feminine in -u: dhenu** | *dhenv-ā* धेन्वा | *dhenu-bhis* धेनुभिस् |

Masculine stems in *-i* and *-u* and feminine stems in *-ā* form the instrumental singular irregularly:

| | Instrumental singular | Instrumental plural |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Masculine in -i: kavi** | *kavi-n-ā* कविना | *kavi-bhis* कविभिस् |
| **Masculine in -u: paśu** | *paśu-n-ā* पशुना | *paśu-bhis* पशुभिस् |
| **Feminine in -ā: devatā** | *devat-ay-ā* देवतया | *devatā-bhis* देवताभिस् |

Masculine and neuter stems in *-a* form the instrumental singular and plural irregularly:

| | Instrumental singular | Instrumental plural |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Masculine in -a: deva** | *devena* देवेन | *devais* देवैस् |
| **Neuter in -a: phala** | *phalena* फलेन | *phalais* फलैस् |

Interrogative pronoun *kim*:
*   Masculine / Neuter: *kena* केन (sg.), *kais* कैस् (pl.)
*   Feminine: *kayā* कया (sg.), *kābhis* काभिस् (pl.)

Demonstrative pronouns:

| | | Instrumental singular | Instrumental plural |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **tad** ("he, she, it; the mentioned") | M / N | *tena* तेन | *tais* तैस् |
| | F | *tayā* तया | *tābhis* ताभिस् |
| **etad** ("this" - very close) | M / N | *etena / enena* एतेन / एनेन | *etais* एतैस् |
| | F | *etayā / enayā* एतया / एनाय | *etābhis* एताभिस् |
| **idam** ("this" - close) | M / N | *anena / enena* अनेन / एनेन | *ebhis* एभिस् |
| | F | *anayā / enayā* अनया / एनाय | *ābhis* आभिस् |

Regarding the use of *enena* etc., what was said in [Lesson 7](lektion07.md) applies.
Except for the Nominative and Accusative, the forms of the neuter stems in *-a*, the interrogative pronoun, and the demonstrative pronouns are identical to the forms of the corresponding masculine.

### 10.2.1. On the Use of the Instrumental (*tṛtīyā* = तृतीया)

The Instrumental (*tṛtīyā*) primarily answers the questions:
*   Whereby? / By what?
*   With what?
*   With whom?

It is used:
1.  In passive sentences: the agent (*kartṛ*) stands in the Instrumental.
2.  To indicate the means or instrument with which something is accomplished.
3.  To indicate the way along which someone reaches a place.
4.  To express connection, union, or accompaniment.
5.  With postpositions meaning "together with" or "with"; e.g., *saha* = सह.
    e.g., *brāhmaṇena saha* = ब्राह्मणेन सह = "together with the Brahmin."

## 10.3. The Law of Cerebralization for *-n-* (Word Sandhi)

An *-n-* followed by a vowel or *n, m, y, v* is replaced by **-ṇ-** if *ṛ, ṝ, r, ṣ* precede the *-n-* within the word, provided no sound other than vowels, gutturals (*k, kh, g, gh, ṅ*), labials (*p, ph, b, bh, m*), *y, v, h*, or *anusvāra* stands between these sounds and the following *-n-*.

Therefore:
*   *guru* → *guruṇā* गुरुणा (Inst. sg.)
*   *śūdra* → *śūdreṇa* शूद्रेण
*   *īśvara* → *īśvareṇa* ईश्वरेण
*   *kṣatriya* → *kṣatriyeṇa* क्षत्रियेण
*   *naraka* → *narakeṇa* नरकेण

This also explains the **ṇ** in:
*   *śravaṇa* श्रवण
*   *śṛṇoti* शृणोति
*   *kāraṇa* कारण
*   *brāhmaṇa* ब्राह्मण

## 10.4. The Passive, Indicative Present (*yak* = यक्)

Formation:
**Root (usually zero grade) + -ya- + Ātmanepada ending**

e.g.,

| Root | 3rd sg. Pass. Pres. Ind. | 3rd pl. Pass. Pres. Ind. |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **nī** 1 U (*nayati*) | *nī-ya-te* नीयते "he/she/it is led" | *nī-ya-nte* नीयन्ते |
| **man** 4 Ā (*manyate*) | *man-ya-te* मन्यते "he/she/it is thought" | *man-ya-nte* मन्यन्ते |
| **viś** 6 P (*viśati*) | *viś-ya-te* विश्यते "it is entered / betrodden" | *viś-ya-nte* विश्यन्ते |

Note that for verbs of the 4th present class, the *Ātmanepada* and the Passive have identical forms! Whether *Ātmanepada* or Passive is intended can only be inferred from the sentence construction.

### 10.4.1. Special Rules for Passive Formation

1.  Roots ending in *-i* or *-u* lengthen their vowel before the passive suffix *-ya-*:
    *   *ji* 1 P → *jīyate, jīyante* जीयते जीयन्ते
    *   *śru* 5 P → *śrūyate, śrūyante* श्रूयते श्रूयन्ते
    *   *su* 5 U → *sūyate, sūyante* सूयते सूयन्ते
2.  Roots with *-a-* between consonants that are neither nasals nor *y, r, v* remain unchanged in the full grade:
    *   *labh* 1 Ā → *labhyate, labhyante* लभ्यते लभ्यन्ते
3.  The following roots also form their passive in the full grade or the gradation stage of the present stem:
    *   *āp* 5 P → *āpyate, āpyante* आप्यते आप्यन्ते
    *   *khād* 1 P → *khādyate, khādyante* खाद्यते खाद्यन्ते
    *   *smṛ* 1 P → *smaryate, smaryante* स्मर्यते स्मर्यन्ते
4.  Note the zero grade of verbs beginning with *ya, va, ra*, or those where these sounds stand after another consonant (**Samprasāraṇa**):

| Root | Zero Grade | Passive |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **yaj** 1 U यज् | \*yj → *ij* | *ijyate, ijyante* इज्यते, इज्यन्ते |
| **vad** 1 P वद् | \*vd → *ud* | *udyate, udyante* उद्यते, उद्यन्ते |
| **prach** 6 P प्रछ् | \*prcch → *pṛcch* | *pṛcchyate, pṛcchyante* पृच्छ्यते, पृच्छ्यन्ते |

5.  Final *-ṛ* after a single consonant is replaced by **-ri** before the passive suffix *-ya-*:
    *   *kṛ* 8 U → *kriyate, kriyante* क्रियते क्रियन्ते
    *   (But: *smṛ* 1 P → *smaryate* स्मर्यते)
6.  Some roots in *-an* have two alternative passive stem formations:
    - one in *-an-ya*
    - one in *-ā-ya-* (long *ā* as a substitute for a hypothetical long nasal)
    *   *tan* 8 U → *tanyate* OR *tāyate* तन्याते, तायते

## 10.5. Vocabulary 1

Learn the following words:
*   **gṛha** n. गृह : house.
*   **grāma** m. ग्राम : village.
*   **nagara** n. नगर : city/town.
*   **yajña** m. यज्ञ : sacrifice. (In India, the sacrifice is primarily a veneration of the deity as a guest, thereby putting the deity under obligation).
*   **puṇya** n. पुण्य : good deed, merit (by which one earns well-being and good rebirths).
*   **pāpa** n. पाप : misdeed, evil (opposite of *puṇya*).
*   **satya** n. सत्य : truth. (In India, magic power was attributed to the true word; indeed, the entire cosmic order is maintained and created by it).
*   **anṛta** n. अनृत : untruth, lie (opposite of *satya*). (*an-* "un-" + *ṛta* n.; *ṛta* is a central concept in the Veda).
*   **ṛṣi** m. ऋषि : Vedic sage, composer of Vedic hymns. All Brahmins trace their descent from such *ṛṣis*.
