# Lesson 47

## 47.1. The Imperative (*loṭ*)

The Imperative (*loṭ*) is used to express:
*   A command or order (also expressed by the Gerundive).
*   A regulation or rule.
*   A demand or wish.
*   A request or invitation.
*   Deliberation or a question.
*   Permission or blessing.
*   A sense that it is time for an action.

**Examples:**
*   *ehi* = "Come!"
*   *grāmaṃ bhavān āgacchatu* = "Please come to the village!" (polite command).
*   *vedam adhyayai* = "Let me study the Veda." (wish/deliberation).
*   *bhava tātaprasādaḥ* = "May your father be pleased." (blessing).
*   *bhavatā praviśyatām* = "Please enter." (lit. "May it be entered by you"; polite passive command).

**Note on the 1st Person:**
In classical Sanskrit, the 1st person forms of the Imperative are actually old Vedic **subjunctive** (*leṭ*) forms, often expressing "Let us..." or "I shall...".

## 47.2. Formation of the Imperative for Thematic Stems

The Imperative is formed from the **present stem**.

### 47.2.1. Endings for Thematic Stems (Classes 1, 4, 6, 10, Passive)

| Person | Parasmaipada | Ātmanepada |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| | **Sing. / Plur.** | **Sing. / Plur.** |
| **1st (*uttamaḥ*)** | *-āni / -āma* | *-ai / -āmahai* |
| **2nd (*madhyamaḥ*)** | **-ø** (or *-tāt*) / *-ta* | *-sva / -dhvam* |
| **3rd (*prathamaḥ*)** | *-tu* (or *-tāt*) / *-ntu* | *-tām / -ntām* |

*Note: The ending **-tāt** for the 2nd and 3rd singular is used specifically for blessings (e.g., **bhadro bhavatāt** — "May you be happy!").*
