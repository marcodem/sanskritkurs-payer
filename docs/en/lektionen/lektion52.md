## 52.1. Neuters of Stems in *-i* and *-u*

In the neuter declension of stems ending in *-i* and *-u*, an *-n-* is inserted before case endings starting with a vowel. This is influenced by the *-in* stems.

| Case | vāri n. ("water") | madhu n. ("honey") |

| **Nom./Acc.** | *vāri* | *madhu* |

| **Dat.** | *vāriṇe* | *madhuṇe* |

## 52.2. Nominal Formation

### 52.2.1. Past Active Participle (PPP + *-vant*)
A past active participle is formed by adding the suffix **-vant** (feminine: **-vatī**) to the Past Passive Participle (PPP).
*   **kṛta** (done) + *-vant* → **kṛtavant** / **kṛtavatī** (one who has done).
*   **bhinna** (split) + *-vant* → **bhinnavant** (one who has split).
*   Declination follows the *-vant* pattern (masc.) and *devī* pattern (fem.).

### 52.2.2. Suffix *-maya / -mayī*
This suffix added to a noun forms an adjective meaning:
*   "made of"
*   "consisting of"
*   "full of" / "rich in"

**Examples:**
*   **annamaya** 3: rich in food (*annam*). ![](/images/lekt5202.jpg)
*   **cinmaya** 3: consisting of intellect (*cit*).
*   **vāṅmaya** 3: consisting of speech (*vāc*).
*   **somamaya** 3: made of Soma.

*Note: Sometimes used as a neuter noun to indicate an "abundance" (e.g., **annamayam** n. = abundance of food).*

### 52.2.3. Suffix *-eya*
Added to feminine nouns to indicate:
*   "child of"
*   "descendant of"
Requires **Vṛddhi** of the first vowel.
*   **Kuntī** → **Kaunteya** m. (son of Kunti, i.e., Arjuna or Bhima).
![](/images/lekt5203.jpg)

## 52.3. Adverbial Suffix *-śas*
Forms distributive adverbs:
*   **ekaśas**: one by one, singly. ![](/images/lekt5204.jpg)
*   **dviśas**: in pairs, by twos.
*   **śataśas**: by hundreds.
*   **bhāgaśas**: part by part.
*   **sarvaśas**: completely, everywhere.

## 52.4. Verbal Compounds of "Becoming" (*abhūtatadbhāvaḥ*)

### 52.4.1. *cvi*-Formations
Nouns can be prefixed to the roots **kṛ** (to make), **as** (to be), or **bhū** (to become) to express that something *becomes* or *is made into* what the noun designates.

**Vowel changes:**
*   *-a / -ā* → **-ī** (e.g., *kṛṣṇī-karoti*: makes black).
*   *-i / -u* → **long vowel** (e.g., *śucī-bhavati*: becomes pure; *gurū-bhavāmi*: I become a master).
*   *-ṛ* → **-rī** (e.g., *pitrī-bhavati*: becomes a father).
*   *-n* stems: the *-n* drops and the preceding vowel follows the rules above (e.g., *rājī-babhūva*: became king).
![](/images/lekt5209.jpg)

### 52.4.2. Suffix *-sāt*
Expresses that something *completely* becomes or is *entirely* transformed into something.
*   **agni-sāt-bhavati**: becomes entirely fire.
*   **bhasma-sāt-karoti**: turns entirely into ash (*bhasman*).

It can also indicate dependence or acquisition:
*   **rāja-sāt-bhavati**: becomes property of the king.

## 52.5. Word Repetition (*dviruktam*)
Expresses repetition, distribution, or intensification.
*   **punaḥ punaḥ**: again and again.
*   **pṛthak-pṛthak**: separately, individually.
*   **yuge yuge**: in every age.
*   **ekaikam**: each one.

## 52.6. Numerals (*saṃkhyā*)

### 52.6.1. Cardinal Numbers (1–10)
1. **eka** (declines like *sarva*).
2. **dvi** (Dual).
3. **tri** (Plural).
4. **catur** (Plural).
5. **pañcan**.
6. **ṣaṣ**.
7. **saptan**.
8. **aṣṭan**.
9. **navan**.
10. **daśan**.

### 52.6.2. Syntax of Numerals
*   **1–19**: Adjectives (must agree in case, number, gender).
*   **20+**: Substantives (e.g., *viṃśati* 20, *triṃśat* 30, *śata* 100, *sahasra* 1000). Usually govern the genitive or are used in apposition.

### 52.6.3. Special Compounding
*   **Dvigu**: A *Tatpuruṣa* with a numeral as the first member expressing an aggregate. Usually neuter (e.g., **tribhuvanam** n. = "The Three Worlds").
*   **Saptarṣi**: The Seven Rṣis (The Great Bear). ![](/images/lekt5206.jpg)
*   **Ṣaṇmātura**: Son of six mothers (Kartikeya). ![](/images/lekt5207.jpg)

## 52.8. Exercise
**Manusmṛti (Excerpts):**
1. *sarvaṃ paravaśaṃ duḥkhaṃ sarvam ātmavaśaṃ sukham |* (All dependence is sorrow, all independence is joy.)
2. *agnau prāstāhutiḥ samyag ādityam upatiṣṭhate | ādityāj jāyate vṛṣṭir vṛṣṭer annaṃ tataḥ prajāḥ ||* (Oblation cast in fire reaches the sun; from the sun comes rain, from rain food, and from food beings.)

**Asvaghosa's *Buddhacarita*: The Enlightenment**
*   Having conquered the army of Mara with steadfastness and peace... he remembered the succession of his former births in the first watch of the night. ![](/images/lekt5216.jpg)
*   "In that place I was such a one, having fallen from there I came here" — thus he remembered thousands of births as if experiencing them.
