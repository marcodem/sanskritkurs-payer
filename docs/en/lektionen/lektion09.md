# Lesson 9

## 9.1. Samāhāradvandva = सआहारद्वन्द्व ("Collective Dvandva")

When a Dvandva is intended to express that a series of elements, etc., constitute a single unit, it generally appears in the neuter singular:

e.g.,

āhāra-nidrā-maithuna-bhayam = आहारनिद्रामैथुनभयम् does not merely mean "food intake (āhāra m.), sleep (nidrā f.), mating (maithuna n.), and fear (bhaya n.)", but rather refers to these four as a single unit that characterizes animal life.

In addition to this fundamental rule for the formation of Samāhāradvandvas, there are a number of further rules to be found in Pāṇini 2.4.2–17 or, for example, in Mr. R. Kale, *A Higher Sanskrit Grammar*, reprint 1969, § 189–192a. In the various stages of linguistic development, the same rules were not always observed!

## 9.2. On the Formation of Nominal Stems

### 9.2.1. -ka (taddhita)

The *taddhita* suffix *-ka* forms, among other things:

1.  Diminutives (diminutive forms, analogous to the German *-chen*, *-lein*, *-li*, *-le*):  
    putra m. "son" » putraka m. = पुत्रक "little son" 
2.  Nouns denoting an approximate similarity:  
    aśva m. "horse" » aśvaka m. = अश्वक "little horse, a thing similar to a horse" 
3.  Nouns without a difference in meaning from the underlying noun:  
    putraka m. पुत्रक = putra m. पुत्र् 
4.  Nouns expressing "being in relation to the concept of the base word":  
    kāśi f. "Benares" » kāśika 3 = काशिक "born in Benares, originating from Benares"  
    rūpa n. "form/shape" » rūpaka 3 = रूपक "possessing the form of something"

### 9.2.2. -aka (kṛt), fem. often -ikā

The *kṛt* suffix *-aka*, feminine often *-ikā*, forms agent nouns (nouns expressing the doer) from every root.

A final vowel of the root, as well as an *-a-* in the penultimate position of the root, are replaced by their lengthened grade (*vṛddhi*) (there are some exceptions); the remaining vowels are replaced by their strengthened grade (*guṇa*).

Examples:

| --- | --- |

see above, e.g., śrāvaka, yājaka, jayaka

dvijātīnām = Genitive (case) plural of dvijāti (translate here as: "towards the twice-born")
