# Lesson 34

## 34.1. The Perfect (*liṭ*)

In classical Sanskrit, only the Indicative and Participle are used from the Perfect stem. There are two types of Perfect formation:
*   **Reduplicated Perfect** (*dvitvaliṭ*)
*   **Periphrastic Perfect** (*anuprayogaliṭ*)

**Periphrastic Perfect** is used for:
1.  Derived conjugations (Causative, Desiderative, Frequentative, Denominatives).
2.  Roots beginning with a long vowel (except *ā-*), or a vowel (except *a-*) followed by two consonants.
3.  Some other specific roots.

## 34.2. The Reduplicated Perfect (*dvitvaliṭ*)

### 34.2.1. Stem Gradation

**Formation:**
*   **Strong Stem** (Singular Parasmaipada): Reduplicated high/extended-grade root + ending.
*   **Weak Stem** (all other forms): Reduplicated deep-grade root + ending.

**No Stem Gradation:** Roots of the following forms generally do not have stem gradation (neither in the Perfect nor elsewhere):
*   (Consonant)-Consonant-Long Vowel-Consonant
*   (Consonant)-Consonant-Vowel-Consonant-Consonant
*   *a-*Consonant or *ā-*Consonant (in the Perfect specifically).

### 34.2.2. Perfect Endings

| | 3. Singular | 3. Plural |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Parasmaipada** | *-a* | *-ur* |
| **Ātmanepada** | *-e* | *-re* |

### 34.2.3. Connecting Vowel *-i-*

The connecting vowel **-i-** is always inserted before the 3.pl.Ā ending (*-re*) and often before other endings starting with a consonant.

### 34.2.4. Reduplication rules

For initial consonants, the rules from [Lesson 33](lektion33.md) apply. The reduplication vowel is generally the short version of the root vowel. 
*   *Ṛ, ṝ, ḷ* and final diphthongs are reduplicated with **-a-**.
*   *y-* and *v-* initial roots often reduplicate with *i-* and *u-*, which merge with the root vowel in weak stems (*saṃprasāraṇa*).
    - **vac**: *uvāca* (3.sg.P), *ūcur* (3.pl.P)
    - **yaj**: *iyāja* (3.sg.P), *ījur* (3.pl.P)

### 34.2.5. Vowel-Initial Roots

1.  **a-, ā-**: Reduplicated with *a-*, appearing as **ā-**.
    - **as** ("to be/throw"): *āsa* (3.sg.P), *āsur* (3.pl.P).
2.  **i-, u-**: Reduplicate with *iy-* / *uv-* in strong stems, and merge into long **ī- / ū-** in weak stems.
    - **i**: *iyāya* (3.sg.P), *īyur* (3.pl.P).
    - **iṣ**: *iyeṣa* (3.sg.P), *īṣur* (3.pl.P).
3.  Initial **a-** before two consonants or initial **ṛ-**: Reduplicated with **ān-**.
    - **añj**: *ānañja*, *ānañjur*.
    - **ṛdh**: *ānardha*, *ānṛdhur*.

## 34.3. Types of Reduplicated Perfect

Perfect stems are categorized by their gradation patterns (Type I to V).

## 34.4. Perfect Type I: No Stem Gradation

Used for roots that do not change stem grade (see 34.2.1).
*   **bandh** 9P: *babandha*, *babandhur*.
*   **jīv** 1P: *jijīva*, *jijīvur*.
*   **āp** 5P: *āpa*, *āpur*.
*   **as** 2P/4: *āsa*, *āsur*.

## 34.5. Perfect Type II: Strong High, Weak Deep

Used for roots of the form (Consonant)-(Consonant)-i/u/ṛ/ḷ-Consonant.
*   **bhid**: *bibheda*, *bibhidur*, *bibhide*, *bibhidire*.
*   **muc**: *mumoca*, *mumucur*, *mumuce*, *mumucire*.
*   **vṛt**: *vavṛte*, *vavṛtire*.

## 34.8. Reading Exercise

Translate the following text from the *Padma Purāṇa* regarding gifts to Brahmins:

1. *kṣitiṃ saśasyāṃ yo dadyād brāhmaṇāya dvijottama | viṣṇuloke sukhaṃ bhuṅkte yāvad indrāś caturdaśa || 1 ||*
2. *saptadvīpāṃ mahīṃ dattvā yat puṇyaṃ prāpyate dvija | tat puṇyaṃ prāpruyānmartyo dhenuṃ yacchan dvijātaye || 2 ||*
3. *tilapramāṇaṃ svarṇaṃ yo brāhmaṇāya prayacchati | hariniketanaṃ yāti yuktaṃ koṭikulair api || 3 ||*
4. *sālaṅkārāṃ dvijaśreṣṭha kanyāṃ yacchati yo naraḥ | sa gacched brahmasadanaṃ punarjanma na vidyate || 4 ||*
5. *annaṃ vāri dvijaśreṣṭha yena dattam mahītale | tena dattāni dānāni sarvāṇi ca dvijarṣabha || 5 ||*

**Explanations:**
*   Vocative of *a*-stems ends in **-a** (e.g., *deva!*).
*   *caturdaśa*: 14.
*   *sapta*: 7.
*   *janma*: birth (Nom./Acc. sg. of *janman* n.).
*   *sarva* 3: all, whole.
