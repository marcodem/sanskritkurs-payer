# Lesson 22

## 22.1. The Absolutive (*ktvā* / *lyap*)

In Sanskrit, if one wishes to express that one action of an agent (*kartṛ*) precedes another action of the same agent, or accompanies it as a co-occurring circumstance, the **Absolutive** (*ktvā* / *lyap*) is used. This translates roughly as:

> "after having done that, he does this"; "he does that, then he does this"; "as a result of this, he does that"; "I came, saw, and conquered."

The Absolutive is a **verbal adverb**, meaning it is neither conjugated nor declined. With few exceptions, it always shares the same agent (*kartṛ*) as the main action it precedes or accompanies. Therefore, the agent of the Absolutive stands in the **Nominative** (*prathamā*) or **Instrumental** (*tṛtīyā*), depending on the main sentence's construction.

Along with compounds, the Absolutive is one of the most frequent means of expression in Sanskrit.

When translating into English, avoid overusing "after." Instead, use common English expressions for temporal sequences.

**Schema:**
(modifiers of the Absolutive: adverbials, object, etc.) - **Absolutive** - **Absolutive** - ... - **Absolutive** - Agent + Verbal Sentence (Active or Passive)

Examples:
*   *gṛhaṃ praviśya bālāṃ dṛṣṭvā naro vadati*
    Passive construction: *gṛhaṃ praviśya bālāṃ dṛṣṭvā nareṇodyate*
    "The man enters the house, sees the little girl, and addresses her."
*   Plural: *gṛhaṃ praviśya bālāṃ dṛṣṭvā narā vadanti.*

## 22.2. Formation of the Absolutive

*   Verbs **without** a preverb: Absolutive in **-tvā**
*   Verbs **with** a preverb: Absolutive in **-ya** or **-tya**

### 22.2.1. Verbs without a Preverb: Absolutive in **-त्वा (-tvā)**

Formation: (Usually) root in the **weak grade** (as it appears before the PPP) + **-tvā**.

Only the negative prefix *a-* / *an-* can be combined with the suffix *-tvā*: *akṛtvā* "without having done."

Examples:
*   **āptvā**: after having reached/attained.
*   **āsitvā**: after having sat.
*   **itvā**: after having gone.
*   **sthitvā**: after having stood.
*   **jitvā**: after having conquered.
*   **uktvā**: after having spoken.

### 22.2.2. Verbs with a Preverb

#### 22.2.2.1. Roots ending in a long vowel (except -ā) or a consonant

Formation: (Usually) root in the **weak grade** + **-ya**.

Examples:
*   **upanīya**: after having led near.
*   **prabhūya**: after having stood forth / having had power.
*   **prāpya**: after having attained.

#### 22.2.2.2. Roots ending in **-ā**

Formation: Root remains in the **full grade** + **-ya**.

Example:
*   **upasthāya**: after having approached / stepped near. (Compare with the non-preverb form: *sthitvā*).

#### 22.2.2.3. Roots that end in short **-i, -u, -ṛ** in the weak grade

Formation: Root in the **weak grade** + **-tya**.

Examples:
*   **prastutya**: after having praised loudly.
*   **vismṛtya**: after having forgotten.
*   **saṃskṛtya**: after having prepared for sacrifice.

#### 22.2.2.4. Roots in **-am / -an** that end in **-a** in the weak grade

Optionally:
Root in *-am / -an* + **-ya**
OR:
Root in *-a* + **-tya**

Example:
*   **vigamya** or **vigatya**: after having passed away.

## 22.3. Vocabulary

*   **kāma** m.: wish, desire; desired gift, sensual pleasure, love, the god of love Kāma.
    *   *kāmam* (Acc. used adverbially): according to desire, to one's heart's content.
    ![](/images/lekt2202.jpg)
    Fig.: The god Kāmadeva, 19th century.
*   **śak** 5 P (*śaknoti*, Pass. *śakyate*, PPP *śakta*, Inf. *śaktum*): to be able, can.
    From this:
    *   **śakti** f.: ability, power, capacity; also: divine power, personified as the female companion especially of Śiva.
    *   **śakra** m.: "the Mighty One" (epithet of Indra).
    ![](/images/lekt2203.jpg)
    Fig.: *durgāśaktiḥ* = *durgaiva śaktiḥ*. Kolkata.
*   **arha** 1 P (*arhati*, Pass. *arhyate*, PPP *arhita*, Inf. *arhitum*): to deserve (to be worthy of), to be allowed, to be obliged to, should. (In the 2nd person, *arha* + Infinitive is often used as a mild command: "You should...").
    *   **arhant** 3 (Part. Präs. P): "a worthy one." In Buddhism and Jainism: one who has attained final liberation (Nirvana).
*   **vrata** n.: vow, religious duty, religious observance (one promises something to a deity to obtain something in return). Examples: fasting, abstinence from preferred foods, sexual abstinence, reading of sacred scriptures, performing specific rites, feeding Brahmins, etc.
*   **car** 1 P (*carati*, Pass. *caryate*, PPP *carita*, Inf. *caritum*): to graze, to wander about, to move, to act, to perform (e.g., *vrataṃ car*: to practice a vow, especially sexual abstinence).
    From this:
    *   **cara** 3: movable; n.: that which moves = animals (in contrast to plants).
    *   **caraṇa** n., m.: foot.
    *   **carita** n.: life conduct, life deeds.
    *   **brahmacarya** n.: practice of the Veda (*brahman*) = study of the Veda in the first stage of life (that of the *brahmacārin*), which requires strict sexual abstinence; therefore also: celibate lifestyle.
