# Lesson 25

## 25.1. Formation of the Ablative (*pañcamī* = "fifth case-ending")

Except for masculine and neuter stems in *-a* and pronouns, the forms of the **Ablative** (*pañcamī*) in the singular are identical to those of the **Genitive** (*ṣaṣṭhī*) in all declension classes.

Except for personal pronouns, in all declensions the forms of the Ablative in the plural are identical to those of the **Dative** (*caturthī*).

Now you can see the reason for the order of cases (*vibhakti*) in Sanskrit: they are arranged so that identical forms are placed as close together as possible.

**Ablative singular of masculine / neuter stems in -a:**
*   *deva* → Abl. sg. **devāt**

**Interrogative, Relative, and Demonstrative Pronouns:**

| | Abl. Sg. Masc./Neut. | Abl. Sg. Fem. |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **kim** | *kasmāt* | *kasyās* |
| **yad** | *yasmāt* | *yasyās* |
| **tad** | *tasmāt* | *tasyās* |
| **etad** | *etasmāt* | *etasyās* |
| **idam** | *asmāt* | *asyās* |

## 25.2. Use of the Ablative (*pañcamī*)

> "The ablative designates that which remains fixed when something moves away from it."
> — Pāṇini 2,3,28 + 1,4,24

The Ablative primarily answers the questions "From where?" and "Why?".

1. It designates the point of departure, origin, and material:
Examples:
*   *grāmād āgacchati* = "He comes from the village."
*   *aśvāt patitaḥ* = "fallen from the horse."
*   *tebhyo labdham* = "received from them."

The Ablative can also designate the person from whom one buys, hears, or wishes something:
Examples:
*   *guror dharmaṃ śṛṇoti* = "he hears about the dharma from the teacher."
*   *brāhmaṇaḥ kṣatriyād dhenumicchati* = "the Brahmin wishes for a milch cow from the Kṣatriya."

2. The Ablative is used with verbs meaning "to keep away from," "to protect from," "to defend against," "to fear":
Example:
*   *aribhyo rakṣati* = "he protects from the enemies."

3. The Ablative designates the reason or cause:
Examples:
*   *krodhāt putraṃ hanti* = "He slays his son out of anger."
*   *kṛtapāpatvān narakaṃ gacchati* = "Because he has done evil, he goes to hell" (literally: "due to the state of having done evil").
*   *pāpakaraṇān narakaṃ gacchati* = "Because of doing evil, he goes to hell."

Nouns that are not feminine can stand in either the **Instrumental** (*tṛtīyā*) or the **Ablative** (*pañcamī*) to express the reason for an action. Feminine nouns usually stand in the Instrumental in this sense, though they can occasionally be in the Ablative.

## 25.3. The Suffix *-tas*

To clearly express an ablative meaning in the singular, one can append the suffix **-tas** to the stem. This forms adverbs that answer the question "From where?":
Examples:
*   **āditas** = "from the beginning."
*   **dharmatas** = "because of the dharma, according to dharma."

The suffix *-tas* is also used with pronominal stems:
*   **tad**: *tatas* = "from there, there, then, therefore."
*   **yad**: *yatas* = "from which, from where, since, because."
*   **kim**: *kutas* = "whence? why?"

## 25.4. Other Ways to Express Reasons/Causes

1. **Relative Clauses**:
Relative clauses often express a causal ("because"), consecutive ("consequently"), or final ("for the purpose of") relationship. The following forms serve as causal conjunctions:
*   Ablative: *yatas... tatas...* OR *yasmāt... tasmāt...* = "because..., therefore..."
*   Instrumental: *yena... tena...* = "since/by which..., therefore/thereby..."

Example:
*   *yato (yasmād / yena) dhanaṃ icchati [tato (tasmād / tena)] rāmo vrataṃ carati* = "Because Rāma wishes for wealth, he practices the vow."

2. **hi**:
Main clauses can be linked using the particle **hi** ("for/because"). A sentence with *hi* (which cannot stand first in a sentence, but usually second in prose) provides a reason for the preceding or following sentence:
Example:
*   *janāḥ puṇyaṃ kurvanti | svargaṃ hi gantumicchanti* = "People perform merits. For they wish to reach heaven."

3. **Instrumental (*tṛtīyā*)**:
As mentioned, the instrumental is used alongside the ablative to indicate reasons. For feminine nouns, the instrumental is generally mandatory.
Example:
*   *krodhena putraṃ hanti* = "He slays his son out of / with anger."

4. **Nouns**:
One can also express reasons using nouns like **kāraṇa** n. ("cause") or **hetu** m. in the Instrumental or Ablative, following a Genitive or as part of a compound:
Example:
*   *puṇyasya kāraṇāt (hetoh)* = "because of the merit."

5. **iti**:
The motive for an action can be presented as a thought using **iti**:
Example:
*   *satyasaṃbuddhaḥ sugata ity ānando gautamaṃ dharmaṃ pṛcchati* = "Because the Sugata is fully awakened to the truth, Ānanda asks Gautama about the doctrine" (literally: "Thinking, 'The Sugata is fully awakened...'").
