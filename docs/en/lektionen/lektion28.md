# Lesson 28

## 28.1. The Causative (*ṇi, ṇic, kārita*)

To express that someone or something causes or effects that someone else or something else does or undergoes something (expressed by a root, with or without preverbs), the **Causative** (*ṇi, ṇic, kārita*) is used.

Example:
*   **gam** "to go" → Causative: **gamayati** "he causes someone to go; he sends."
*   **dṛś** "to see" → Causative: **darśayati** "he makes someone see = he shows."

## 28.2. Sentence Construction with the Causative

**Schema 1:**
If the root underlying the causative is **intransitive** (i.e., takes no accusative object) or expresses **motion, knowledge, information, or eating**:
> **Agent (*kartṛ*) of the causative** in the Nominative (*prathamā*) — **Agent of the caused action** in the Accusative (*dvitīyā*) — (**Goal of motion, object of knowledge, etc.** in the Accusative) — **Verb in the Causative**.

Examples:
*   *durjano rāmaṃ mohayati* = "A wicked man causes Rāma to be confused = confuses Rāma."
    (Result: *rāmo muhyati* = "Rāma is confused.")
*   *rāmaḥ śatrūn svargaṃ gamayati* = "Rāma makes the enemies go to heaven = sends the enemies to heaven."
    (Result: *śatravaḥ svargaṃ gacchanti* = "The enemies go to heaven.")
*   *guru rāmaṃ vedārthaṃ vedayati* = "The master makes Rāma know the meaning of the Veda = explains the Veda to Rāma."
    (Result: *rāmo vedārthaṃ vetti* = "Rāma knows/understands the meaning.")
*   *sa rāmaṃ vedam adhyāpayati* = "He teaches Rāma the Veda."
    (Result: *rāmo vedam adhīte* = "Rāma studies the Veda.")
*   *rāmo devān amṛtam āśayati* = "Rāma lets the gods eat the food of immortality."
    (Result: *devā amṛtam aśnanti* = "The gods eat immortality.")

![](/images/lekt2802.jpg)
Fig.: *sa narān prabandhavijñānam adhyāpayati* (He teaches the men management science).

According to Schema 1, the root **dṛś** is also constructed:
*   *(sa) rāmaṃ putraṃ darśayati* = "he shows the son to Rāma."

The following verse summarizes examples of causative formation according to Schema 1 (verbs are in the 3rd person Imperfect Parasmaipada):
> *śatrūn agamayad svargaṃ*
> *vedārthaṃ svān avedayat |*
> *āśayaccāmṛtaṃ devān*
> *vedam adhyāpayad vidhim |*
> *āsayad salile pṛthvīṃ*
> *yaḥ sa me śrīharirgatiḥ ||*
(Hari is my refuge and goal, who sent the enemies to heaven, taught his own the meaning of the Veda, fed the gods with immortality, taught the Creator the Veda, and placed the earth in the water.)

![](/images/lekt2801.jpg)
Fig.: *sa me śrīharirgatiḥ*. ISKCON Temple, Bangalore.

**Schema 2:**
For **transitive** verbs (other than those mentioned in Schema 1), and for all verbs when a causative action itself is caused (causative of a causative):
> **Agent of the causative** in the Nominative — **Agent of the caused action** in the Instrumental (*tṛtīyā*) — **Object of the caused action** in its original case (usually Accusative) — **Verb in the Causative**.

Examples:
*   *(sa) rāmeṇa bhāryāṃ tyājayati* = "He causes Rāma to leave his wife."
    (Result: *rāmo bhāryāṃ tyajati*.)
*   *viṣṇumitro rāmeṇa govindaṃ gamayati* = "Viṣṇumitra causes Rāma to send Govinda away."
    (Result: *rāmo govindaṃ gamayati* → *govindo gacchati*.)

**Exceptions to Schema 1 (Constructed according to Schema 2):**
*   **nī** "to lead"
*   **vah** "to carry/drive" (if the agent is not the driver)
*   **smṛ** "to remember" (usually)
*   **ad** "to eat"
*   **khād** "to chew"

Examples:
*   *rāmo bhṛtyena bhāraṃ nāyayati vāhayati vā* = "Rāma lets the servant lead or carry the load."
*   *rāmo bālenānam ādayati khādayati vā* = "Rāma lets the boy eat or chew the food."

**Verbs that can follow both Schema 1 and Schema 2:**
*   **hṛ** "to hold, fetch"
*   **kṛ** "to do, make"
*   **dṛś** in the Ātmanepada causative.

**Schema A (Passive Causative Construction):**
> **Agent of the causative** in the Instrumental — **Agent of the caused action** in the Nominative — **Object of the caused action** in its original case (Accusative) — **Verb in the Passive Causative**.

Examples:
*   *guruṇā rāmo grāmaṃ gamyate* = "Rāma is sent to the village by the master."
*   *rāmeṇa bhṛtyaḥ kaṭaṃ kāryate* = "The servant is made to make a mat by Rāma."

## 28.3. Formation of the Present Stem of the Causative

The thematic endings of the present are added to the causative stem. There are four main types:

1.  **Full-grade root + -aya-**:
    *   *viś* → *veśayati* (leads in)
    *   *lubh* → *lobhayati* (entices)
    *   *dṛś* → *darśayati* (shows)
    *   *vṛt* → *vartayati* (turns/rolls trans.)
2.  **Dehn-grade root + -aya-** (often vocalic roots or roots in *-a-* + single consonant):
    *   *bhū* → *bhāvayati* (causes to be, produces)
    *   *i* → *āyayati* (causes to go)
    *   *nī* → *nāyayati* (causes to lead)
    *   *kṛ* → *kārayati* (causes to do)
    *   *vac* → *vācayati* (causes to speak, reads aloud)
3.  **Deep-grade root + -aya-**:
    *   *duṣ* → *dūṣayati* (spoils, corrupts)
4.  **Root + -paya-** (roots in *-ā* and others):
    *   *sthā* → *sthāpayati* (sets up, places)
    *   *adhi-i* → *adhyāpayati* (teaches)

**Irregular Formations:**
*   **han** → *ghātayati* (causes to kill/slay).
*   **pā** (protect) → *pālayati* (protects, saves; related to *pṛ*).

## 28.4. Passive of the Causative

Formed by: **Causative stem without -aya- + passive suffix -ya-**.
Examples:
*   *bhāvyate* "it is produced."
*   *sthāpyate* "it is set up."
(Note: Often uses full/dehn-grade instead of the deep-grade used in normal passives).

## 28.5. Future of the Causative

Formed from the causative stem, omitting the final *-a-*: **-ay-iṣya-**.
Example:
*   *budh* → *bodhayiṣyati* "he will awaken."

## 28.6. PPP of the Causative

**Causative stem on -ay- (without a) + -i- + -ta**.
Examples:
*   *gam* → *gamayati* → *gamita* "sent" (Simple: *gata*).
*   *sthā* → *sthāpayati* → *sthāpita* "set up."

## 28.7. Absolutive of the Causative

*   In **-tvā**: *sthāpayitvā* "after setting up."
*   In **-ya**: Usually to the stem without *-aya-*: *prabodhy* "after awakening."
    If the root has a short *-a-* before one consonant, then to the *-ay-* stem: *āgamayya*.

## 28.8. Infinitive of the Causative

**Causative stem on -ay- (without a) + -i- + -tum**.
Example:
*   *jan* → *janayati* → *janayitum* "to produce."

## 28.9. The 10th Present Class (*curādi*)

Some roots follow this formation without being strictly causative.
*   *kam* 10Ā → *kāmayate* "to love."
*   *cur* 10U → *corayati* "to steal."
(Future: *corayiṣyati*; Passive: *coryate*).

## 28.10. Vocabulary

*   **vid** 2P (*vetti, vidanti*, Fut. *vediṣyati*, Pass. *vidyate*, Kaus. *vedayati*, PPP *vidita*): to know.
    *   **vidyā** f., **veda** m.
*   **vid** 6U (*vindati*, Pass. *vidyate* "there is"): to find.
*   **adhi-i** 2Ā (*adhīte, adhīyate*, Kaus. *adhyāpayati*): to study/teach.
    *   **adhyayana** n.: study; **adhyāya** m.: chapter/lesson.
*   **kam** 10Ā (*kāmayate*, PPP *kānta*!): to love.
    ![](/images/lekt2804.jpg)
*   **cur** 10 (*corayati*): to steal.
*   **darśayati**: to show.
*   **mānayati**: to honor, esteem.
*   **vācayati**: to read aloud.
*   **vādayati**: to play a musical instrument ("cause it to speak").
    ![](/images/lekt2803.jpg)
*   **bhāra** m.: load, burden.
    ![](/images/lekt2805.jpg)
