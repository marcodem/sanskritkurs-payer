# Lesson 41

## 41.1. Two Wisdom Sayings (*subhāṣite*)

1.  *pustakasthā ca yā vidyā / parahaste ca yaddhanam |*
    *kāryakāle samutpanne / na sā vidyā na taddhanam || 1 ||*
    "Knowledge that resides in books and wealth that is in another's hand—when the time for action arises, that is neither knowledge nor wealth."
    
    ![](/images/lekt4102.jpg)
    Fig.: *pustakasthā ca yā vidyā ...*

2.  *upadeśo hi mūrkhāṇāṃ / prakopāya na śāntaye |*
    *payaḥpānaṃ bhujaṅgānāṃ / kevalaṃ viṣavardhanam || 2 ||*
    "Giving advice to fools leads to anger, not to peace. Providing milk to snakes only increases (their) venom."

## 41.2. Further Primary (*kṛt*) Formations in *-a*

### 41.2.1. Present Participle Middle, Passive, and Future Participle Middle

#### 41.2.1.1. Thematic Stems (Classes 1, 4, 6, 10, Passive, Future)

**Formation:**
**Present/Passive/Future Stem + *-māna*** (fem. *-mānā*)

**Examples:**
*   **yaj** 1U, Pres. Part. Ā: *yajamāna* 3 ("one who sacrifices for their own benefit, the patron of the sacrifice").
*   **man** 4Ā, Pres. Part. Ā: *manyamāna* 3 ("thinking").
*   **kṛ** 8U, Pres. Part. Pass.: *kriyamāṇa* 3 ("being done").
*   **dā** 3U, Fut. Part. Ā: *dāsyamāna* 3 ("one who will give for their own benefit").

#### 41.2.1.2. Athematic Present Classes (2, 3, 5, 7, 8, 9)

**Formation:**
**Weak Present Stem + *-āna*** (fem. *-ānā*)
(The stem form is usually the one found before the 3rd pl. Ā ending *-ate*).

**Examples:**
| Class | Root | Present Participle Middle | Formation Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **2.** | *dviṣ* | *dviṣāṇa* | *dviṣ + āna* |
| **3.** | *hu* | *&lt;juhvāna&gt;* | *ju-hu + āna* |
| **5.** | *su* | *sunvāna* | *su-nu + āna* |
| **7.** | *rudh* | *rundhāna* | *ru-n-dh-āna* |
| **8.** | *tan* | *tanvāna* | *tan-u + āna* |
| **9.** | *krī* | *krīṇāna* | *krī-ṇ-āna* |

### 41.2.2. Gerundives (Participles of Passive Necessity) (*kṛtya*)

To express passive necessity ("what must/should be done"), adjectives are formed using roots or derived stems with one of the following suffixes:
*   **-tavya** (fem. **-tavyā**)
*   **-anīya** (fem. **-anīyā**)
*   **-ya** (fem. **-yā**)
*   **-tya** (fem. **-tyā**) (instead of *-ya/-yā* in specific cases)

#### 41.2.2.1. The Suffix *-tavya*

Attached to roots and derived stems (like Causatives) in the same way as the Infinitive suffix *-tum*:
*   High-grade (*guṇa*) root + **tavya**
*   High-grade root + **-i-** + **tavya** (for *seṭ* roots)

**Examples:**
*   **ji** 1P → **jetavya** 3 ("one who must be defeated").
*   **vṛt** 1Ā → **vartitavya** 3 ("that where one should be").
*   **budh** (Caus.) → **bodhayitavya** ("one who should be awakened").

#### 41.2.2.2. The Suffix *-anīya*

**Formation:**
*   High-grade root + **-anīya**
*   Causative/Class 10: Root form (without *-aya-*) + **-anīya**

**Examples:**
*   **dā** 3U → **dānīya** 3 ("to be given").
*   **ji** 1P → **jayanīya** 3 ("to be defeated").
*   **kṛ** 8U → **karaṇīya** 3 ("to be done").
*   **dṛś** → **darśanīya** 3 ("to be seen, worth seeing").
*   **budh** (Caus.) → **bodhanīya** 3 (*bodh + anīya*).
*   **dā** (Caus.) → **dāpanīya** 3 (*dā-p + anīya*).

#### 41.2.2.3. The Suffix *-ya*

**Formation:**
Root (in deep, high, or extended grade) + **-ya**.

**Treatment of Final Vowels:**
1.  **Roots in *-ā***: Form the Gerundive in **-eya**.
    - *jñā* 9U → *jñeya* 3 ("to be known").
    - *dā* 3U → *deya* 3 ("to be given").
2.  **Roots in *-i, -ī, -u, -ū, -ṛ***: Usually take High or Extended grade.
    - *smṛ* 1P → *smarya* 3 ("to be remembered").
    - *nī* 1U → *neya* 3 ("to be led").
    - *vikrī* 9Ā → *vikreya* 3 ("to be sold, for sale").
    ![](/images/lekt4103.jpg)
    Fig.: *vikreyāṇi puṣpāni* (Flowers for sale)
3.  **Roots in vowels followed by *-ya***:
    - *stu* 2U → *stavya* 3 ("to be praised").
    - *stu* 2U → *stāvya* 3 ("necessarily to be praised").

**Consonantal Roots:**
*   **Deep grade**: *dṛś* → *dṛśya* 3 ("visible, worth seeing").
*   **Deep grade with vowel change**: *śās* 2P → *śiṣya* 3 ("one to be taught = student").
    ![](/images/lekt4106.jpg)
    Fig.: *dṛśyo mandiraḥ* (Visible temple)
*   **High grade**: *dviṣ* 2U → *dveṣya* 3 ("to be hated = enemy").

**Causatives / Class 10**: Root form (without *-aya-*) + **-ya**.
*   **man** (Caus.) → **mānya** 3 ("to be honored, highly respected").
    ![](/images/lekt4107.jpg)
    Fig.: *mānyaḥ* (Honored one)

#### 41.2.2.4. The Suffix *-tya*

Used with specific roots ending in *-i, -u, -ṛ* (instead of *-ya*).
**Formation:** Deep-grade root + **-tya**.
*   **i** 2P → **itya** 3 ("to be gone").
*   **śru** 5P → **śrutya** 3 ("to be heard").
*   **kṛ** 8U → **kṛtya** 3 ("to be done").

## 41.3. Syntax of Gerundives

**Attributive Use:**
*   *darśanīyaṃ nagaram* = "a city worth seeing"

**Predicative Use (Passive Construction):**
Expresses obligation, command, or necessity.
*   *kāśī dvijair draṣṭavyā* = "Benares must be seen by the twice-born."
    ![](/images/lekt4108.jpg)
    Fig.: *darśanīyaṃ nagaraṃ kāśī*

**Usage Differences:**
*   **-tavya**: Always expresses necessity; preferred predicatively. Avoided in compounds.
*   **-anīya, -ya, -tya**: Can express possibility ("-able") or worthiness ("-worthy"). Can be negated with *a-/an-* and used as the final member of *tatpuruṣa* compounds.

**Constraint**: Gerundives should not be combined with *su-* or *dus-*. Use *tatpuruṣas* like *sukara* ("easy to do") instead.

## 41.4. Vocabulary

*   **mūrkha** m. / **mūḍha**: fool, stupid person.
*   **bhujanga** m.: snake, serpent.
    ![](/images/lekt4105.jpg)
*   **kevalam** (adv.): only, alone, completely.
*   **viṣa** n.: poison, venom.
    ![](/images/lekt4104.jpg)
*   **śās** 2P (*śāsti*, Weak stem *śiṣ*, 3.pl.P *śāsati*, PPP *śiṣṭa*): to correct, rule, teach.
    *   **śāsana** n. / **śāsanā** f.: royal edict, teaching, religion.
        ![](/images/lekt4109.jpg)
    *   **śāstra** n.: science, teaching, technical work.
    *   **śāstrin** m.: learned man, scientist, scholar.
        ![](/images/lekt4110.jpg)
    *   **śiṣya** 3: student, pupil.
*   **śaraṇa** 3: protecting; n. protection, refuge.
*   **saṅgha** m.: community, assembly (especially the Buddhist order).
*   **kanyā** f.: young girl, daughter, maiden.
*   **ati-** (Preverb): over, beyond, excessive.
    *   **i + ati** 2P (*atyeti*): to pass away, to go beyond.
    *   **atīta** (PPP): passed away; n. the past.
