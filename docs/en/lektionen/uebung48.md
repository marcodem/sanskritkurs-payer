# Exercise 48

A) Translate the subhāṣitāni at the beginning of the lesson.

सत्यम् वद ॥१॥

> Speak the truth!

धर्मं चर ॥२॥

> Practice the Dharma (hold to right and custom)!

मातृदेवो भव ॥३॥

> Let your mother be a deity to you (revere your mother like a goddess)!

गौरवं प्राप्यते दानात् ॥४॥

> Dignity is attained through generosity.

श्वः कार्यमद्य कुर्वीत ॥५॥

> Do today what is to be done tomorrow!  
> (Do not put off until tomorrow what you can do today!)

विद्याविहीनः पशुः ॥६॥

> One without knowledge is a beast.

लाघवं वैयाकरणस्य भूषणम् ॥७॥

> Conciseness is the ornament of the grammarian.

B) Translate into Sanskrit (use the imperative and, if possible, roots of the 2nd and 3rd present classes):

1. Having obtained a son, leave the family!

> पुत्रं लब्ध्वा कुलं जहाहि । जहीहि । जहिहि ॥१॥

2. Descendants of Puru, fear those who have done evil!

> पैरवाः कृतपापेभ्यो बिभीत ॥२॥

3. The girls should give food to the beggars.

> कन्या भिक्षुभ्यो ऽन्नं ददतु ॥३॥

4. We want to speak.

> ब्रवाम । वचाम ॥४॥

5. With the words "Come, monk!", Buddha admitted the man into the monastic order (upasam-pad causative).

> एहि भिक्ष इति बुद्धो नरमुपसमपादयत् ॥५॥

6. Be true descendants of Manu!

> सद्मानवाः स्त । सन्मानवाः स्त ॥६॥

7. I want to praise Śiva and the other gods.

> शिवादिदेवान्स्तवानि ॥७॥

8. Tell!

> आख्याहि ॥८॥

9. Measure the hells!

> नरकान्मिमीष्व ॥९॥

10. They (pl.) should lie on these couches.

> एतेषु शयनेषु शेरताम् ॥१०॥

11. The men who are like tigers should kill those who are enemies of Indra.

> पुरुषव्याघ्रा इन्द्रशत्रून्घ्नन्तु ॥११॥

12. Concentrate!

> समाधेहि । समाधत्स्व ॥१२॥

13. Sit here!

> अत्राध्वम् ॥१३॥

14. We want to eat these fruits.

> तानि फलान्यदाम ॥१४॥

15. The servant should milk the cow.

> दासो धेनुं दोग्धु ॥१५॥

16. O king, protect the Dharma and the people!

> राजन्धर्मं जनांश्च पाहि ॥१६॥

17. Teach the students the Veda!

> शिष्याञ्शाधि वेदम् । शिष्याञ्छाधि वेदम् ॥१७॥

18. He should put on new clothes.

> नवानि वस्त्राणि वस्ताम् ॥१८॥

19. They (pl.) should sit in my house.

> मम गृह आसताम् ॥१९॥

20. Husbands should support (i.e. maintain) their wives.

> भर्तारो भार्या बिभ्रतु ॥२०॥

Manusmṛti 9 (Duties of Women):

अस्वतन्त्राः स्त्रियः कार्याः पुरुषैः स्वैर्दिवानिशम् ।  
विषयेषु च सज्जन्त्यः संस्थाप्या आत्मनो वशे ॥२॥

> Men must keep their women in dependence day and night. Women who are attached to sense objects must be kept under the control of their men.

पिता रक्षति कौमारे भर्ता रक्षति यौवने ।  
रक्षन्ति स्थाविरे पुत्रा न स्त्री स्वातन्त्र्यमर्हति ॥३॥

> Her father protects her in her childhood, her husband protects her in her youth, her sons protect her in old age. A woman is not fit for independence.

काले ऽदाता पिता वाच्यो वाच्यश्चानुपनयन्पतिः ।  
मृते भर्तरि पुत्रस्तु वाच्यो मातुररक्षिता ॥४॥

> Blameworthy is the father who does not give his daughter away at the right time. Blameworthy is the husband who does not have sexual intercourse with her at the right time. Blameworthy is the son who does not protect his mother after the death of her husband.

सूक्ष्मेभ्यो ऽपि प्रसङ्गेभ्यः स्त्रियो रक्ष्या विशेषतः ।  
द्वयोर्हि कुलयोः शोकमावहेयुररक्षिताः ॥५॥

> Women must be protected particularly from even minor opportunities. Unprotected, they would bring sorrow to two families.

इमं हि सर्ववर्णानां पश्यन्तो धर्ममुत्तमम् ।  
यतन्ते रक्षितुं भार्यां भर्तारो दुर्बला अपि ॥६॥

> Seeing this as the highest duty of all classes, even weak husbands strive to protect their wives.

स्वां प्रसूतिं चरित्रं च कुलमात्मानमेव च ।  
स्वं च धर्मं प्रयत्नेन जायां रक्षन्हि रक्षति ॥७॥

> He who carefully protects his wife protects his offspring, his conduct, his family, himself, and his own religious duty.

पतिर्भार्यां संप्रविश्य गर्भो भूत्वेह जायते ।  
जायायास्तद्धि जायात्वं यद् अस्यां जायते पुनः ॥८॥

> The husband enters his wife, becomes an embryo, and is then born on this world. This is the wifehood of the wife (jāyā), that her husband is born again from her.
