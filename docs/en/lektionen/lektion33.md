# Lesson 33

## 33.1. Reduplication (*abhyāsa* m.)

Reduplication is used in the formation of:
*   The 3rd present class (and some verbs from other classes).
*   The Perfect tense.
*   The Reduplicated Aorist.
*   Freqentatives and Desideratives.

The syllable added before the root is called the **reduplication syllable**. It consists of a consonant and a vowel.

### 33.1.1. The Reduplication Consonant

1.  **General Rule**: The first consonant of the root is repeated.
    - *dā* 3 → *dadāti* (3.sg.P)
    - *pṛ* 3 → *piparti* (3.sg.P)
    - *mā* 3Ā → *mimīte* (3.sg.Ā)

2.  **Aspirates**: An aspirated initial consonant is reduplicated by the corresponding non-aspirated consonant.
    - *dhā* 3 → *dadhāti*
    - *bhī* 3 → *bibheti*

3.  **Gutturals**: A guttural is reduplicated by the corresponding non-aspirated palatal.
    - **k, kh** → **c**
    - **g, gh** → **j**
    - **h** is always reduplicated as **j**.
    - *hu* 3 → *juhoti*

4.  **Consonant Clusters**: If a root starts with multiple consonants, only the first one is repeated (subject to rules 2 and 3).

5.  **Sibilants**: If a root starts with a cluster of **Sibilant + voiceless stop**, the sibilant is skipped, and the voiceless stop is reduplicated.
    - *sthā* 1 → *tiṣṭhati*

## 33.2. The 3rd Present Class (*juhotyādi* = "juho etc.")

**Formation:**
*   **Strong Stem**: Reduplicated high-grade root + ending.
*   **Weak Stem**: Reduplicated deep-grade root + ending.

### 33.2.1. The Reduplication Vowel of the 3rd Class

*   The regular vowel is **-i-**.
*   Roots containing **u** reduplicate with **-u-**.
*   The roots **dā, dhā, hā** reduplicate with **-a-**.

**Example:**
### **hu** 3P "to pour into the fire (sacrifice)"
*   Strong Stem: *juho-*
*   Weak Stem: *juhu-*

| | 3. sg. P | 3. pl. P | 3. sg. Ā | 3. pl. Ā |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Present** | *juhoti* | *juhvati* (*juhu-ati*) | *juhute* | *juhvate* |
| **Imperfect** | *ajuhot* | *ajuhavur* (*a-juho-ur*) | *ajuhuta* | *ajuhvata* |
| **Optative** | *juhuyāt* | *juhuyur* | *juhvīta* | *juhvīran* |

## 33.3. On the Ablaut of *ā*

In the *ā*-group, the vowel *ā* can behave in two ways:

**Reihe A (e.g., *dhā*):**
*   **Deep Grade**: **-i-** (before consonants, e.g., *hita*) or **Ø** (before vowels, e.g., *dadhati*).
*   **High Grade**: **-ā-** (e.g., *dadhāti*).

**Reihe B (e.g., *pā*):**
*   **Deep Grade**: **-ī-** (e.g., *pīta*).
*   **High Grade**: **-ā-** (e.g., *pātum*).

## 33.4. Roots ending in *-ā* of the 3rd Class

Roots ending in *-ā* (except *dā* and *dhā*) usually weaken to **-ī-** in the weak stem before consonants, and disappear before vowels.

**Examples:**
*   **mā** 3Ā "to measure": *mimīte* (3.sg.Ā), *mimate* (3.pl.Ā).
*   **hā** 3P "to leave": *jahāti* (3.sg.P), *jahati* (3.pl.P).

### 33.4.1. The Roots **dā** and **dhā**

These reduplicate with **-a-** and lose the root vowel in the weak stem. Note the aspiration dissimilation in *dhā*.

### **dhā** 3U "to put, set, assign"

| | 3. sg. P | 3. pl. P | 3. sg. Ā | 3. pl. Ā |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Present** | *dadhāti* | *dadhati* | *dhatte* (*dadh-te*) | *dadhate* |
| **Imperfect** | *adadhāt* | *adadhur* | *adhatta* | *adadhata* |
| **Optative** | *dadhyāt* | *dadhyur* | *dadhīta* | *dadhīran* |

The forms of **dā** (to give) follow the same pattern, replacing *dh* with *d* (e.g., *dadāti, dadati, datte, dadate*).

## 33.5. Present Participle Active of the 3rd Class

All forms of the Parasmaipada present participle are formed from the **weak stem**.
Example: **dā** → *dadat* (m./n.), *dadatī* (f.).

## 33.7. Exercise

A) Form sentences using the pattern: *rāmas ... annaṃ dadāti.* (to a monk, to the fire, to a Śūdra woman, to a virtuous son, etc. in Singular and Plural).

B) Fill in the correct forms in Present, Imperfect, and Optative:
1. *brāhmaṇo ghṛtam agnau ... (hu)*
2. *buddhagatā bhayān na ... (bhī)*
3. *sugataḥ kulam ... (hā)*
4. *durjanā bhikṣubhyo 'nnaṃ na ... (dā)*
5. *sādhuḥ kṛṣṇe matiṃ ... (dhā + sam + ā)*
6. *īśvaro lokān ... janās tu na ... (mā)*
7. *dāsā bhārān ... (bhṛ)*
8. *brāhmaṇī pātraṃ jalena ... (pṛ)*

C) Translate:
1. One who is yoked by Yoga concentrates (*samādhatte*) his mind on wisdom leading to the end of suffering.
2. He who gives gifts to a monk will also receive (*ādadīta*) the merit of giving.
3. Because Brahmins do not carry loads, the servant of the Brahmin carried (*abhibhar*) the load to the house.
   ![](/images/lekt3308.jpg)
4. The warrior hero took his son (*putram ādāya*) and left the family to fight. Because he was killed by the enemy in battle, he left his body and attained a new birth.
5. Even Devadatta's desire for the end of suffering does not fulfill (*piparti*) happiness. This desire is only fulfilled (*sampūryate*) through wisdom.
6. From the holy man who gives fearlessness to all beings, beings are not afraid (*bibhyati*), and he is not afraid of them.
7. Those of small minds do good and leave (*jahati*) evil out of fear of hell and greed for heaven. But those of infinite wisdom enlightened by Buddha would not fear hell nor greed for heaven. They have hindered both fear and greed.
