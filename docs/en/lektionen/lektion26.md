# Lesson 21

## 26.1. The Cerebralization Law for *-s-* within a word

The sound **-s-** is replaced by **-ṣ-** if it is preceded by *k, r, l,* or any vowel except *a* or *ā*, either immediately or separated only by *anusvāra* or *visarga*, provided that the following sound is not *ṛ* or *r*.

This sandhi rule also applies to the prefix *dus-* before voiceless gutturals and labials in compounds.

Example:
*   *teṣām* (from *te-sām*)
*   But: *tāsām* (*tā-sām*) because of the preceding *ā*.

## 26.2. The Cerebralization Law for Dentals within a word

Dental stop consonants are replaced by the corresponding retroflexes when they follow a retroflex sound (including *-ṣ-*):
*   *iṣ + -ta* = **iṣṭa**
*   *ti + sth + a + ti* = **tiṣṭhati** (*ṣ* due to the preceding *i*, *ṭh* due to the now preceding *ṣ*).

## 26.3. Word Sandhi for Palatals, Gutturals, *ṣ, h* before *-s*

The sounds **-k, -kh, -g, -gh; -c, -j; -ś, -ṣ; -h** combine with a following **s-** to form **-kṣ**.

Examples can be seen in the future tense formations below.

## 26.4. Grassmann’s Law of Aspiration Dissimilation

When two aspirated consonants occur in successive syllables (Aspirate - Vowel - Aspirate), the first loses its aspiration.
Example: **\*dhu(gh)** → **du(g)h**

If the second sound loses its aspiration due to other sound laws, the aspiration reappears on the first sound.
Examples: See the future tense of *duh* and *budh* below.

## 26.5. Word Sandhi for *-m, -n* before Sibilants

Before sibilants (*ś, ṣ, s*), final **-n** and **-m** are replaced by **-ṃ-** (*anusvāra*).
Example: See the future of *man* below.

## 26.6. Use of the Simple Future (*lṛṭ*, *bhaviṣyantī* f.)

The Simple Future (*lṛṭ*) expresses:
*   The future.
*   An intended or willed action ("I will go," "I want to go").
*   An expectation ("it will happen thus").
*   Hope.
*   Fear.
*   Probability.
*   The speaker's opinion.
*   A command.

The future tense partly competes with the Optative.
Example:
*   *vrataṃ kariṣyan devagṛhaṃ gacchati* = "One who wishes to perform a vow goes to the temple."

## 26.7. Formation of the Simple Future (*lṛṭ*, *bhaviṣyantī* f.)

Simple Future: **Future Stem + Primary Ending**

Future Stem:
(Usually) **full-grade root + -sya**
OR
(Usually) **full-grade root + -i- + -sya** (= **-iṣya**)

Whether a root uses the connecting vowel *-i-* must be learned as part of the stem forms! Generally, the root form and the connecting vowel of the future match those of the Infinitive. Some roots form the future optionally with or without *-i-*.

The future participle Parasmaipada is formed by adding the suffix **-nt-** to the future stem; the feminine is optionally in **-ntī** or **-tī**:
*   *kṛ* → *kariṣyant*, *kariṣyantī* / *kariṣyatī*

Examples:

| Root | Future 3rd sg. P (Ā) |
| :--- | :--- |
| **nī** | *neṣyati* |
| **bhū** | *bhaviṣyati* (*bho + i + ṣya + ti*) |
| **diś** | *dekṣyati* (*deś + sya + ti*) |
| **dṛś** | *drakṣyati* |
| **pac** | *pakṣyati* |
| **pracch** | *prakṣyati* |
| **yaj** | *yakṣyati* |
| **sṛj** | *srakṣyati* |
| **pad** | *patsyati* |
| **ad** | *atsyati* |
| **duh** | *dhokṣyati* (*dogh + sya + ti*) |
| **budh** | *bhotsyate* (*bodh* before voiceless = *bhot*) |
| **man** | *maṃsyate* (*man + sya + te*) |
