# Lesson 36

## 36.1. Special Perfect Formations

1.  **Initial Consonant Changes**: Some roots change their initial consonant after the reduplication syllable.
    - **ji** 1P: *jigāya*, *jigyur*.
    - **ci** 5U: *cikāya* / *cicāya*, *cikyur* / *cicyur*.
    - **han** 2P: *jaghāna*, *jaghnur*.

2.  **vid** 2P ("to know"): Has a perfect form with **present** meaning.
    - *veda* (he/she knows), *vidur* (they know).

3.  **ah** ("to say"): Exists only in the Perfect (Type I) with **present** meaning.
    - *āha* (he/she says), *āhur* (they say).

4.  **bhū** 1P: Has the irregular perfect stem *babhū-* (becoming *babhūv-* before vowels).
    - *babhūva* (3.sg.P), *babhūvur* (3.pl.P).

## 36.2. The Periphrastic Perfect (*anuprayogaliṭ*)

**Usage:**
1.  Derived conjugations (Causative, Desiderative, etc.).
2.  Roots starting with a long vowel (except *ā-*), or a vowel (except *a-*) before two consonants.
3.  Certain other roots.

**Formation:**
**(Weak) Present Stem + *-ām* + Perfect of *kṛ, as,* or *bhū*.**
*Note: The final vowel of the stem is strengthened (*guṇa*) before -ām.*

**Examples:**
*   **īkṣ** 1Ā ("to see"): *īkṣāṃ cakre / īkṣām āsa / īkṣāṃ babhūva*.
*   **bandh** (Causative: *bandhaya-*): *bandhayāṃ cakāra / bandhayām āsa / bandhayāṃ babhūva*.

## 36.3. Perfect Passive

In the Perfect, the passive is expressed using the **Ātmanepada** forms. However, the use of the **PPP** with a passive construction is often preferred.
Example: *tena puṇyaṃ cakre* = *tena puṇyaṃ kṛtam* ("By him a meritorious deed was done").

## 36.5. Exercise

A) Form both reduplicated and periphrastic perfects for: *bibhrate, bibhyati, juhoti, vetti*.

B) Form the periphrastic perfect for **ās** (to sit) in 3.sg. and 3.pl.

C) Form the corresponding Perfect for: *jīyate, vidanti, vindati, tyājayisyati, aikṣanta, agāpayat, apadyanta, cinoti (2 forms), hanti, prabhaviṣyanti, krāmanti, prāvrajan, tyakṣyati.*

D) Translate:
1. When the son was born, Sugata left (*tatyāja*) family and wealth and went forth (*pravavrāja*) from the home into homelessness. Having recognized (*prajñāya*) the noble truths by wisdom and having been freed from suffering by wisdom, he enlightened (*bodhayām āsa*) the noble people who wished to be freed—so say the monks (*bhikṣava āhuḥ*).
   ![](/images/lekt3605.jpg)
2. The Brahmins piled up (*cikyire*) the fire for the great sacrifice to the gods. While the Brahmins were praising the gods like Indra, the fire consumed the food of the sacrifice. Thus, through the sacrifice, the Brahmins pleased (*ādayāṃ cakruḥ*) the great gods and made them hear (*śrāvayāṃ babhūvuḥ*) the hymns.
3. The warriors who protected Dharma defeated (*vijigyuḥ*) the enemies who wished to conquer the great city, but they did not kill (*nā jaghnuḥ*) them.
4. The twice-born who has studied the Veda knows (*veda*) the Dharma of the twice-born.
5. It is not possible to live by knowledge alone. Those who know (*viduḥ*) this should not fail to study.
6. That holy man looked at (*īkṣāṃ cakre*) the wicked mind of greed and sin.

## 36.7. Reading Exercise

1. **Mahābhārata 13.7.25-26**:
   "By whom (one) pleases the father, by him Prajāpati is pleased. By whom (one) pleases the mother, by him the earth is honored. By whom (one) pleases the teacher, by him the Absolute (*brahma*) may be honored. All his Dharmas are respected, for whom these three are respected. But for whom these are not respected, all his actions are fruitless."
   ![](/images/lekt3607.jpg)

2. **Manusmṛti 4.154** (On conduct towards the elderly):
   "One should greet the elderly and give them one's own seat. One should sit near with hands joined (*kṛtāñjaliḥ*) and follow behind (*pṛṣṭhato 'nviyāt*) when they walk."

3. **Manusmṛti 2.98** (On the master of senses, *jitendriya*):
   "That man who, having heard, touched, seen, eaten, or smelled, neither rejoices nor fades away—he is to be known as a *jitendriya*."
