# Lesson 43

## 43.1. Personal Endings of the 2nd Person (*madhyamaḥ*)

| Type | Parasmaipada | Ātmanepada |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| | **Sing. / Plur.** | **Sing. / Plur.** |
| **Primary** | *-si / -tha* | *-se / -dhve* |
| **Secondary** | *-s / -ta* | *-thās / -dhvam* |
| **Perfect** | *-tha / -a* | *-se / -dhve* |

*Note: The secondary ending of the 2. pl. P. (-ta) is identical to the secondary ending of the 3. sg. Ā.*

## 43.2. Formation of 2nd Person Verbal Forms

The 2nd person refers to the person(s) being addressed:
*   **Singular**: "Thou" / "You"
*   **Plural**: "You all" (three or more)

## 43.3. Personal Pronouns of the 2nd Person

*   **tvad**: "Thou / You" (Singular)
*   **yuṣmad**: "You all" (Plural)

## 43.6. Dialogue (*saṃvādaḥ*)

**SUREŚA**: Aśoka, where are you going now?
**AŚOKA**: I am going to the market. I will return quickly after buying some wares.
**SUREŚA**: Is there any festival at your house today?
**AŚOKA**: Yes. Today is the birthday festival of my father (*pitṛpādānām*).
**SUREŚA**: What is your father's age?
**AŚOKA**: My father's age is sixty-five years. Today many relatives and friends will come to my house. You should come too.
**SUREŚA**: My dear, at what time will the people come?
**AŚOKA**: Why do you ask the time? Come by six o'clock (*ṣaḍvādanam*).
**SUREŚA**: What if I come by half-past six?
**AŚOKA**: No, you must definitely come at six o'clock exactly.
**SUREŚA**: Very well. Then you go; otherwise, there will be a delay.

*(Aśoka enters the shop, approaches the merchant and speaks)*
**AŚOKA**: O best of merchants, I have come to buy some wares. Give them to me quickly.
**MERCHANT**: My dear child, what do you wish to buy? In my shop, there are many excellent wares, and they are of small price. Tell me, how much of what do you wish to buy?
**AŚOKA**: At what price is a Seer (*serabhārā*) of sugar sold?
**MERCHANT**: The price is not excessive. I will give a Seer of sugar for only eight Annas (*aṣṭāṇakena*).
**AŚOKA**: Then weigh it quickly and give me one Seer's worth of sugar.
**MERCHANT**: *(Packing the sugar in paper)* Child, take the sugar. And give me the eight Annas. Give it quickly.
**AŚOKA**: *(Taking it in his hand and noticing its light weight)* Hey merchant! This does not look like a Seer of sugar. Weigh it again properly and then give it.
**MERCHANT**: *(Laughing)* What is this? It's of no consequence. Your trouble of carrying the weight will be less.
**AŚOKA**: *(Thinking for a moment and placing four Annas before him)* O merchant, take the price. I must go home quickly. *(Having said this, he departs from there)*
**MERCHANT**: Hey boy! Come, come! Listen for a moment! Where are you going after giving only a small price? Give four Annas more.
**AŚOKA**: Merchant! What is this? It's of no consequence. Your trouble of counting the Annas will be much less! *(Having said this, he runs away quickly)*

*(The cheating merchant in the shop was unable to do anything; there was only regret in his mind)*

Aśoka came home and told the whole story. All the relatives and friends there experienced great joy. Thus, the birthday festival ended happily.
