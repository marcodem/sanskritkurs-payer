### 49.1.2. Class 5 (*svādi*)
**Rule**: The 2nd person singular Parasmaipada is endungslos if the *-u* of the suffix is preceded by only one consonant.

**su (5U)**: *sunavāni / sunavāma* (1st P), **sunu** / *sunuta* (2nd P), *sunotu / sunvantu* (3rd P).
*Ātmanepada*: *sunavai / sunavāmahai* (1st), *sunuṣva / sunudhvam* (2nd), *sunutām / sunvatām* (3rd).

**āp (5P)**: *āpnavāni / āpnavāma* (1st), **āpnuhi** / *āpnutata* (2nd), *āpnotu / āpnuvantu* (3rd).

## 49.3. Primary Nominal Suffix *-as* (Neuter)

This suffix forms abstract or action nouns (*nomina actionis*). The root vowel usually takes High grade (*guṇa*).

**Examples:**
*   **man** 4Ā ("to think") → **manas** n. (thought, mind).
*   **nam** 1P ("to bow") → **namas** n. (bow, adoration).
    ![](/images/lekt4907.jpg)
*   **cit** 1P ("to perceive") → **cetas** n. (insight, consciousness).

## 49.6. Translation Exercise: From the *Kādambarī* of Bāṇa (7th century AD)

**King Śūdraka's questions to the parrot Vaiśampāyana:**

The king spoke: "Let all this be as it is for a moment. Remove our curiosity. Tell us from the beginning, in its entirety, your birth: In which country were you born? How were you born? Who gave you your name? Who is your mother, and who is your father? How did you attain the knowledge of the Vedas? How did you gain familiarity with the technical works (*śāstras*)? From where were the arts acquired? Is this a recollection of a former birth or a boon? Or are you someone in the guise of a bird living in hiding? Where did you live before? What is your age? How were you caged? And how did you fall into the hands of a Chandala? And how did you come here?"

Vaiśampāyana, having been asked with great respect by the king, himself moved by curiosity, contemplated for a moment and spoke respectfully: "Lord, such is my story. If there is curiosity, let it be heard."
