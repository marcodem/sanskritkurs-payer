# Exercise 19

## Exercise: Relative Clauses

Translate the following sentences into Sanskrit (solutions provided):

1. The Vaiśya woman, whose son has died, cries.
   **यस्या वैश्यायाः पुत्रो मृतः सा रोदिति ।**
2. Rāma sacrifices to the deity who protects him.
   **या देवता रामं रक्षति तां यजते ।**
3. The poet praises the Kṣatriya whose wealth he desires.
   **यस्य क्षत्रियस्य धनं लुभ्यति तं कविः स्तौति ।**
4. Fire burns the house of the man who does not worship Agni with a sacrifice.
   **यो नरो ऽग्निं न यजते तस्य गृहमग्निर्दहति ।**
5. The tiger-like man kills the Kṣatriya warriors who were conquered by Rāma (Passive).
   **यैः क्षत्रिययोधै रामो जितस्तान्पुरुषव्याघ्रो हन्ति ॥**

## Subhāṣitas

Translate and reflect on the following verses:

1. येन येन च वातेन / वारिदो वारि मुञ्चति । / तेन तेन च वातेन / छत्रं वहति पण्डितः ॥१॥
   "With whichever wind the cloud releases water, with that same wind the wise man carries his umbrella."
   (Meaning: One should adapt to the circumstances; follow the wind.)

2. सा भार्या या प्रियं ब्रूते / स पुत्रो यस्तु जीवति । / स जीवति गुणो यस्य / धर्मो यस्य स जीवति ॥३॥
   "She is a true wife who speaks kindly. He is a true son who lives [righteously]. He truly lives who has virtue. He truly lives who has Dharma."

3. यस्यार्थास्तस्य मित्राणि / यस्यार्थास्तस्य बान्धवाः । / यस्यार्थाः स पुमांल्लोके / यस्यार्थाः स हि पण्डितः ॥४॥
   "He who has wealth has friends. He who has wealth has relatives. He who has wealth is a [true] man in the world. He who has wealth is indeed a scholar."
