# Exercise 15

## Proverbs

Translate the proverb at the beginning of the lesson:

गुरुशुश्रूषया विद्या  
पुष्कलेन धनेन वा ।  
अथवा विद्यया विद्या  
चतुर्थी नैव विद्यते ॥

Knowledge is gained through service to a teacher, or for abundant wealth, or in exchange for knowledge. A fourth possibility does not exist.

## Exercise: Tatpuruṣa Compounds

Examine and dissolve the following Tatpuruṣa compounds. Provide the meaning in English.

1. **balakṛtaḥ** (बलेन कृतः): done by force, a violent act.
2. **bāladhanasya** (बालस्य धनस्य): of the property of a minor.
3. **narakākam** (काक इव नरम्): a man like a crow.
4. **lokaguroḥ** (लोकस्य गुरोः): of the teacher of the world.
5. **jaleśvareṇa** (जलस्येश्वरेण): by the Lord of the Waters (Varuṇa).
6. **janapānam** (जनानां पानम्): drink for the people.
7. **guṇavacannāni** (गुणानां वचनाni): words of quality (adjectives).
8. **mṛgeśvaraiḥ** (मृगाणामीश्वरैः): by the lords of the beasts (lions).
9. **buddhikṛtāyāḥ** (बुद्ध्या कृतायाः): of [the action] done with intellect (rational).
10. **yajñāṅgāni** (यज्ञस्य अङ्गानि): limbs/parts of the sacrifice (sacrificial implements).
11. **gṛhajanena** (गृहस्य जनेन): by the family (people of the house).
12. **grāmalekhakāḥ** (ग्रामाणं लेखकाः): the village clerks.
13. **nāgadevaḥ** (नागानां देवः): snake-god or elephant-king.
14. **satyavadanasya** (सत्यस्य वदनस्य): of the speaking of truth.
15. **svargamārgaḥ** (स्वर्गस्य मार्गः): the way to heaven.
16. **varṇadharmaḥ** (वर्णानां धर्मः): the law of the castes/ranks.
