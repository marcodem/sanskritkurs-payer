# Lesson 40

## 40.1. Wisdom Sayings (*subhāṣitāni*)

1.  *vidyā dadāti vinayaṃ / vinayād yāti pātratām |*
    *pātratvād dhanam āpnoti / dhanād dharmaṃ tataḥ sukham || 1 ||*
    "Knowledge gives discipline; from discipline one attains worthiness; from worthiness one obtains wealth; from wealth (comes) Dharma, and from that, happiness."

2.  *sukhārthī cet tyajed vidyāṃ / vidyārthī cet tyajet sukham |*
    *sukhārthinaḥ kuto vidyā / kuto vidyārthinaḥ sukham || 2 ||*
    "If one desires pleasure, he should give up knowledge; if one desires knowledge, he should give up pleasure. How can a pleasure-seeker have knowledge? How can a knowledge-seeker have pleasure?"

3.  *ācāryāt pādam ādatte / pādaṃ śiṣyaḥ svamedhayā |*
    *pādaṃ sabrahmacāribhyaḥ / pādaṃ kālakrameṇa ca || 3 ||*
    "A student receives a quarter (of knowledge) from the teacher, a quarter through his own intelligence, a quarter from his fellow students, and a quarter in the course of time."

## 40.2. First Person of the Perfect (*liṭ*)

**Endings:**
| | Parasmaipada | Ātmanepada |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Singular** | *-a* | *-e* |
| **Plural** | *-ma* | *-mahe* |

**Important Notes:**
1.  In most cases, the 1st person singular ending is identical to the 3rd person singular.
2.  **Types III and V**: 
    - The 1. sg. P can be either high-grade (*guṇa*) or extended-grade (*vṛddhi*), while the 3. sg. P must be extended-grade.
    - The 1. sg. Ā is always identical to the 3. sg. Ā.
3.  **Connecting Vowel *-i-***: Most roots take it before consonant endings. However, eight specific roots (and their derivatives) **never** take the connecting *-i-* (except before *-re*):
    - *kṛ, bhṛ, vṛ* ("choose"), *sṛ, dru* ("run"), *śru, stu, sru* ("flow").

### Examples by Type:
*   **Type I (No gradation)**: *bandh* → *babandha* (1.sg.P), *babandhima* (1.pl.P).
*   **Type II (Strong High, Weak Deep)**: *bhid* → *bibheda* (1.sg.P), *bibhidima* (1.pl.P).
*   **Type III (Reduplicated)**:
    - **kṛ**: *cakāra / cakara* (1.sg.P), *cakṛma* (1.pl.P).
    - **nī**: *nināya / ninaya* (1.sg.P), *ninyima* (1.pl.P).
*   **Type IV (Roots in -ā)**: *dā* → *dadau* (1.sg.P), *dadima* (1.pl.P).
*   **Type V (Consonant-a-Consonant)**:
    - **gam**: *jagāma / jagama* (1.sg.P), *jagmima* (1.pl.P).
    - **pac**: *papāca / papaca* (1.sg.P), *pecima* (1.pl.P).

## 40.3. First Person of the Periphrastic Perfect

Formed by adding the Perfect of *kṛ, as,* or *bhū* to the *-ām* stem.
*   **īkṣ** 1Ā: *īkṣāṃ cakre / īkṣām āsa / īkṣāṃ babhūva* (1.sg.Ā).
*   *īkṣāṃ cakṛmahe / īkṣām āsimha / īkṣāṃ babhūvima* (1.pl.Ā).

## 40.4. Syntax

Since the verb ending already indicates the person, the personal pronouns *aham* (I) and *vayam* (we) are often omitted unless emphasis is needed.

## 40.5. Personal Pronouns (*asmad*)

| Case | Singular ("I") | Plural ("We") |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nom.** | *aham* | *vayam* |
| **Acc.** | *mām (mā)* | *asmān (nas)* |
| **Instr.** | *mayā* | *asmābhiḥ* |
| **Dat.** | *mahyam (me)* | *asmabhyam (nas)* |
| **Abl.** | *mat* | *asmat* |
| **Gen.** | *mama (me)* | *asmākam (nas)* |
| **Loc.** | *mayi* | *asmāsu* |

*Note: Enclitic forms (in brackets) cannot start a sentence or be used before particles like ca, vā, eva.*

**As compound members:**
- Singular: **mad-** (e.g., *mad-pustakam*: my book).
- Plural: **asmad-** (e.g., *asmad-pustakāni*: our books).

## 40.6. Vocabulary

*   **pātra** n.: worthy person, master, vessel.
*   **medhā** f.: wisdom, intelligence, thought.
*   **pustaka** m.n.: manuscript, book.
*   **kḷp** 1Ā (*kalpate*, PPP *kḷpta*): to be in order, to suit (Lok.); to be shaped/formed.
    *   **vikalpa** m.: alternative, doubt.
*   **tud** 6U (*tudati*, PPP *tunna*): to strike, hit.
*   **tṛ** 1P (*tarati*, PPP *tīrṇa*): to cross, traverse; to be saved from.
    *   **tīrtha** n.: ford, holy bathing place, pilgrimage site.
    ![](/images/lekt4003.jpg)
    *   **tīrthaṅkara** m.: "ford-maker" (one of the 24 Jain teachers).
    ![](/images/lekt4002.jpg)
*   **ava-** (Preverb): down, away.
    *   **avatāra** m.: "down-coming," incarnation of a god (especially Vishnu's 10 avatars).
    ![](/images/lekt4001.jpg)
*   **svap** 2P (*svapiti*, Perf. *suṣvāpa*, PPP *supta*): to sleep.
    *   **svapna** m.: sleep, dream.
    *   **supti** f.: deep sleep.
    ![](/images/lekt4004.jpg)
