# Lesson 56

## 56.1. The Reduplicated Aorist

**Formation**: Augment + Reduplicated Root + -a- + Thematic Secondary Endings.

The stem syllable can be in its weak or high grade. For roots ending in *-i* or *-u*, the weak grade is the rule (replacing *-i/-u* with *-iy/-uv*). Roots in *-ā* (representing *-e*) drop the final vowel.

### Paradigm: shri (1U, "to resort to, to lean on")
| Person | Parasmaipada | Ātmanepada |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| | **Sing. / Plur.** | **Sing. / Plur.** |
| **1st** | *ashishriyam / ashishriyāma* | *ashishriye / ashishriyāmahai* |
| **2nd** | *ashishriyas / ashishriyata* | *ashishriyathās / ashishriyadhvam* |
| **3rd** | *ashishriyat / ashishriyan* | *ashishriyata / ashishriyanta* |

**Common roots using this Aorist:**
*   **dru** (1P, "to run"): *adudruvat*.
*   **pat** (1P, "to fall"): **apaptat** (redupl. *pa-pt*).
*   **vac** (2P, "to say"): **avocat** (represented as *a-va-ucat*).
*   **Mandatory** for all Class 10 roots and all Causatives.

### 56.1.1. Reduplicated Aorist for Class 10 and Causatives
The formation follows complex rules to ensure an alternating rhythm (short-long-short):
1.  **Vowel Changes in the Stem**: Long vowels are shortened (*ā → a*, *ī/e/ai → i*, *u/o/au → u*).
2.  **Vowel Changes in the Reduplication**:
    *   **i** or **u** become **ī** or **ū** if the stem is short (e.g., *bhid* → *abībhidat*; *tud* → *atūtudat*).
    *   **a** often becomes **ī** (e.g., *kṛ* → *acīkarat*; *bhū* → *abībhavat*).
3.  **Vowel-Initial Roots**: Use "Attic Reduplication" (e.g., *ash* → *ashishat*; *arha* → *ārjihat*).

## 56.2. The Injunctive

The Injunctive refers to augmentless forms of the Imperfect or Aorist. While not recognized as a separate category by traditional Indian grammarians, scholars like **Karl Hoffmann** identified its distinct functions in the Veda (asive/timeless mention).

In classical/post-Vedic Sanskrit, the Injunctive survives almost exclusively in **prohibitive sentences** with the particle **mā**.

## 56.3. Prohibitive Sentences

**Main Form**: **mā** + Injunctive Aorist (or **mā sma** + Imperfect/Aorist).

**Examples:**
*   **mā karmahetur bhūḥ**: "Do not be motivated by the fruits of action!" (Inj. Aor.) — *Bhagavadgītā* 2,47.
*   **klaibyaṃ mā sma gamaḥ**: "Do not yield to unmanliness!" (Inj. Aor.) — *Bhagavadgītā* 2,3.
![](/images/lekt5602.jpg)

**Other forms of prohibition:**
*   **mā** + Imperative (rare).
*   **alam** or **kṛtam** + Instrumental: "Enough/Enough with...!" (e.g., *alaṃ viṣādena* "Enough with despondency!" / "Cheer up!"). ![](/images/lekt5603.jpg)
*   **na** + Gerundive (Participle of Necessity).

## 56.4. sma

1.  **Emphasis**: "Truly, certainly, indeed."
2.  **Narrative Past**: **sma** + Present Indicative expresses the past (especially habitual or continuous action).
    *   *vasati sma*: "He used to live," "He once lived."
