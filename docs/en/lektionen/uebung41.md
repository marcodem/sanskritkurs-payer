# Exercise 41

A) Translate the two proverbs at the beginning of the lesson.

> पुस्तकस्था च या विद्या  
> परहस्ते च यद्धनम् ।  
> कार्यकाले समुत्पन्ने  
> न सा विद्या न तद्धनम् ॥१॥
> 
> Knowledge that stays only in books, and wealth that is in another's hand: when the time of need arises, that is neither knowledge nor wealth.

> उपदेशो हि मूर्खाणां  
> प्रकोपाय न शान्तये ।  
> पयःपानं भुजङ्गानां  
> केवलं विषवर्धनम् ॥२॥
> 
> Advice given to fools leads to anger, not to peace. Giving milk to snakes only increases their poison.

B) Translate:

बुद्धम् शरणं गच्छामि धर्मं शरणं गच्छामि सङ्घं शरणं गच्छामीति बुद्धगतैर्वक्तव्यम् ॥१॥

One who has gone to the Buddha should say: "I go for refuge to the Buddha, I go for refuge to the Dharma, I go for refuge to the Sangha."

> This triple refuge is äußerst important to have the necessary motivation, energy, and endurance on the path to liberation.
> 
> There were and are an unmanageable number of teachers of wisdom and founders of religions. It is impossible to follow everyone individually to test what their teachings are worth. So one must make a selection. The personality of such a teacher must appear particularly trustworthy, so that one can and wants to get involved with him. This trust, this faith, is the content of the first refuge, the refuge in the Buddha.
> 
> The essential thing for Buddhists, however, is not a savior personality in the "fact of his coming"1, but the essential thing for Buddhists is the teaching of liberation, which enables the individual to go the path to liberation himself and thus to realize liberation himself. To do this, one must be tentatively – i.e., as long as one does not yet know from one's own experience that it leads to the goal – so convinced of this teaching that one musters the energy to test this teaching for its truth content. This is the content of the second refuge, the refuge in the teaching of the Buddha or the Dharma/Dhamma, as this teaching is called in Sanskrit or Pāli.
> 
> > 1 Rudolf Bultmann (1884-1976): "The only thing that matters is the *fact* of Jesus' coming, not the *what*, that is, not the historically verifiable data of his life and work."
> 
> Even the best teacher and the best teaching are useless for me if they are not understandable for a normal mortal. Therefore, it is important to be convinced that others have also made it on this path. This is the content of the third refuge, the refuge in the community of those who have attained liberation on the Buddha's path, in Sanskrit or Pāli the Saṅgha.

काशीं पत्स्ये गङ्गां द्रक्ष्यामि तत्र च मरिष्यामीति मन्यमानो मान्यो वृद्धनरः पुत्रांश्च पुत्रपुत्रांश्च धनं च तत्याज काशीं च प्राव्रजत् । एवं च रोध्यं दुःखं तरिष्यतीति मन्ये ॥२॥

> "I will go to Kashi, I will see the Ganges, and I will die there." With this thought, the venerable old man left his sons, his sons' sons, and his wealth, and went forth to Kashi. I think that he will thus overcome the suffering that is to be ended.

कन्यां व्युवह तस्यां च पुत्रमजनयं महाधनं च लेभ एवं सुखमापेत्यतीते मुमोह । ततः प्रजज्ञौ सुखाद्दुःखं जायते तस्माल्लोकसुखमपि त्यजनीयं न च किंचिदिन्द्रियैः स्प्रष्टव्यमिति ॥३॥

> In the past I was deluded that I had attained happiness, because I had married a girl, begot a son with her, and acquired great wealth. Then I recognized that from happiness suffering arises and therefore one should also give up the happiness of the world and touch nothing with one's senses.

विक्रेयाणि विक्रीयापुत्रवैश्यो भिक्षुभ्यो विक्रयफलमददाद्दानपुण्यं चादत्त । एतत्कर्म स्tuत्यमिति भिक्षवः प्रोचुर्बुद्धिमन्तस्तु विकल्पयन्ति किमेवं कुर्वाणो वश्यः पुण्यं चकारेति ॥४॥

> The childless Vaiśya sold goods, gave the proceeds of the sale to the monks, and received the merit of his generosity. The monks proclaimed that this deed is praiseworthy, but the wise doubt whether the Vaiśya acting thus performed a meritorious deed.

गुरुभिः शिष्याः शासितव्याः शिष्यैरध्ययनमध्येतव्यम् ॥५॥

> Teachers must instruct the students, students must learn their lessons.
