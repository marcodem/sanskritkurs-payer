# Lesson 20

## 20.1. Possessive Compounds = Bahuvrīhi m. = बहुव्रीहि

![](/images/lekt2004.jpg)  
Fig.: bahuvrīhiḥ puruṣaḥ (a man with "much rice")  
Jodhpur.  
[Image source: Jared Zimmerman. -- http://www.flickr.com/photos/spoinknet/35414570/. -- Accessed 2008-12-11. -- Creative Commons License (Attribution, Non-Commercial, No Derivatives)]

bahuvrīhiḥ = bahavo vrīhayo yasya saḥ = "one who possesses much rice"

A Bahuvrīhi, unlike a Tatpuruṣa, designates something other than what is expressed by its individual members. While *bāla-putraḥ* as a Tatpuruṣa means "a young son" (i.e., something expressed by the final member), *bāla-putraḥ* as a Bahuvrīhi means "one whose son is young." Thus, the designated person is neither the son (*putra*) nor necessarily young (*bāla*), but someone distinct from the young son – namely, his father.

Possessive compounds are initially always adjectives, though they can be substantivized. Therefore, the grammatical gender of a Bahuvrīhi depends on what it refers to, and not on the gender of the last member of the compound.

Schema for the resolution (*vigraha*) of a Bahuvrīhi:

1st member usually in the Nominative (*prathamā*) sing., dual, or plural – 2nd member in the Nominative sing., dual, or plural – Relativ pronoun in a case other than the Nominative (usually Genitive - *ṣaṣṭhī*) and in the gender and number of the whole Bahuvrīhi – Demonstrative pronoun in the number, case, and gender of the whole Bahuvrīhi.

Examples:

> gata-pāpaḥ = gataṃ pāpaṃ yasya saḥ = "one whose evil has gone" = "one who is free from misdeeds"
> 
> > Akk. sg. gata-pāpam = gataṃ pāpaṃ yasya tam
> > 
> > Inst. sg. gata-pāpena = gataṃ pāpaṃ yasya tena
> > 
> > Gen. sg. gata-pāpasya = gataṃ pāpaṃ yasya tasya
> > 
> > Nom. pl. gata-pāpāḥ = gatāni pāpāni yeṣāṃ te
> > 
> > etc.
> 
> asta-mohā = asto moho yayā sā = "a (woman) who has cast off delusion"
> 
> prāptodako grāmaḥ = prāptam udakaṃ yaṃ sa grāmaḥ = "A village to which the water has come" = "a village threatened by flood"
> 
> puṇyavat-putraḥ (as a Bahuvrīhi) = puṇyavān putro yasya saḥ, or: puṇyavantaḥ putrā yasya saḥ = "one whose son possesses merit" or "one whose sons possess merit."

The relationship between the initial member and the final member in a Bahuvrīhi can be:

*   Attributive
*   Appositional
*   Case-related
*   Adverbial

As with the Tatpuruṣa, the modified member usually stands in the second position.

Traditionally, we distinguish:

*   samānādhikaraṇa-bahuvrīhiḥ: in the resolution sentence (*vigraha-vākya*), the initial member and final member stand in the same case.
*   vyadhikaraṇa-bahuvrīhiḥ: in the *vigraha-vākya*, the initial member and final member stand in different cases.

## 20.3. Bahuvrīhi with an Appositional Initial Member

Schema:

Substantive -- Substantive

Examples:

> śūra-putro naraḥ = śūrā eva putrā yasya sa naraḥ = "a man whose sons are heroes"
> 
> tad-anta 3 = so 'nto yasya saḥ / yasyāḥ sā / yasya tat = "that which has this as its end" = "ending with that"

| Very important are appositional Bahuvrīhis with final members like ādi, ādika, ādya "first, beginning," or more rarely prabhṛti "beginning." Such compounds correspond to "etc.":
|
| Example:
|
| > devā indrādayaḥ = indra ādir yeṣāṃ te devāḥ = "The gods whose beginning is Indra" = "the gods Indra etc." = "Indra and the other gods"

## 20.4. Bahuvrīhi with a Case-related Initial Member

This refers to Bahuvrīhis whose initial member represents a case other than the Nominative (*prathamā*).

Example:

> deva-rūpā = devasyaiva rūpaṃ yasyāḥ sā = "a (woman) whose form is that of a god" = "a woman of divine appearance"

Exception to the general rule regarding the order of members:

The case-related member stands in second position when it designates a part of the body (especially the hand):

Example:

> daṇḍa-hastaḥ = daṇḍo haste (Lok. sg.) yasya saḥ = "one in whose hand is a staff" = "one who carries a staff in his hand."

## 20.5. Bahuvrīhi with an Adverbial Initial Member

The initial member consists of an adverb, a preposition, a preverb, or another indeclinable; such compounds are usually *nitya-samāsa*.

Examples:

> a-putro naraḥ = putro yasya nāsti sa naraḥ = "a man who has no son" = "a sonless man"
> 
> dur-bala 3 = "one whose strength is bad" = "powerless, weak"

*saha* "with" as the initial member of a Bahuvrīhi is often replaced by *sa*.

Example:

> sa-putraḥ = saha-putraḥ = putreṇa sahitaḥ / putreṇa saha = "with a son," "accompanied by a son"

## 20.6. Inflection of the Final Member of a Bahuvrīhi

The final member of a Bahuvrīhi is inflected as a multi-gender adjective, independent of its original gender. Thus, if the final member of a compound has a gender other than its original one, it must be a Bahuvrīhi (unless it is a *samāhāra-dvandva* [neuter sing.]).

In the process of adjectivization:

*   -ā stems become -a stems in the masculine and neuter.
*   -a stems (m., n.) form their feminine in -ā or -ī.
*   -ī stems (f.) must add the suffix -ka, -kā, -ka (n.) at the end of Bahuvrīhis; many other Bahuvrīhis may or must also take this suffix.

## 20.7. Another Classification of Bahuvrīhi Types

The examples below are provided for later study; for some, the necessary grammar and vocabulary have not yet been discussed.

1. Qualifying word as initial member – qualified word as final member: sthira-cittaḥ = sthiraṃ cittaṃ yasya saḥ
2. Indeclinable as initial member: uccaiḥ-śravas
3. Comparison as initial member: kuśāgra-dhīḥ = kuśāgra iva dhīr yasya saḥ
4. Implicit comparison: uṣṭra-mukhaḥ = uṣṭrasya mukham iva mukhaṃ yasya saḥ = "camel-mouthed" = whose mouth is like that of a camel
5. Numeral as initial member for approximate numbers: upa-daśāḥ = daśānāṃ samīpe ye santi te = "approximately ten"
6. Both members are numerals: eka-dvāḥ = eko vā dvau vā = "one or two"
7. With sa-/saha- as initial member: sa-putraḥ / saha-putraḥ
8. With sa- (= samāna) as initial member: sa-janapadaḥ = samāno janapado yasya saḥ
9. nañ-bahuvrīhiḥ = with a-/an- as initial member: a-putraḥ = na vidyate putro yasya saḥ; an-aṅgaḥ = na vidyate aṅgaṃ yasya saḥ
10. With preposition etc. as initial member: pra-vātaḥ = prakṛṣṭo vāto yasmin saḥ; nir-dhanaḥ = nirgataṃ dhanaṃ yasmāt saḥ; su-buddhiḥ = suṣṭhu buddhir yasya saḥ; dur-buddhiḥ = duṣṭā buddhir yasya saḥ
11. Directional designations to indicate intermediate directions: purvottarā "northeast"
12. PPP as initial member: kṛta-kaṭaḥ = kṛtaḥ kaṭo yena saḥ
13. and others

## 20.8. Relationship between Bahuvrīhi and Relative Clause

Although every Bahuvrīhi can be resolved by a relative clause, not every relative clause can be replaced by a Bahuvrīhi. Hermann Georg Jacobi (1850 - 1937), who investigated this relationship (Compositum und Nebensatz, 1897), writes:

> "When translating from German [or another modern language] into Sanskrit, not all subordinate clauses may be rendered by compounds, but only those which are decorative and descriptive; those, however, which contain a conceptually necessary or important definition, also appear in Sanskrit as relative clauses."

![](/images/lekt2002.jpg)  
Fig.: Hermann Jacobi.  
[Image source: Beiträge zur Literaturwissenschaft und Geistesgeschichte Indiens. -- Bonn, 1926]

This may be true. However, Indians then view many things as decorative and descriptive which we would consider "conceptually necessary or important."
