## 54.2. Word Formation: *kṛt* Suffix *-u* on Desiderative Stems

The suffix **-u** is added to Desiderative stems (expressing the wish to do something or being about to do something) to form *Nomina Agentis* (agent nouns).

**Example:**
*   **yuyutsu** (from *yu-yudh + s + u*): "desirous of fighting," "about to fight."

## 54.3. Metrics II: Epic *Triṣṭubh* and *Jagatī*

The **Triṣṭubh** ("Three-shouter") often appears in the epics in the middle of *Śloka* sections, specifically where the mood or action reaches a special climax or conclusion.

### Triṣṭubh
A *Triṣṭubh* stanza consists of four lines (*pādas*) of **11 syllables** each. There are two main schemes based on the position of the caesura (word break):
1.  Caesura after the 4th syllable: **̽ ˉ ̽ ˉ / ̽ ˘ ˉ ˉ ˘ ˉ ˉ**
2.  Caesura after the 5th syllable: **̽ ˉ ̽ ˉ ̽ / ̽ ˘ ˉ ˘ ˉ ˉ**

### Jagatī
The **Jagatī** is the 12-syllable equivalent of the *Triṣṭubh*. The final 5 syllables of each *pāda* follow this quantity: **ˉ ˘ ˉ ˘ ˉ**.

### Classical Formations:
In later classical literature, these forms became strictly fixed:
*   **Indravajrā**: **ˉ ˉ ˘ ˉ ˉ ˘ ˘ ˉ ˘ ˉ ˉ**
*   **Upendravajrā**: **˘ ˉ ˘ ˉ ˉ ˘ ˘ ˉ ˘ ˉ ˉ** (like *Indravajrā* but starting with a light syllable).
*   **Upajāti**: A mixture of *Indravajrā* and *Upendravajrā* lines in one stanza.

## 54.5. The Aorist (*luṅ*)

The Aorist (*luṅ*) describes an action that has just taken place. All Aorists take the **Augment a-**, follow the same rules as the Imperfect (*laṅ*), and use **secondary endings**.

### Types of Aorist:
1.  **Asigmatic (Non-sibilant):**
    *   Root Aorist
    *   Thematic Aorist
    *   Reduplicated Aorist
2.  **Sigmatic (Sibilant):** Contains an *-s-* or a syllable with *-s-* (*-ṣ-*) before the ending.
    *   *-s-* Aorist
    *   *-iṣ-* Aorist
    *   *-siṣ-* Aorist
    *   *-sa-* Aorist

## 54.6. Root Aorist (*Wurzelaorist*)

**Formation**: Augment + Root + Secondary Endings.
The 3rd person plural Parasmaipada ending is **-ur**. The Ātmanepada is generally not used.

**Example: pā (1P, "to drink")**
| Person | Singular | Plural |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1st** | *apām* | *apāma* |
| **2nd** | *apās* | *apāta* |
| **3rd** | *apāt* | **apur** (weak grade) |

This type is formed only from 12 roots, including *dā* (to give), *dhā* (to set), *sthā* (to stand), and *bhū* (to be).

**Conjugation of bhū (1P, "to be"):**
| Person | Singular | Plural |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1st** | *abhūvam* | *abhūma* |
| **2nd** | *abhūḥ* | *abhūta* |
| **3rd** | *abhūt* | **abhūvan** |

### 54.6.1. Passive Aorist (3rd Person Singular)
A special root-aorist form used for the **3rd per. sg. Passive**. It can be formed from all roots using the ending **-i**.

**Rules for the Root:**
*   **High Grade (Guṇa)**: Roots with a short vowel (except *a*) before a single consonant (e.g., *bhid* → **abhedi** "it was split").
*   **Long Grade (Vṛddhi)**:
    *   Roots ending in a vowel (e.g., *kṛ* → **akāri** "it was made").
    *   Roots with *a* before a single consonant (e.g., *vad* → **avādi** "it was said").
*   **Augmented with -y-**: Roots ending in *-ā, -e, -ai, -o* add *-y-* before *-i* (e.g., *dā* → **adāyi** "it was given").
