# Lesson 48

## 48.1. Wisdom Sayings (*subhāṣitāni*)

1.  *satyam vada* || 1 || (Speak the truth.)
2.  *dharmam cara* || 2 || (Practice righteousness.)
3.  *mātṛdevo bhava* || 3 || (Be one to whom the mother is a god.)
4.  *gauravam prāpyate dānāt* || 4 || (Dignity is obtained through giving.)
5.  *śvaḥ kāryam adya kurvīta* || 5 || (One should do today the work of tomorrow.)
6.  *vidyāvihīnaḥ paśuḥ* || 6 || (One devoid of knowledge is a beast.)
7.  *lāghavam vaiyākaraṇasya bhūṣaṇam* || 7 || (Conciseness is the ornament of a grammarian.)

## 48.2. Formation of the Imperative (*loṭ*) for Athematic Stems

### 48.2.1. Endings for Athematic Stems

| Person | Parasmaipada | Ātmanepada |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| | **Sing. / Plur.** | **Sing. / Plur.** |
| **1st (*uttamaḥ*)** | *-āni / -āma* | *-ai / -āmahai* |
| **2nd (*madhyamaḥ*)** | **-dhi / -hi / -ø / -āna** / *-ta* | *-sva / -dhvam* |
| **3rd (*prathamaḥ*)** | *-tu* / *-antu* (Cl. 3: *-atu*) | *-tām / -atām* |

#### Rules for the 2nd Person Singular Parasmaipada Ending:
| Ending | Condition |
| :--- | :--- |
| **-ø** | Roots of Classes 5 and 8 where the final *-u* is preceded by only one consonant. |
| **-āna** | Consonant-ending roots of Class 9 (substitutes for *-nī* + ending). |
| **-hi** | All other present stems ending in a vowel or semivowel (except *juhudhi*). |
| **-dhi** | All other cases (consonant-ending stems). |

### 48.2.2. Stem Gradation
*   **Strong Stem**: All 1st person forms and the 3rd person singular Parasmaipada.
*   **Weak Stem**: All other forms.

### 48.2.3. Examples: Class 2 (*adādi*)

**dviṣ (2U)**
*   *laṭ*: *dveṣāni / dveṣāma* (1st P), *dviḍḍhi / dviṣṭa* (2nd P), *dveṣṭu / dviṣantu* (3rd P).
*   *Ātmanepada*: *dveṣai / dveṣāmahai* (1st), *dvikṣva / dviḍḍhvam* (2nd), *dviṣṭām / dviṣatām* (3rd).

**ās (2Ā)**: *āsai / āsamahai* (1st), *āssva / ādhvam* (2nd), *āstām / āsatām* (3rd).

**duh (2U)**: *dohāni / duhāma* (1st P), *dugdhi / dugdha* (2nd P), *dogdhu / duhantu* (3rd P).

**i (2P)**: *ayāni / ayāma* (1st), *ihi / ita* (2nd), *etu / yantu* (3rd).

**śī (2Ā)**: *śayai / śayāmahai* (1st), *śeṣva / śedhvam* (2nd), *śetām / śeratām* (3rd). (Always uses High grade).

**han (2P)**: *hanāni / hanāma* (1st), **jahi** / *hata* (2nd), *hantu / ghnantu* (3rd).

**as (2P)**: *asāni / asāma* (1st), **edhi** / *sta* (2nd), *astu / santu* (3rd).

**śās (2P)**: *śāsāni / śāsāma* (1st), **śādhi** / *śiṣṭa* (2nd), *śāstu / śāsatu* (3rd).

### 48.2.4. Examples: Class 3 (*juhotyādi*)
Note: The 3rd plural Parasmaipada ends in **-atu**.

**hu (3P)**: *juhavāni / juhavāma* (1st), **juhudhi** / *juhuta* (2nd), *juhotu / juhvatu* (3rd).

**dhā (3U)**: *dadhāni / dadhāma* (1st P), **dhehi** / *dhatta* (2nd P), *dadhātu / dadhatu* (3rd P).

## 48.3. Secondary Derivatives (*taddhita*): Suffixes *-a* and *-ya*

The suffixes **-a** and **-ya** derive new nouns from existing ones. The key characteristic is the **Vṛddhi** (extended grade) of the first syllable of the base word.

**Meaning**: Expresses a relationship ("descended from," "belonging to," "relating to"). Often used for patronymics (names derived from fathers) or abstract nouns.

**Examples:**
| Base Word | Derivative | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| *śuci* ("pure") | **śauca** n. | purity |
| *putra* ("son") | **pautra** m. | grandson |
| *brahman* n. ("Veda") | **brāhmaṇa** m. | Brahmin (knower of Veda) |
| *rājan* m. ("king") | **rājya** n. | kingdom |
| *deva* m. ("god") | **daivya** 3 | divine |
| *guru* 3 ("heavy") | **gaurava** n. | dignity, weight |
| *vyāghra* m. ("tiger") | **vaiyāghra** 3 | relating to a tiger |

## 48.6. Translation: *Manusmṛti* 9 (The Duties of Women)

*asvatantrāḥ striyaḥ kāryāḥ puruṣaiḥ svair divāniśam |*
*viṣayeṣu ca sajjantyāḥ saṃsthāpyā ātmano vaśe || 2 ||*
(Women must be kept in a state of dependence by their men day and night; they should be kept under control when they cling to sensory objects.)

*pitā rakṣati kaumare bhartā rakṣati yauvane |*
*rakṣanti sthavire putrā na strī svātantryam arhati || 3 ||*
(The father protects her in childhood, the husband protects her in youth, and the sons protect her in old age; a woman is never fit for independence.)
