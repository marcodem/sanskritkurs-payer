# Lesson 51

## 51.1. *Upapada* Compounds

*Upapada* compounds (from *upapada* n., "supplementary word") are *Tatpuruṣas* where the final member is a verbal noun that occurs only in compounds and never as a standalone word. These verbal nouns are formed with the *kṛt* suffixes:
*   **-ø** (zero-suffix)
*   **-a**
*   **-t**

They function as *Nomina Agentis* (agent nouns), indicating the doer of the action expressed by the underlying root.

### Examples of Resolutions:
*   *sukhakaraḥ* = *sukhaṃ karotīti sukhakaraḥ* (One who causes happiness).
*   *kulaghnaḥ* = *kulaṃ hantīti kulaghnaḥ* (One who destroys a family).

### Examples by Suffix:

**a) Zero-suffix (-ø)**
*   **-nī** 3 ("leading"): e.g., **senānī** m. (army leader).
*   **-bhuj** 3 ("enjoying, eating"): e.g., **bhūmibhuj** m. (king, "enjoyer of the earth").
*   **-vid** 3 ("knowing"): e.g., **dharmavid** 3 (knower of Dharma).
![](/images/lekt5101.jpg)
Fig.: *bhūmibhuj* (King)

**b) Suffix -t**
*   **-kṛt** 3 ("making"): e.g., **pāpakṛt** 3 (evil-doer).
*   **-jit** 3 ("conquering"): e.g., **śatrujit** 3 (conqueror of enemies).
*   **-bhṛt** 3 ("bearing"): e.g., **bhūmibhṛt** m. (king, "bearer/protector of the earth").
![](/images/lekt5103.jpg)
Fig.: *pāpakṛt* (Evil-doer)

**c) Suffix -a**
*   **-ga** 3 ("going"): e.g., **khaga** m. (bird, "moving in the sky").
*   **-ghna** 3 ("killing"): e.g., **kulaghna** 3 (destroyer of a family).
*   **-ja** 3 ("born in/from"): e.g., **atmaja** m. (son).
*   **-jña** 3 ("knowing"): e.g., **sarvajña** 3 (all-knowing).
*   **-da** 3 ("giving"): e.g., **vārida** m. (cloud, "water-giver").
*   **-pa** 3 ("drinking" or "protecting"): e.g., **dvipa** m. (elephant, "drinking twice"); **bhūpa** m. (king).
*   **-stha** 3 ("standing/being in"): e.g., **gṛhastha** m. (householder).
*   **-kara** 3 ("doing/causing"): e.g., **sukhakara** 3 (causing happiness).
*   **-smara** 3 ("remembering"): e.g., **jātismara** 3 (remembering former births).
![](/images/lekt5102.jpg)
Fig.: *dvipo dvir pibati* (The elephant drinks twice: with trunk and mouth).

## 51.2. Declension of Single-Consonant Stems

Stems ending in a single consonant (other than nasals, semivowels, or *-s*) have no stem gradation. They are declined by adding regular case endings.

**Irregularity**: In the Nom./Acc./Voc. plural of the **Neuter**, a nasal is inserted before the final consonant (e.g., *śatrujinti*).

### 51.2.1. Stems on Non-Palatal Stops
The final consonant follows regular Sandhi laws:
*   Final: voiceless non-aspirate (e.g., *-t*).
*   Before *-su* (Loc. pl.): voiceless non-aspirate.
*   Before voiced consonants: voiced non-aspirate (e.g., *-d*).

**Example: śatrujit 3 ("conqueror of enemies")**
*   **Masc./Fem.**: Nom. *śatrujit*, Akk. *śatrujitam*, Instr. pl. *śatrujidbhis*, Loc. pl. *śatrujitsu*.
*   **Neuter**: Nom. sg. *śatrujit*, Nom. pl. **śatrujinti**.

### 51.2.2. Stems on Palatal Stops (c, ch, j), ś, and ṣ
*   **-c** → **-k**
*   **-ch** → **-ṭ**
*   **-j** → **-k** or **-ṭ**
*   **-ś** → **-ṭ** or **-k**
*   **-ṣ** → **-ṭ** or **-k**

**Examples:**
*   **satyavāc** 3 ("truth-speaking"): Nom. **satyavāk**, Instr. pl. **satyavāgbhis**, Loc. pl. **satyavākṣu**. Neuter pl.: **satyavāñci**.
*   **śeṣabhuj** 3 ("eating remains"): Nom. **śeṣabhuk**, Instr. pl. **śeṣabhugbhis**. Neuter pl.: **śeṣabhuñji**.
*   **parivrāj** m. ("wandering monk"): Nom. **parivrāṭ**, Instr. pl. **parivrāḍbhis**, Loc. pl. **parivrāṭsu**.
![](/images/lekt5104.jpg)

### 51.2.3. Stems on -h
*   Normally *-h* → **-ḍh** (and then to *-ṭ/-ḍ*).
*   If the root begins with *d-*, *-h* → **-gh** (and then to *-k/-g*).

**Examples:**
*   **guh** 3 ("hiding"): Nom. **ghuṭ**, Instr. pl. **ghuḍbhis**. (Note Grassmann's Law).
*   **druh** 3 ("harming"): Nom. **dhruṭ** OR **dhruk**.

## 51.3. Sandhi of Initial *h-*
After a stop, *h-* becomes the voiced aspirate of that stop:
*   *tat + hi* → **taddhi**
*   *vāk + hi* → **vāgghi**
*   *parivrāṭ + hi* → **parivrāḍḍhi**

## 51.4. Sandhi of Initial *ch-*
After a short vowel, *mā*, or *ā*, *ch-* becomes **cch-**:
*   *na + chindati* → **na cchindati**

## 51.5. Internal *-ch-*
Within a word, *-ch-* is replaced by **-cch-** after all vowels:
*   *chid* → *ciccheda*

## 51.7. Exercise

1. **Duties of the Four Stages of Life (*Arthaśāstra* 1, 3, 9-12):**
   *   **Householder (*gṛhastha*):** Living by one's legitimate profession, marrying within comparable/different families, visiting the wife in the *ṛtu*, worshiping gods/ancestors/guests, giving to servants, and eating the remains.
   *   **Student (*brahmacārin*):** Study of the Veda, fire-rituals, ablutions, living on alms, and lifelong service to the teacher (or the teacher's son/fellow student).
   *   **Forest-dweller (*vānaprastha*):** Celibacy, sleeping on the ground, wearing matted hair and antelope skin, fire-offering, ablutions, worshiping gods/ancestors/guests, and forest-food.
   *   **Ascetic (*pravrājaka*):** Control of senses, cessation of work, poverty, abandonment of attachments, living on alms, wandering in the forest, and internal/external purity.

2. **Necessity of *Varṇāśramadharma* (*Arthaśāstra* 1, 3, 16-17):**
   *   Therefore, the king should not let beings stray from their own duty (*svadharma*). He who upholds his *svadharma* rejoices here and hereafter.
   *   The world, with its Aryan boundaries established and its system of classes and stages maintained, protected by the three Vedas, prospers and does not perish.

3. **Reflections of the Parrot Vaiśampāyana from Bāṇa's *Kādambarī*:**
   *   "Alas, the life of these [hunters] is full of delusion and their conduct is despised by good people. For them: their idea of Dharma is making sacrifices of human flesh; their food is meat and wine; their exertion is hunting; their scripture is the jackal's howl; their teachers are owls; their wisdom is bird-knowledge; their friends are dogs; their kingdom is the empty forest; their festival is a drinking bout; their weapons are snakes; their song is the destruction of innocent deer; their wives are captive women; their life is theft; their ornaments are snake-gems; their ointment is elephant-musth. While I was thinking this, the leader of the Shabaras sat down."
