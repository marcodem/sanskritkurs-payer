# Script Exercise 1

## Introduction to Devanāgarī

Sanskrit is most commonly written in the **Devanāgarī** script ("city of the gods"). It is an abugida, meaning that each consonant carries an inherent vowel (usually 'a').

### Vowels (Svara)

Vowels in Sanskrit can be short or long. In the middle of a word, they are written as diacritics (Mātrās) attached to consonants. At the beginning of a word, they take an independent form.

![](/images/schrift001.jpg)

### The Inherent Vowel 'a'

Every consonant in Devanāgarī is assumed to be followed by the vowel 'a' unless marked otherwise. To indicate a consonant without a vowel, a small stroke called **Virāma** (्) is added below.
