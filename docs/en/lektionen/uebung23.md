## Exercise: Infinitives

Form the Infinitive (*-tum*) for the following roots.

### A) Without connecting vowel '-i-'
1. **āp** -> *āptum* (to obtain)
2. **i** -> *etum* (to go)
3. **gam** -> *gantum* (to go)
4. **kṛ** -> *kartum* (to do)
5. **ji** -> *jetum* (to conquer)
6. **dah** -> *dagdhum* (to burn)
7. **nī** -> *netum* (to lead)
8. **pā** (1) -> *pātum* (to drink)
9. **pac** -> *paktum* (to cook)
10. **śru** -> *śrotum* (to hear)
11. **han** -> *hantum* (to kill)
12. **labh** -> *labdhum* (to obtain)

### B) With connecting vowel '-i-'
1. **ās** -> *āsitum* (to sit)
2. **nṛt** -> *nartitum* (to dance)
3. **rakṣ** -> *rakṣitum* (to protect)
4. **vad** -> *vaditum* (to speak)

## Sentences & Compounds

Translate and analyze the compounds:

1. नराः स्वर्गं लब्धुं देवान्यज्ञ्नैर्यष्टुमिच्छन्ति ॥१॥
   To obtain heaven, men wish to worship the gods with sacrifices (*yajñaiḥ*).
2. महापुण्यं कृत्वा गतपापजनेन नरकं गन्तुं न शक्यते ॥२॥
   Having performed great merit (*mahāpuṇyam*), it is not possible for a person free from sin (*gatapāpa-janena*) to go to hell.
3. सुगतो लोकान्मोक्तुमार्यसत्यान्युपदिशति ॥४॥
   In order to liberate the worlds, the Buddha (*Sugata*) teaches the Noble Truths (*ārya-satyāni*).
4. धर्मं श्रोतुकामा ब्राह्मणी सपुत्रा गुरुं द्रष्टुं महानगरं गata ॥८॥
   Desiring to hear the Dharma (*dharma-śrotu-kāmā*), the Brahmin woman went to the great city (*mahā-nagaram*) with her son to see the teacher.

## Subhāṣita: Humans and Animals

आहारनिद्राभयमैथुनं च / सामान्यमेतत्पशुभिर्नराणाम् । / धर्मे हि तेषा्मधिको विशेषो / धर्मेण हीनाः पशुभिः समानाः ॥
"Eating, sleeping, fear, and procreation are common to both humans and animals. The distinguishing feature of humans is Dharma. Without Dharma, they are equal to animals."
