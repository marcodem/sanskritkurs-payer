## 15.1. Subhāṣitam

> गुरुशुश्रूषया विद्या  
> पुष्कलेन धनेन वा ।  
> अथवा विद्यया विद्या  
> चतुर्थी नैव विद्यते ॥
> 
> guruśuśrūṣayā vidyā  
> puṣkalena dhanena vā |  
> athavā vidyayā vidyā  
> caturthī naiva vidyate ||
> 
> Knowledge [is obtained] by service to the teacher,  
> or by abundant wealth,  
> or knowledge [is exchanged] for knowledge;  
> a fourth [way] does not exist.

## 15.2. Determinative Compounds = Tatpuruṣa m. = तत्पुरुष

The relationship between things designated by nouns (substantives and adjectives) can be expressed not only by a genitive construction but also by a Tatpuruṣa (तत्पुरुष). Likewise, attributive coordination of adjectives or appositional coordination of substantives can be expressed by a specific type of Tatpuruṣa, the so-called Karmadhāraya (m.) = कर्मधारय.

तत्पुरुषः = तस्य पुरुषः "his servant," i.e., an example of such a compound serves as the name for this entire category.

In determinative compounds (Tatpuruṣa), one noun (substantive or adjective) is defined more closely by another noun or adverb. The word being defined usually forms the final member of the compound.

The relationship of the initial member (determining member) to the final member (determined member) can be:

*   **Attributive or Appositional**: When resolving the compound, the initial member forms a more precise definition for the final member in the same case (i.e., outside a sentence context in the Nominative (प्रथमा), or in the sentence in the syntactically required case).
*   **Case-related**: When resolving the compound, the initial member stands in a different case than the final member (i.e., not in the Nominative - प्रथमा).
*   **Adverbial**: The initial member is a non-declinable word.

The case relationship of the two members within a compound is independent of the case in which the whole compound stands, as the compound functions as a single declinable word:

e.g.

> Nom. sg. tatpuruṣaḥ = tasya puruṣaḥ (his servant)
> Akk. sg. tatpuruṣam = tasya puruṣam
> Instr. sg. tatpuruṣeṇa = tasya puruṣeṇa
> Gen. sg. tatpuruṣasya = tasya puruṣasya
> Nom. pl. tatpuruṣāḥ = tasya puruṣāḥ
> etc.

The gender of a Tatpuruṣa is – with few exceptions – that of its final member.

## 15.3. Determinative Compounds with an Attributive/Appositional Initial Member = Karmadhāraya m. = कर्मधारय

When resolving a Karmadhāraya, both members of the compound stand in the same case.

e.g.

> guṇavat-putraḥ = guṇavān putraḥ = "a son with good qualities"
> 
> > Akk. sg. guṇavat-putram  
> > Nom. pl. guṇavat-putrāḥ
> 
> puṇyavat-kṣatriyā = puṇyavatī kṣatriyā = "a meritorious Kṣatriya woman"
> 
> sādhu-janāḥ = sādhavo janāḥ = "good people"
> 
> iṣṭ-devatā = iṣṭā devatā = "the desired deity = the deity to whom one has a special relationship of devotion and refuge"
> 
> ![](/images/lekt1501.jpg)  
> Fig.: Lakṣmī, the goddess of prosperity, an iṣṭa-devatā for many Indians.  
> Painting by Rājā Ravi Varmā (1848 - 1906).  
> [Image source: Wikipedia, Public domain]

Regarding the order of members in a Karmadhāraya, the following special rule should be noted:

*   If a Karmadhāraya expresses a COMPARISON, then the thing being compared to stands as the final member of the compound:
    
    nara-siṃhaḥ = siṃha iva naraḥ = "a man like a lion"
    puruṣa-vyāghraḥ = vyāghra iva puruṣaḥ = "a man like a tiger"
    
*   However, if a Karmadhāraya expresses an APPOSITION (closer definition of the final member by a substantive), the defining word stands as the initial member, as required by the general rule for the sequence of members in a Tatpuruṣa:
    
    nara-siṃhaḥ can also be resolved as: nara eva siṃhaḥ = "A lion who is (in reality) a man."
      
    Such appositional Karmadhārayas are resolved in indigenous commentaries as in the example above using *eva*.

## 15.4. Determinative Compounds with an Initial Member in a Non-Attributive Case Relationship = Tatpuruṣa in the Narrower Sense

Tatpuruṣa constructions are possible for combinations of nouns (substantives and adjectives) where the initial member – in accordance with the rules of syntax – can stand in any case. As expected, the initial member most frequently represents a Genitive (षष्ठी), as this is the standard case for expressing the relationship between nouns.

e.g.

> kṣatriya-putraḥ = kṣatriyasya putraḥ = "the son of a Kṣatriya," "a young Kṣatriya," "a member of the Kṣatriya group"
> 
> > Akk. sg. kṣatriya-putram  
> > Gen. sg. kṣatriya-putrasya  
> > etc.
> 
> guru-bhāvaḥ = guror bhāvaḥ = "the nature of a teacher"
> 
> dhana-lobhaḥ = dhanasya lobhaḥ = "desire for wealth, greed"
> 
> loka-gatiḥ = lokasya gatiḥ = "the way of the world, the behavior of people"

Almost any genitive relationship can be replaced by a Tatpuruṣa. For the few exceptions, see e.g., Kale, *A Higher Sanskrit Grammar* § 211, and the corresponding passages in Pāṇini.

The initial member of a Tatpuruṣa can theoretically represent any case. However, not all syntactically possible case relationships can be replaced by a Tatpuruṣa. In case of doubt, the corresponding rules can be found in Kale, § 203 - 217 or Pāṇini 2,1,22 - 2,2,22.

When resolving the compound, the initial member can be in the singular, dual, or plural. Which possibility is intended must be inferred from the meaning and context.

Examples:

The initial member represents the Accusative (द्वितीया): e.g., with certain PPPs related to verbs of motion (Pāṇini 2,1,24):

> grāma-gataḥ = grāmaṃ gataḥ = "one who has gone to the village"  
> naraka-patitā = narakaṃ patitā = "one (fem.) who has fallen into a hell"

The initial member represents the Instrumental (तृतीया): e.g., frequently the agent (kartṛ) of nominal formations with kṛt-suffixes (e.g., PPP):

> deva-kṛtam = devena / devaiḥ kṛtam = "made by a god / by gods"  
> Could also be resolved as: devasya / devānāṃ kṛtam = "deed / doing of a god / of the gods; divine act"
> 
> buddha-rakṣitā = buddhena rakṣitā = "she who is protected by the Buddha" (a proper name)

## 15.5. Decomposition of Compounds (except Dvandvas)

Although Sanskrit compounds can be formed of any length and are indeed very common (compounds of 10 to 30 members are not rare!), all compounds – with the exception of Dvandvas – can be systematically decomposed hierarchically into two parts at a time:

![](/images/lekt1502.jpg)

... and so on until the individual word stems are reached.

e.g.

guṇavat-putra-kṛta-puṇyam

> 1st Level (main break): guṇavat-putra-kṛtam ॥1॥ puṇyam
> 
> 2nd Level (1st secondary break): guṇavat-putreṇa ॥2॥ kṛtam ॥1॥ puṇyam
> 
> 3rd Level (2nd secondary break): guṇavatā ॥3॥ putreṇa ॥2॥ kṛtam ॥1॥ puṇyam
> 
> = "the meritorious deed (merit) done by my virtuous son"

Different types of compounds can be mixed, e.g., initial member: Bahuvrīhi (बहुव्रीहि) – final member: Tatpuruṣa, etc.

e.g.

brāhmaṇa-kṣatriya-vaiśya-dharmaḥ

> 1st Level: brāhmaṇa-kṣatriya-vaiśyānāṃ dharmaḥ (Initial member: Itaretaradvandva)
> 
> 2nd Level: brāhmaṇānāṃ kṣatriyāṇāṃ vaiśyānāṃ ca dharmaḥ
> 
> = "the dharma of Brahmins, Kṣatriyas, and Vaiśyas"

Very often, there are various possibilities for resolving a compound. Which one is correct or at least best must be decided based on context and content. Sometimes such a decision is impossible. Frequently, two resolution possibilities are presumably intended by the author. In such cases, one must render both possibilities in the translation (connected by "and," "or," "respectively," and the like).

e.g.

puṇyavat-putra-kṛtam

> 1st Level: either - or
> 
> 1. puṇyavat-putreṇa kṛtam
> 2. puṇyavat-putrasya kṛtam
> 3. puṇyavat putra-kṛtam
> 
> 2nd Level: accordingly
> 
> 1. puṇyavatā putreṇa kṛtam = "that which was done by my meritorious son"
> 2. puṇyavataḥ putrasya kṛtam = "the deed of my meritorious son"
> 3. 1. puṇyavat putreṇa kṛtam = "the meritorious thing done by my son"
>    2. puṇyavat putrasya kṛtam = "the meritorious deed of my son"

## 15.6. The Form of the Initial Member in Compounds (samāsa m. = समास)

In all types of compounds, the initial member is usually the unchanged word stem. Two-stem nouns stand in the weak stem. Feminine adjectives that modify a subsequent member in the compound generally stand in the masculine stem:

e.g.

puṇyavat-kṣatriyā = puṇyavatī kṣatriyā = "a Kṣatriya woman who possesses merit"  
guṇavat-putraḥ = guṇavān putraḥ = "a son with good qualities"

## 15.7. Classification of Tatpuruṣas

1. prathamā-tatpuruṣaḥ: initial member stands in the Nominative (prathamā) in the resolution sentence (vigraha-vākya).
2. dvitīyā-tatpuruṣaḥ: initial member stands in the Accusative (dvitīyā) in the vigraha-vākya.
3. tṛtīyā-tatpuruṣaḥ: initial member stands in the Instrumental (tṛtīyā) in the vigraha-vākya.
4. caturthī-tatpuruṣaḥ: initial member stands in the Dative (caturthī) in the vigraha-vākya.
5. pañcamī-tatpuruṣaḥ: initial member stands in the Ablative (pañcamī) in the vigraha-vākya.
6. ṣaṣṭhī-tatpuruṣaḥ: initial member stands in the Genitive (ṣaṣṭhī) in the vigraha-vākya.
7. saptamī-tatpuruṣaḥ: initial member stands in the Locative (saptamī) in the vigraha-vākya.

* Karmadhārayaḥ: appositional Tatpuruṣa, including:
    * upamāna-pūrvapada-karmadhārayaḥ: vigraha-vākya with *iva* after the first member.
    * upamānottarapada-karmadhārayaḥ: vigraha-vākya with *iva* after the second member.
    * rūpaka-samāsaḥ: vigraha-vākya with *eva*.
    * dvigu-samāsaḥ: numeral as the initial member.
* nañ-tatpuruṣaḥ (niṣedha-tatpuruṣaḥ): with negation a-, an- as the initial member.
* gati-samāsaḥ: with a preposition as the initial member.

### 15.7.1. Classification of Karmadhārayas

1. viśeṣaṇa-pūrvapada-karmadhārayaḥ: initial member is an adjective (viśeṣaṇa).
2. viśeṣaṇobhayapada-karmadhārayaḥ: both members are adjectives, including adjectives expressing a temporal sequence: "first bathed, then anointed."
3. upamāna-pūrvapada-karmadhārayaḥ (= upamā-samāsaḥ): comparison in the initial member, attribute compared in the final member: e.g., "beautiful as a lotus."
4. upamānottarapada-karmadhārayaḥ (= upamita-samāsaḥ): comparison in the final member.
5. rūpaka-samāsaḥ: vigraha-vākya with *eva* after the initial member (metaphor).
6. saṃbhāvanapūrvapada-karmadhārayaḥ: both members refer to the same person or thing, e.g., kālidāsa-kaviḥ = kālidāsa iti kaviḥ = "the poet Kālidāsa."
7. Karmadhāraya with ku- / kad- as the initial member: "bad..."
8. Karmadhāraya with kim- as the initial member: blame/censure.
9. PPP + negated PPP: "partly," e.g., kṛtākṛtam = "partly done."
10. dvigu-samāsaḥ: numeral as the initial member.
