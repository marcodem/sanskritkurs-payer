# Exercise 50

A) To review the declension: the following verse contains all declension forms in the singular for गुरु m. (teacher/master):

गुरुरेव गतिर्गुरुमेव भजे  
गुरुणैव सहास्मि नमो गुरवे ।  
न गुरोः परमं शिशुरस्मि गुरोर्  
मतिरस्ति गुरौ मम पाहि गुरो ॥

> The Guru alone is my refuge, the Guru alone I serve,  
> I am with the Guru alone, salutations to the Guru.  
> There is nothing higher than the Guru, I am the child of the Guru,  
> My mind is in the Guru, O Guru, protect me!

B) Translate:

Manusmṛti 4, 178

येनास्य पितरो याता  
येन याताः पितामहाः ।  
तेन यायात्सतां मार्गम्  
तेन गच्छन्न रिष्यते ॥१॥

> One should walk on the path of the good, on which the fathers have gone, on which the grandfathers have gone; when walking on this path, one suffers no harm.

Manusmṛti 3, 63

कुविवाहैः क्रियालोपैर्  
वेदानध्ययनेन च ।  
कुलान्यकुलतां यान्ति  
ब्राह्मणातिक्रमेण च ॥२॥

> Families become non-families (are ruined) through bad marriages, through the omission of rites, through the non-study of the Veda, and through offenses against Brahmins.

Manusmṛti 3, 60

संतुष्टो भार्यया भर्ता  
भर्त्रा भार्या तथैव च ।  
यस्मिन्नेव कुले नित्यम्  
कल्याणं तत्र वै ध्रुवम् ॥३॥

> In whatever family the husband is always satisfied with the wife and the wife likewise with the husband, in that family prosperity is certainly constant.

Manusmṛti 3, 75–76: On the necessity of sacrifice

स्वाध्याये नित्ययुक्तः स्याद्  
दैवे चैवेह कर्मणि ।  
दैवे कर्मणि युक्तो हि  
बिभर्तीदं चराचरम् ॥४॥  
अग्नौ प्रास्ताहुतिः सम्यग्  
आदित्यमुपतिष्ठते ।  
आदित्याज्जायते वृष्टिर्  
वृष्टेरन्नं ततः प्रजाः ॥५॥

> One should be constantly engaged in the study of one's own Veda and in the rites for the gods. For he who is engaged in the rites for the gods supports this entire world of the moving and the non-moving. The offering thrown properly into the fire reaches the sun; from the sun arises rain, from rain food, and from that the creatures.

Yogasūtra 2, 16–17

हेयं दुःखमनागतम् ॥६॥  
द्रष्टृदृश्ययोः संयोगो हेयहेतुः ॥७॥

> The suffering that has not yet come is to be avoided.  
> The cause of that which is to be avoided is the connection of the seer and the seen [= the perceiver and the perceivable].

Kauṭilīya Arthaśāstra 1, 15: On the king's advisors

न किंचिदवमन्येत  
सर्वस्य शृणुयान्मतम् ।  
बालस्याप्यर्थवद्वाक्यम्  
उपयुन्जीत पाण्डितः ॥८॥

> [The king] should despise nothing; he should hear the opinion of everyone. A wise man should utilize even the words of a child if they are meaningful.

Manusmṛti 2, 140–142: Definitions of आचार्या (ācārya), उपाध्याय (upādhyāya), गुरु (guru)

उपनीय तु यः शिष्यं  
वेदमधापयेत्द्विजः ।  
सकल्पं सरहस्यं च  
तमाचार्यां प्रचक्षते ॥९॥

> They call that twice-born man an Ācārya who, after performing the initiation (upanayana) of a student, teaches him the Veda, along with the ritual and the secret doctrine.

एकदेशं तु वेदस्य  
वेदाङ्गान्यपि वा पुनः ।  
यो ऽध्यापयति वृत्त्यर्थम्  
उपाध्यायः स उच्यते ॥१०॥

> He who teaches a portion of the Veda or the auxiliary sciences for the sake of his livelihood is called an Upādhyāya.

निषेकादीनि कर्माणि  
यः करोति यथाविधि ।  
संभावयति चान्नेन  
स विप्रो गुरुरुच्यते ॥११॥

> That Brahmin is called a Guru who vorschriftsmäßig performs the Niṣeka and other rites and maintains him with food.
