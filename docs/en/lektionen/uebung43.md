B) Translate into Sanskrit:

1. Why do you (pl.) sit while the teacher stands?

> कस्माद्गुरौ तिष्ठति सीदथ । कस्माद्गुरौ तिष्ठत्याध्वे ॥१॥

2. Do you doubt whether a good deed has a good fruit?

> किं विकल्पयसि किं सुकर्मणः सुफलमस्तीति ॥२॥

3. Will you (pl.) show the father the innermost temple shrine?

> कच्चित्पितरं गर्भगृहं दर्शयिष्यथ । कच्चित्पित्रे गर्भगृहं देक्ष्यथ ॥३॥

4. Which poet's song of praise did you (sg.) sing?

> कस्य कवेः स्तोत्रमगायः ॥४॥

5. Will you (pl.) sell these fruits?

> एतानि फलानि विक्रेष्यध्वे ॥५॥

6. What did you (sg.) command?

> किमाज्ञापयः ॥६॥

7. When did you (sg.) stay (vṛt) in Benares?

> कदा काश्यामवर्तथाः ॥७॥

8. Did you (pl.), as masters of the sacrifice, worship the gods with a sacrifice?

> कच्चिद्देवानयजध्वम् । कच्चिद्देवान्यजमाना अयजध्वम् ॥८॥

9. In which city were you (sg.) born?

> कस्मिन्नगरे ऽजायथाः ॥९॥

10. How do you (sg.) rescue yourself (cross over) from the enemy?

> कथं शत्रुं तरसि ॥१०॥

| Dialogue | |
| --- | --- |
| Sureśa: | Aśoka, where are you going now? |
| Aśoka: | I am going to the shop. I will quickly buy some goods and return. |
| Sureśa: | Is there a festival at your house today? |
| Aśoka: | Yes. Today is the birthday celebration of my respected father. |
| Sureśa: | How old is your respected father? |
| Aśoka: | My father's age is sixty-five years. Today many relatives and friends will come to my house. You must also come! |
| Sureśa: | My dear, at what time will the people come? |
| Aśoka: | Why do you ask about the time? Come by six o'clock! |
| Sureśa: | And if I come by half past six? |
| Aśoka: | Not at all! You must definitely come at six o'clock in the prescribed manner. |
| Sureśa: | So be it! Go then! Otherwise you will be late. |
| Aśoka: | (Enters a shop, approaches the merchant and says:) O best of merchants, I have come to buy some goods. Give them to me quickly! |
| Merchant: | My dear, what do you wish to buy? In my shop there are many excellent goods and they are very cheap. Say how much of what you wish to buy! |
| Aśoka: | For what price is a seer of sugar sold? |
| Merchant: | Not a high price. I will give you a seer of sugar for only eight Annas. |
| Aśoka: | Then weigh out quickly a seer of sugar and give it to me! |
| Merchant: | (Wrapping the sugar in paper into a packet:) Boy, take the sugar! And give me eight Annas. Give it quickly. |
| Aśoka: | (Taking it in his hand and noticing its light weight:) O merchant! This does not look like a seer of sugar. Weigh it correctly again and give it to me. |
| Merchant: | (With a laugh:) What of it? It doesn't matter. Your trouble of carrying the load will be less. |
| Aśoka: | (Thinking a little in his mind and placing four Annas before him:) O merchant, take the price. I must go home quickly. (Having said this, he departs.) |
| Merchant: | Hey boy! Come, come, listen then! Where are you going after paying only a small price? Give me another four Annas! |
| Aśoka: | O merchant! What of it? It doesn't matter. Your trouble of counting the coins will be less! (Having said this, he runs away quickly.) |

The cheating merchant standing in the shop could do nothing; there was only regret in his mind.

Aśoka came home and told the whole story. All the relatives and friends there experienced great joy. Thus the birthday celebration ended joyfully.

(After: Sarala Sanskrit Shikshak III, p. 4f.)
