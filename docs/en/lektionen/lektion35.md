# Lesson 35

## 35.1. Perfect Type III: Strong High/Extended-Grade

**Formation:**
**Strong Stem:**
*   3. sg. P: Extended grade (*vṛddhi*)
*   2. sg. P: High grade (*guṇa*)
*   1. sg. P: Choice of high or extended grade.

### 35.1.1. Perfect Type IIIa: Strong High/Extended, Weak Deep

**Formation:**
**Weak Stem:** Deep grade.
Used for roots of the form:
*   (Consonant)-(Consonant)-i/ī/u/ū
*   (Consonant)-ṛ

Before vowel-initial endings:
*   *-i/-ī* becomes *-iy-*
*   *-u/-ū* becomes *-uv-*
*   *-ṛ* becomes *-r-*

**Examples:**
*   **i** 2P: *iyāya*, *īyur*.
*   **nī** 1U: *nināya*, *ninyur*, *ninye*, *ninyire*.
*   **stu** 2U: *tuṣṭāva*, *tuṣṭuvur*, *tuṣṭuve*, *tuṣṭuvire*.
*   **pū**: *pupāva*, *pupuvur*, *pupuve*, *pupuvire*.
*   **kṛ**: *cakāra*, *cakrur*, *cakre*, *cakrire*.

### 35.1.2. Perfect Type IIIb: Strong High/Extended, Weak High

**Formation:**
**Weak Stem:** High grade.
Used for roots of the form:
*   (Consonant)-(Consonant)-ṝ
*   Consonant-Consonant-ṛ (roots ending in -ṛ preceded by multiple consonants)

**Examples:**
*   **pṛ** 3P: *papāra*, *paparur*.
*   **smṛ** 1P: *sasvāra* (*sasmarya*?), *sasmarur*.
*   **saṃskṛ** 8U: *sañcaskāra*, *sañcaskarur*, *sañcaskare*, *sañcaskarire*.

## 35.2. Perfect Type IV: Roots in *-ā / -ai*

**Formation:**
**Strong Stem:**
*   1./3. sg. P Perfect ends in **-au**.
*   2. sg. P: High grade *-ā* or deep grade *-i*.
**Weak Stem:**
*   Before consonants: Deep grade **-i-**.
*   Before vowels: Deep grade **Ø** (disappears).

**Example:**
*   **dā** 3U: *dadau*, *dadur*, *dade*, *dadire*.

## 35.3. Perfect Type V: Roots *Consonant-a-Consonant*

### 35.3.1. Perfect Type Va: Weak Deep

Used for specific roots like *gam, han, jan, vac, vad, yaj*.
*   **gam** 1P: *jagāma*, *jagmur*.
*   **han** 2P: *jaghāna*, *jaghnur*.
*   **jan** 4Ā: *jajñe*, *jajñire*.
*   **vac** 2P: *uvāca*, *ūcur*.
*   **vad** 1P: *uvāda*, *ūdur*.
*   **yaj** 1U: *iyāja*, *ījur*, *īje*, *ījire*.

### 35.3.2. Perfect Type Vb: No Reduplication, *-e-* in Weak Stem

Used for roots with *a* between two simple consonants where the initial consonant remains unchanged in reduplication (i.e., not a guttural, aspirate, or *h*).
*   **pac** 1U: *papāca*, *pecur*, *pece*, *pecire*.
*   **naś** 4P: *nanāśa*, *neśur*.

### 35.3.3. Perfect Type Vc: Strong High, Weak High

Used for all other roots with middle *-a-* that don't fit the above (e.g., roots starting with clusters, gutturals, or aspirates).
*   **kram** 1U: *cakrāma*, *cakramur*, *cakrame*, *cakramire*.

## 35.4. Vocabulary

*   **naś** 4P (*naśyati*, Perf. *nanāśa/neśur*, PPP *naṣṭa*): to go lost, vanish, perish.
*   **naś + pra** 4P (*praṇaśyati*): to vanish, perish.
*   **kram** 1U (*krāmati*), 4P (*krāmyati*, Perf. *cakrāma*, PPP *krānta*): to step, go.

*   **gai** 1P (*gāyati*, Perf. *jagau/jagur*, PPP *gīta*): to sing, recite, proclaim in verse.
    *   **gītā** f.: song, canto.

## 35.5. Exercise

A) Form the corresponding Perfect forms for:
*   *gāyanti, smaranti, eṣyati, gacchanti, kurute, neṣyante, jāyate, jānāti, saṃskaroti, punīte, bibhyati, pipurati, datte, yajante, bhajanti (unreg. Type Vb!), vakti, adadhuḥ, bibhrate, mariṣyanti, aśaknuvan, avadan, apibat, apān, alabhata, ahan, manyante, akrāman, aśṛṇvan, mimate, apadyata, sunoti, atiṣṭhat, patiṣyanti, apacat, ajahur, dhakṣyanti, stauti, tanute, acarat, juhvati, aherat.*
    ![](/images/lekt3503.jpg)

B) Translate:
1. Once, a great warrior undertook (*upacakrame*) to sacrifice. Indra took away his sacrificial beast. When the animal had vanished, a wicked Brahmin said to the warrior: "The animal was taken because of the warrior's bad conduct."
2. Rāma was without a son (*aputra*). He desired a son but did not obtain one. Therefore he sacrificed to the gods and performed vows like celibacy (*brahmacarya*). The gods heard Rāma's sacrifice and gave Rāma a desired son.
3. The Brahmin women cooked ghee for the sacrifice. While the Brahmin women were cooking, the Brahmins prepared the place of sacrifice. Then the warriors sacrificed to the gods like Śiva and the Brahmins also sacrificed.
   ![](/images/lekt3504.jpg)
4. The Arhats broke the family bonds and hindered greed, anger, and delusion; they recognized the truth and, freed from suffering, attained the joy of liberation.

C) Rewrite the sentences in B) by replacing Perfect forms with Imperfect forms.
