# Lesson 6

## 6.1. The Verbal Sentence

Schema I: Verbal Form

> e.g., *yajati* = यजति = "He (she, it) venerates with a sacrifice" / "He (she, it) sacrifices"

Schema II: Agent (*kartṛ* m. = कर्तṛ) - Verbal Form

> e.g., *rāmo yajati* = रामो यजति = "Rāma venerates with a sacrifice" / "Rāma sacrifices"

If the agent (*kartṛ* m. = कर्तृ) is named in a verbal sentence that is not in the passive, the agent stands in the **nominative** (first case, *prathamā* = प्रथमा). In number (*vacana* n. = वचन), the agent and the verbal form must agree.

## 6.2. On the Formation of Verbal Forms

A finite verbal form, i.e., a verbal form with a personal ending, expresses the following in Sanskrit:

1.  **Meaning** (*artha* m. अर्थ)
2.  **Person and Number** (*vacana* n. = वचन) (I, you, he/she/it, we two, you two, they two, we, you, they)
3.  **Voice** (*genus verbi*):
    1.  **Parasmaipada** (n. = परस्मैपद) ("word form with reference to another"): **Active**
        e.g., *yajati* = यजति = "He venerates with a sacrifice" (namely, the priest who performs the sacrifice for someone else).
    2.  **Ātmanepada** (n. = आत्मनेपद) ("word form with reference to oneself"): **Middle**
        e.g., *yajate* = यजते = "He venerates with a sacrifice in his own interest" (namely, the sacrificer who sacrifices for himself and his family).
    3.  **Passive** (*karman* n. = कर्मन्)
        e.g., *ijyate* = इज्यते = "He/it is venerated with a sacrifice."
        
        In many cases, the nuance of meaning in the *Ātmanepada* is no longer recognizable; even classical poets often use the *Ātmanepada* without any difference in meaning from the *Parasmaipada*. Nevertheless, in translation, one should always check whether such a nuance is present.
        
        Verbs used in both *Parasmaipada* and *Ātmanepada* (in the specific *Ātmanepada* sense) are called **Ubhayapada** verbs (*ubhayapada* n. = उभयपद = "both word forms").
        
        Some verbs are used either only in the *Parasmaipada* or only in the *Ātmanepada*. In these cases, the choice of *Ātmanepada* or *Parasmaipada* carries no specific meaning. e.g., *manyate* = मन्यते = "he (she, it) thinks" (without any specific *Ātmanepada* meaning).
4.  **Mood** (*Modus*):
    1.  **Indicative**: form of a statement.
    2.  **Optative**: form of a wish or possibility.
    3.  **Imperative**: form of a command.
5.  **Tense** (*Tempus*):
    1.  **Present**: current time.
    2.  **Imperfect**
    3.  **Perfect**
    4.  **Aorist**
    5.  **Future**: time to come.
    6.  **Conditional**
    
    The tenses are formed from tense stems: Present stem, Aorist stem, Perfect stem, Future stem.

## 6.3. Indicative Present (*laṭ* = लट्)

The present is the tense of the current time, and especially of duration.

The Indicative (statement form) Present is formed by attaching the so-called **primary endings** to the present stem.

e.g.,

| Root (*dhātu* = धातु) | Present Stem | 3rd Person singular Indicative Present Parasmaipada |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **viś = विश्** | *viśa* = विश | *viśati* = विशति = "he (she, it) enters" |
| **bhū = भू** | *bhava* = भव | *bhavati* = भवति = "he (she, it) becomes/is" |
| **nṛt = नृत्** | *nṛtya* = नृत्य | *nṛtyati* = नृत्यति = "he (she, it) dances" |

## 6.4. Primary Endings of the Third Person (*prathama* m. = प्रथम = "first (!) person")

| | Singular (*ekavacana* n. एकवचन) | Plural (*bahuvacana* n. बहुवचन) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Parasmaipada** n. परस्मैपद | **-ti** -ति | **-nti** -न्ति |
| **Ātmanepada** n. आत्मनेपद | **-te** -ते | **-nte** -न्ते |

e.g., *yaj* = यज् = "to venerate with a sacrifice," "to sacrifice"

> 3rd sg. P. *yajati* = यजति  
> 3rd pl. P. *yajanti* = यजन्ति
> 
> 3rd sg. Ā. *yajate* = यजते

## 6.5. Formation of the Present Base

### 6.5.1. Verbs of the 6th Present Class (*tudādi* = तुदादि = "tud etc.")

| Rule: Present stem = Root in weak grade (as listed) + *a-* |
| :--- |

| Root (*dhātu* m. धातु) | Present Stem |
| :--- | :--- |
| **viś** विश् | *viśa-* विश- |
| **sṛj** सृज् | *sṛja-* सृज- |

### 6.5.2. Verbs of the 1st Present Class (*bhvādi / bhūvādi* = भ्वादि / भूवादि = "bhū etc.")

| Rule: Present stem = Root in full grade (rarely lengthened grade) + *a-* |
| :--- |

| Root (*dhātu* m. धातु) | Full Grade | Full Grade before *a-* | Present Stem |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **bhū** भू | *bho* भो | *bhav* भव् | *bhava-* भव- |
| **nī** नी | *ne* ने | *nay* नय् | *naya-* नय- |
| **smṛ** स्मृ | *smar* स्मर् | *smar* स्मर् | *smara-* स्मर- |
| **yaj** यज् | *yaj* यज् | *yaj* यज् | *yaja-* यज- |

If the vowel is in a **long closed syllable**, i.e., a short vowel before two or more consonants, the formation of the full grade is omitted:
*   *nind* निन्द् → *nind* निन्द् → *nind* निन्द् → *ninda-* निन्द-

### 6.5.2.1. Word Sandhi of *e* and *o*

Before vowels within a word, *e* is replaced by *ay*, and *o* by *av*.

### 6.5.2.2. Gradation (Ablaut)

| Weak Grade (Zero Grade) | Full Grade (Guṇa m. गुण) | Lengthened Grade (Vṛddhi f. वृद्धि) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| ø | a | ā |
| i/ī | e | ai |
| u/ū | o | au |
| ṛ/ṝ | ar | ār |
| ḷ | al | āl |

### 6.5.3. Verbs of the 4th Present Class (*divādi* = दिवादि = "div etc.")

| Rule: Present stem = Root in weak grade (as listed) + *ya-* |
| :--- |

| Root (*dhātu* m. धातु) | Present Stem |
| :--- | :--- |
| **nṛt** नृत् | *nṛtya-* नृत्य- |
| **muh** मुह् | *muhya-* मुह्य- |
| **yudh** युध् | *yudhya-* युध्य- |
| **man** मन् | *manya-* मन्य- |

### 6.5.4. Thematic Vowel, Thematic Present Classes

The *a* in the stem-forming suffixes of present classes is called the **thematic vowel**. Present classes with an *a* in the stem-forming suffix are therefore called "**thematic present classes**."

## 6.6. Spoken Sanskrit: Further Questions (*praśna* m. = प्रश्न)

*   *N.N. kiṃ karoti?* = N.N. किं करोति = "What does N.N. do?"
*   *N.N. (plural) kiṃ kurvanti?* = N.N. किं कुर्वन्ति = "What do the N.N.s do?"
    *   *karoti, kurvanti* = करोति, कुर्वन्ति from *kṛ* = कृ 8 U: "to do, to make"
*   *kiṃ kuśalam?* = किं कुशलम् = "Is all well with you? How are you?"
    *   Answer: *sarvathā kuśalam* = सर्वथा कुशलम् = "(I am) well in every regard."

## 6.7. Vocabulary

In Sanskrit dictionaries, verbs are listed in their root form. The number after the root indicates the conjugation class.

*   **P**: Root is only used in *Parasmaipada*.
*   **Ā**: Root is only used in *Ātmanepada*.
*   **U**: *Ubhayapada* ("both word forms"): Root is used in both *Parasmaipada* and *Ātmanepada*, and in the *Ātmanepada* it carries the specific *Ātmanepada* sense (when something happens in one's own interest).
*   **()**: The 3rd person (*prathama*) singular Indicative Present (*laṭ*) is given in parentheses.

Learn the following words:

*   **yaj** 1 U (*yajati*) यज् यजति : to venerate with a sacrifice, to sacrifice.
*   **bhū** 1 P (*bhavati*) भू भवति : to become, to arise, to be.
*   **smṛ** 1 P (*smarati*) स्मृ स्मरति : to bring to mind, to remember.
*   **nṛt** 4 P (*nṛtyati*) नृत् नृत्यति : to dance.
*   **nī** 1 U (*nayati*) नी नयति : to lead.
*   **man** 4 Ā (*manyate*) मन् मन्यते : to think.
*   **muh** 4 P (*muhyati*) मुह् मुह्यति : to be confused.
*   **yudh** 4 Ā (*yudhyate*) युध् युध्यते : to fight.
*   **viś** 6 P (*viśati*) विश् विशति : to enter.
*   **sṛj** 6 P (*sṛjati*) सृज् सृजति : to release, to let go from oneself: to emanate.
