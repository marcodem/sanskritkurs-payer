# Exercise 40

A) Translate the Subhāṣitāni at the beginning of the lesson.

> विद्या ददाति विनयं  
> विनयाद्याति पात्रताम् ।  
> पात्रत्वाद्धनमाप्नोति  
> धनाद्धर्मं ततः सुखम् ॥१॥

Knowledge gives discipline; from discipline one attains worthiness. From worthiness one attains wealth; from wealth (one performs) Dharma, and then (attains) happiness.

> सुखार्थी चेत्त्यजेद्विद्यां  
> विद्यार्थी चेत्त्यजेत्सुखम् ।  
> सुखार्थिनः कुतो विद्या  
> कुतो विद्यार्थिनः सुखम् ॥२॥

If one desires happiness, he should give up knowledge; if one desires knowledge, he should give up happiness. For a seeker of happiness, where is knowledge? And for a seeker of knowledge, where is happiness?

> आचार्यात्पादमादत्ते  
> पादं शिष्यः स्वमेधया ।  
> पादं सब्रह्मचारिभ्यः  
> पादं कालक्रमेण च ॥३॥

A student receives one quarter (of knowledge) from his teacher, one quarter from his own intelligence, one quarter from his fellow students, and one quarter in the course of time.
