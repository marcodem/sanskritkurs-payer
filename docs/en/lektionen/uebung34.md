Translate the following text from the Padma Purana about gifts to Brahmins:

> क्षितिं सशस्यां यो दद्याद्ब्राह्मणाय द्विजोत्तम ।  
> विष्णुलोके सुखं भुङ्क्ते यावदिन्द्राश्चतुर्दश ॥१॥  
> सप्तद्वीपां महीं दत्त्वा यत्पुण्यं प्राप्यते द्विज ।  
> तत्पुण्यं प्राप्नुयान्मर्त्यो धेनुं यच्छन्द्विजातये ॥२॥  
> तिलप्रमाणं स्वर्णं यो ब्राह्मणाय प्रयच्छति ।  
> हरिनिकेतनं याति युक्तं कोटिकुलैरपि ॥३॥  
> सालङ्कारां द्विजश्रेष्ठ कन्यां यच्छति यो नरः ।  
> स गच्छेद्ब्रह्मसदनं पुनर्जन्म न विद्यते ॥४॥  
> अन्नं वारि द्विजश्रेष्ठ येन दत्तम् महीतले ।  
> तेन दत्तानि दानानि सर्वाणि च द्विजर्षभ ॥५॥

Best of the twice-born! Whoever gives the earth along with its crops to a Brahmin, O best of the twice-born, enjoys happiness in the world of Viṣṇu for as long as fourteen Indras. O twice-born! The merit that is obtained by giving away the whole earth with its seven continents, that merit a mortal shall obtain by giving a cow to a Brahmin. Whoever gives gold as small as a sesame seed to a Brahmin goes to the residence of Hari (Viṣṇu) even joined by ten million families. O best of the twice-born! That man who gives a daughter along with jewelry goes to the seat of Brahmā; a rebirth does not exist (for him). O best of the twice-born! Whoever has given food and water on the surface of the earth, by him all gifts have been given, O bull among the twice-born!

Explanations:

Vocative sg. of masculines/neuters in -a ends in -a: e.g. deva "God!"

चतुर्दश fourteen

सप्त seven

जन्म Nom./Acc. sg. of जन्मन् n. birth

सर्व 3 "all, whole" (declined according to pronominal declension)
