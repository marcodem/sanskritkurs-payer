# Lesson 16

## 16.1. On the Sandhi of Final Consonants

To form compounds in which the preceding element consists of stems ending in a consonant, the following rules are required:

Final guttural, retroflex, dental, and labial stops (i.e., excluding nasals and palatals).

| In pause and before voiceless sounds | Replacement by the corresponding voiceless, unaspirated sound:
e.g.

> \-g + p- » -kp-  
> \-d + ph- » -tph-  
> \-dh + p- » -tp-

 |
| Before voiced non-nasal sounds | Replacement by the corresponding voiced, unaspirated sound:

e.g.

> \-t + g- » -dg-  
    > \-k + b- » -gb-  
    > \-dh + d- » -dd-  
    > \-k + a- » -ga-

 |
| Before nasals | Replacement by the corresponding nasal:

e.g.

> \-t + n- » -nn-  
    > \-p + n- » -mn-

 |

Exception to the above rule:

Final dental stop (non-nasal):

| Before palatals | Replacement by the relevant unaspirated palatal:
i.e.

> \-t + c- » -cc-  
    > \-d + c- » -cc-  
    > \-th + c- » -cc-  
    > \-dh + c- » -cc-  
    > \-t/-th/-d/-dh + ch- » -cch-  
    > \-t/-th/-d/-dh + j- » -jj-  
    > \-t/-th/-d/-dh + jh- » -jjh-

 |
| --- | --- |
| + ś |  » -cś- or -cch- |
| Before retroflexes | Replacement by the relevant unaspirated retroflex:

i.e.

> \-t/-th/-d/-dh + ṭ(h)- » -ṭṭ(h)-  
    > \-t/-th/-d/-dh + ḍ(h)- » -ḍḍ(h)- 

 |
| Before l- | Replacement by -l:

i.e.

> \-t/-th/-d/-dh + l- » -ll-

 |

Examples:

तद् + फल = तत्फल

पुण्यवत् + ब्राह्मण = पुण्यवद्ब्राह्मण

पुण्यवत् + नर = पुण्यवन्नर

पुण्यवत् + पुरुष = पुण्यवत्पुरुष

सत्यवत् + चैतन्य =सत्यवच्चैतन्य

सुखवत् + लोक = सुखवल्लोक

पुत्रवत् + जन = पुत्रवज्जन

बलवत् + शत्रु = बलवच्छत्रु
