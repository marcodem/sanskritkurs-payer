# Exercise 14

## Proverbs

Translate the following proverbs and learn them by heart:

1. नीचो वदति न कुरुते / वदति न साधुः करोत्येव ॥१॥
   A lowly person speaks but does not act; a good person speaks not, but acts indeed.
2. शीलं नरस्य भूषणम् ॥२॥
   Virtue is the ornament of man.
3. सत्येन जनानां सुखं भवति ॥३॥
   Through truth, people gain happiness.
4. पापा नराः स्वर्गं न लभन्ते ॥४॥
   Sinful men do not reach heaven.
5. सत्यं लोकस्य दीपः ॥५॥
   Truth is the light of the world.

## Exercise

A) Form the Genitive Singular and Plural for the following words. Provide the meaning and gender of each word.

1. **anṛta** (n. untruth): *anṛtasya* / *anṛtānām*
2. **ṛṣi** (m. Vedic seer): *ṛṣeḥ* / *ṛṣīṇām*
3. **pāda** (m. foot): *pādasya* / *pādānām*
4. **buddhi** (f. intellect/wisdom): *buddheḥ* (or *buddhyāḥ*) / *buddhīnām*
5. **guru** (m. master): *guroḥ* / *gurūṇām*
6. **svarga** (m. heaven): *svargasya* / *svargāṇām*

B) Translate:

1. ब्राह्मणस्य पुत्रो ब्राह्मण्या ग्रामं गतः ।
   The son of the Brahmin went to the village with the Brahmin woman [or: to the village of the Brahmin woman].
2. यज्ञस्याग्निनान्नं दग्धम् ।
   The sacrificial fire has burned the food.
3. बुद्धः सत्यस्य बुद्ध्या मुक्तः ।
   The Buddha was liberated through the knowledge of the truth.
4. अधर्मो ऽनृतस्य वदनमित्यृषयो वदन्ति ।
   The Vedic seers say that speaking untruth is unrighteous.
5. नरा देवानां यज्ञैर्न मुच्यन्ते ।
   Men are not liberated through sacrifices to the gods.
6. बलवन्तः क्षत्रियाः शत्रूणां धनवन्ति नगराणि जयन्ति ।
   The powerful Kṣatriyas conquer the wealthy cities of the enemies.
