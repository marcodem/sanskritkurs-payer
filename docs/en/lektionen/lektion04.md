Regarding the Nom. sg. masc.:

*   *sa, eṣa* (स एष) occurs before all sounds except short *-a*
*   *so, eṣo* (सो एषो) occurs before *-a*, which is elided (dropped) with an *avagraha*
*   *saḥ, eṣaḥ* (सः एषः) occurs only in a pause (end of sentence, end of verse)

To construct sentences using these pronouns (*sarvanāman* n.), knowledge of the following Sandhi rules is also required:

### 4.1.2. Sandhi of final -m

A final *-m* is replaced by *anusvāra* (-ṃ) before consonants. At the end of a sentence or verse, and before vowels, *-m* remains unchanged.

### 4.1.3. Sandhi of final -e

1.  Before *a-*, *-e* remains unchanged: the initial *a-* is elided with an *avagraha*:  
    e.g., *ete + agnayas* » *ete 'gnayaḥ* = एते ऽग्नयः "These fires"
2.  Before vowels other than short *a-*, *-e* is usually replaced by *-a*, resulting in hiatus. Replacement by *-ay* also occurs:  
    e.g., *ime + īśvarās* » *ima īśvarāḥ* = इम ईश्वराः (rarely: *imay īśvarāḥ* = इमयीश्वराः) "These LORDS"

### 4.1.4. Sandhi of final -d

A final *-d* is replaced by *-t* in a pause, as well as before voiceless gutturals (*k, kh*) and labials (*p, ph*).

The replacement before other consonants will be discussed later. For the time being, avoid such phonetic collisions!

An example of other questions:

> viṣṇuḥ kaḥ? = विष्णुः कः Answer: viṣṇur īśvaraḥ = विष्णुरीश्वरः. (In a nominal sentence, one may place the subject, for example, at the beginning to emphasize its connection to the question.)

NOTE: The answers you are able to provide to such questions using your current knowledge of Sanskrit will, of course, not yet correspond to idiomatically perfect Sanskrit in every respect.
