# Exercise 27

## Exercise: Locative Case (Saptamī)

Insert the expressions in brackets in the Locative Singular and - where it makes sense - Plural. Note the Sandhi changes!

**Sentence: Rāmas ... vasati (Rāma lives in ...)**

1. (grāma - village) -> **Rāmo grāme vasati.** (Rāma lives in the village.)
2. (guru - teacher) -> **Rāmo gurau vasati.** (Rāma lives with the teacher.)
3. (satyavān kaviḥ - truthful poet) -> **Rāmaḥ satyavati kavau vasati.** (Rāma lives with the truthful poet.)
4. (gṛha - house) -> **Rāmo gṛhe vasati.** (Rāma lives in the house.)
5. (tad nagaram - that city) -> **Rāmas tasmin nagare vasati.** (Rāma lives in that city.)

## Sentences & Compounds (Locative Absolute)

Translate and analyze the compounds. Note the use of the **Locative Absolute** (indicated by "While...", "When...", or "As...").

1. धर्मं वदति गुरौ दुर्जना न शृण्वन्ति ॥१॥
   While the teacher speaks the Dharma (*dharmaṃ vadati gurau*), wicked people do not listen.
2. बुद्धकाले नरैरार्यसत्यानि श्रोतुं शक्यन्ते ॥२॥
   In the time of a Buddha (*buddha-kāle*), the Noble Truths can be heard by men.
3. पुत्रे मृते ऽपुत्रा ब्राह्मणी पुत्रं लब्धुं व्रतं करोति ॥४॥
   Her son having died (*putre mṛte*), the childless Brahmin woman performs a vow to obtain [another] son.
4. उपनीतबालैर्गुरुकुल उष्यते ॥५॥
   The boys, having been initiated (*upanītaiḥ bālaiḥ*), live in the teacher's family (*guru-kule*).
5. गुरौ तिष्ठति बाल आसितुं नार्हति ॥७॥
   While the teacher stands (*gurau tiṣṭhati*), a boy should not sit.
6. गुरुषूपदिशत्सु सुनीतबाला वक्तुं नार्हन्ति ॥१०॥
   While the teachers are instructing (*guruṣu upadiśatsu*), well-behaved children should not chatter.
