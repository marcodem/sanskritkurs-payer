B) Translate:

अक्रोधेन जयेत्क्रोधमसाधुं साधुना जयेज्जयेत्सत्येन चानृतम् ॥१॥

> One should conquer anger with non-anger, one should conquer the wicked with the good, one should conquer the false with the truth.

पुत्राः किं पितृभ्यः पिण्डान्प्रायच्छत ॥२॥

> Sons, did you give rice-balls (piṇḍas) to the ancestors?

कानि शास्त्राणि काश्यामध्यैथाः ॥३॥

> Which textbooks did you study in Kashi?

बुद्धपुत्राश्चेन्महाशयनेषु शयीरन्प्रव्रज्यायां कृतं व्रतं न चरेयुः ॥४॥

> If the sons of Buddha (= monks) were to lie on large beds, they would not keep the vow made at the time of their initiation as novices.

अप्यार्ययुद्धाख्यानमाख्यास्यन्तं गुरुमभ्यनन्दः ॥५॥

> Did you also welcome the teacher who was to tell the story of the noble warriors?

द्वेष्यमपि न द्विष्यात लोभनीयं च न लुभ्येतैवं च प्रसन्ना भविष्यथ ॥६॥

> You (pl.) should not hate even that which is hateful and you should not covet that which is desirable, and thus you will become serene.

न योत्स्य इत्यर्जुन उवाच ॥७॥

> "I will not fight," thus said Arjuna.

अशोच्यानशोचः प्रज्ञावादांश्च प्रवक्तुमैच्छः ।  
मृताञ्जीवतश्च बुद्धिमन्तो न शोचन्ति ॥८॥

> You have mourned for those not worth mourning for and wished to speak words of wisdom. The wise mourn neither for the dead nor for the living.
