## 38.2. Sound Changes of Nasals within Words

Nasals within a word are assimilated to the following consonant (replaced by the nasal of the same class).
After *c* and *j*, *n* is replaced by *ñ*.

## 38.3. Declension of -n Stems

### 38.3.1. Stems in *-an, -man, -van* after Vowels
Nouns ending in *-an*, and those in *-man* or *-van* after a vowel, have **three stems**:

1.  **Strong Stem** (*-ān, -mān, -vān*): Nom. sg. m./f., Acc. sg. m./f., Nom./Acc. dual m./f., Nom. pl. m./f., Nom./Acc. pl. n.
2.  **Middle (Weak) Stem** (*-a*): Used before endings starting with a consonant (e.g., *-bhis, -su*).
3.  **Weakest Stem** (*-n, -mn, -vn*): Used before endings starting with a vowel (except those listed for the strong stem).

**Examples:**
*   **rājan** m. (king): Strong *rājān-*, Middle *rāja-*, Weakest *rājñ-*.
*   **sīman** f. (border): Strong *sīmān-*, Middle *sīma-*, Weakest *sīmn-*.
*   **nāman** n. (name): Strong *nāmān-*, Middle *nāma-*, Weakest *nāmn-*.

| Case | rājan (m.) | sīman (f.) | nāman (n.) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nom. Sg.** | *rājā* | *sīmā* | *nāma* |
| **Acc. Sg.** | *rājānam* | *sīmānam* | *nāma* |
| **Instr. Sg.** | *rājñā* | *sīmnā* | *nāmnā* |
| **Loc. Sg.** | *rājñi / rājani* | *sīmni / sīmani* | *nāmni / nāmani* |
| **Nom. Pl.** | *rājānaḥ* | *sīmānaḥ* | *nāmāni* |
| **Acc. Pl.** | *rājñaḥ* | *sīmnaḥ* | *nāmāni* |
| **Instr. Pl.** | *rājabhiḥ* | *sīmabhiḥ* | *nāmabhiḥ* |
| **Loc. Pl.** | *rājasu* | *sīmasu* | *nāmasu* |

### 38.3.2. Stems in *-man, -van* after Consonants
These have only **two stems**:
1.  **Strong Stem**: *-mān, -vān*.
2.  **Weak Stem**:
    - Before consonants: *-ma, -va*.
    - Before vowels: *-man, -van*.

**Examples:**
*   **ātman** m. (self/soul): Nom. sg. *ātmā*, Acc. sg. *ātmānam*, Instr. sg. *ātmanā*, Loc. sg. *ātmani*.
*   **brahman** n. (Brahman/Veda): Nom. sg. *brahma*, Acc. sg. *brahma*, Instr. sg. *brahmaṇā*, Loc. sg. *brahmaṇi*.

### 38.3.3. Stems in *-in, -min, -vin*
These have no stem gradation in the base form.
*   **Nom. sg. m.**: ends in **-ī** (*balī*).
*   **Nom. sg. n.**: ends in **-i** (*bali*).
*   **Femininum**: formed with **-ī** (*balinī*).

## 38.4. Taddhita Suffix *-in*
Used to form adjectives meaning "possessing" or "characterized by".
*   **hastin** m. (elephant, "having a trunk/hand"): *hastin* denotes something with a special "hand" (trunk), whereas *hastavant* means someone with normal hands.

## 38.5. Syntax of *nāman*
To say "someone named N.N.", use: **N.N. (Nom.) nāma**.
Example: *āsīd rājā nalo nāma* (There was a king named Nala).
![](/images/lekt3808.jpg)

## 38.6. Syntax of *ātman*
*ātman* (m.) is used in the singular as a **reflexive pronoun** ("oneself") for all genders, numbers, and persons.
*   *ātmany eṣā doṣaṃ na paśyati* (She sees no fault in herself).
*   *ātmānaṃ stuvanti* (They praise themselves).
*   The genitive *ātmanaḥ* means "one's own".

## 38.7. Consonant Stems in Compounds
Consonant stems appear in the form they take before the locative plural ending *-su* (the "middle" stem).
Example: *rāja-putra* (king's son).

## 38.8. *-an* stems in Bahuvrīhis
Usually, the feminine of an *-an* stem at the end of a Bahuvrīhi is formed with **-ī** from the weakest masculine stem (e.g., *durṇāmnī*).

## 38.12. Reading Exercise: The Ten Fools (*Daśa Mūḍhāḥ*)

The actions of fools are usually amusing. Once, ten fools set out on a journey. They came to a deep river. Swimming with their arms, they somehow crossed it and reached the other bank. Among them was an old man. He, wishing to know if everyone had arrived, counted them one by one. But only nine were counted by him. Then he cried out: "Oh! We started as ten, now we are only nine! Surely one of us has drowned in the river. Look for him!" Then each of them also counted. But only nine were seen. Then a great noise arose among the agitated fools. Not far from there was the hermitage of a sage. Living there, the sage saw their searching and laughed loudly. Hearing the sound of his laughter, the fools approached him quickly and asked the reason for the laughter. The sage said: "Oh! You do not know your own selves! Each of you did not count himself (*na ātmānam agaṇayat*). Therefore this delusion has arisen." Hearing this, the fools, ashamed and with heads hanging, went on. (*Saṃskṛtapradhamādarśa*)
