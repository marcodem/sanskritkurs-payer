# Lesson 5

## 5.1. Nominal Compounds = *samāsa* m. = समास

The formation of word compositions on a very large scale is a characteristic of Sanskrit.

The most important forms of compounds are:

*   Copulative compounds = *dvandva* n. = द्वन्द्व
*   Determinative compounds = *tatpuruṣa* m. = तत्पुरुष
*   Possessive compounds = *bahuvrīhi* m. = बहुव्रीहि

(The latter two will be discussed later!)

## 5.2. On Sanskrit Terminology for Compounds

*   *ghaṭakapadāni* n. pl. = घटकपदानि = component parts of a compound
*   *vigrahavākyam* n. = विग्रहवाक्यम् = resolution (explanation) of a compound
*   *nityasamāsaḥ* m. = नित्यसमासः = a compound for which there is no *vigrahavākya*, or whose *vigrahavākya* is not possible using the words of the compound itself.
*   *aluksamāsaḥ* m. = अलुक्समासः = a compound in which the first member retains a case ending.
*   *luksamāsaḥ* m. = लुक्समासः = a compound whose preceding members are without case endings (the standard case).
*   *madhyamapadalopī* m. = मध्यमपदलोपी = a compound in which one or more middle members are omitted.

## 5.3. Copulative Compounds = *Dvandva* n. ("Pair") = द्वन्द्व

Copulative compounds serve to link grammatically similar, coordinated members (nouns or adjectives).

| A *Dvandva* designates: |
| :--- |
| 1. either the **summative connection** of the persons, things, or qualities designated by its individual members: *Itaretaradvandva* = इतरेतरद्वन्द्व |
| 2. or: it summarizes these persons, things, or qualities into a **single unit**: *samāhāradvandva* ("summation *dvandva*") = समाहारद्वन्द्व |

In the first case (*Itaretaradvandva*), the *Dvandva* takes the grammatical gender of its last member and the dual ending if it concerns two things, or the plural ending if it concerns more than two things. Singular endings are also permissible.

In the second case (*Samāhāradvandva*), the *Dvandva* is generally neuter, regardless of the gender of its last member, and stands in the singular.

| The resolution (*vigraha*) of a *Dvandva* is done with "and" (*ca* = च), occasionally with "or" (*vā* = वा) or "each." |
| :--- |

(Dual *dvandvas* will be discussed later!)

### 5.3.1. Component Members of Compounds

The preceding members of compounds (not just *Dvandvas*) usually take the form of the unchanged nominal stem. The individual members of compounds are connected according to the rules of **Sentence Sandhi** (i.e., the Sandhi we have learned so far, in contrast to word Sandhi, which is applied within a word before formative elements).

### 5.3.2. Plural Itaretaradvandva

Examples of plural *dvandvas*:

*   *brāhmaṇa-kṣatriya-vaiśya-śūdrāḥ* = ब्राह्मणक्षत्रियवैश्यशूद्राः
    *   = *brāhmaṇāḥ kṣatriyā vaiśyāḥ śūdrāś ca* = ब्राह्मणाः क्षत्रिया वैश्याः शूद्राश्च = "Brahmins, Kṣatriyas, Vaiśyas, and Śūdras"
    *   or = *brāhmaṇaḥ kṣatriyo vaiśyaḥ śūdraś ca* = ब्राह्मणः क्षत्रियो वैश्यः शूद्रश्च = "(a/the) Brahmin, Kṣatriya, Vaiśya, and Śūdra"

*   *kavi-guravaḥ* = कविगुरवः
    *   = *kavayo guravaś ca* = कवयो गुरवश्च = "poets and teachers" (plural). ("A poet and a teacher" would have to be in the dual!)

For the order of members in a *Dvandva*, the following rules (among others) apply:

*   The designation of the different estates (*varṇa*) should be ordered according to the hierarchy of the estates, starting with the highest.
*   The name of the older brother should precede the name of the younger brother.
*   In general, the more important word should be placed at the beginning.
*   Words ending in *-i* or *-u* should precede.
*   Words ending in *-a* and beginning with a vowel should precede.
*   Words containing fewer syllables should precede.

Where two or three of the latter rules could apply simultaneously, the rule given later in the list should always be followed.

## 5.4. Vocabulary

Learn the following words:

*   **abhiniveṣa** m. = अभिनिवेष : inclination towards, tenacity, persistence; specifically: attachment to the body as if it were one's own property.
*   **kāma** m. = काम : wish, desire, desired gift, sensual pleasure, love; the god of love Kāma.
    ![](/images/lekt0501.jpg)
    Fig.: The god Kāma = *kāmadeva* = कामदेव, 18th century.
*   **krodha** m. = क्रोध : anger.
*   **kleśa** m. = क्लेश : agony, affliction, torment.
*   **trayī** f. = त्रयी : triad; specifically the three Vedas (*Ṛgveda*, *Sāmaveda*, *Yajurveda*).
*   **daṇḍanīti** f. = दण्डनीति : politics (a *tatpuruṣa* from *daṇḍa* m. = "stick, power, rule, punishment" and *nīti* f. "[right] guidance").
*   **dvijāti** 3 = द्विजाति = *dvija* 3 = द्विज : "twice-born".
*   **dveṣa** m. = द्वेष : hatred.
*   **maitrī** f. = मैत्री : friendship, friendliness, kind benevolence.
*   **rāga** m. = राग : (red) color, passion, love.
*   **lobha** m. = लोभ : greed, avarice.
*   **varṇa** m. = वर्ण : color, category, estate (caste).
*   **vārttā** f. = वार्त्ता : livelihood, economics.
*   **vidyā** f. = विद्या : knowledge, science.
*   **avidyā** f. = अविद्या : ignorance, nescience.
*   **ca** = च : and (placed after the word it connects; if several words are connected by *ca*, it stands after the first word of that word complex; if several words are connected, *ca* can stand after each one, but preferably only after the last member).
*   **ca ... ca** = च ... च : both ... and.

## 5.5. Exercises

A) Translate the following sentences and compounds and resolve the *Dvandvas* occurring in them into Sanskrit:

1.  *catvāro varṇā brāhmaṇakṣatriyavaiśyaśūdrāḥ.* (Āpastambīyadharmasūtra I,1,1,4 = Vāsiṣṭhadharmaśāstra II,1)
    चत्वारो वर्णा ब्राह्मणक्षत्रियवैश्यशूद्राः ||
    Explanation: *catvāras* = चत्वारस् = "four"

2.  *trayo varṇā dvijātayo brāhmaṇakṣatriyavaiśyāḥ.* (Vāsiṣṭhadharmaśāstra II,1)
    त्रयो वर्णा द्विजातयो ब्राह्मणक्षत्रियवैश्याः ||
    Explanation: *trayas* = त्रयस् = "three"

3.  *sāmavedargvedayajurvedās trayī.* (Kauṭilīya-arthaśāstra 1.3.1.) (In proper Sanskrit: *sāmārgyajurvedās trayī*)
    सामवेदर्ग्वेदयजुर्वेदास्त्रयी ||

4.  The three enemies of man that form the gate to hell (Viṣṇusmṛti 33,1+6): *kāmakrodhalobhāḥ*
    कामक्रोधलोभाः ||

5.  *maitrīkaruṇāmuditopekṣāś catvāro brahmavihārāḥ.*
    मैत्रीकरुणामुदितोपेक्षाश्चत्वारो ब्रह्मविहाराः ||
    Explanation: *brahmavihāra*: "Abodes of Brahma," also called "Incommensurables": boundless attitudes. They represent Buddhist forms of meditation with which the meditator gradually "radiates" through all of reality. These four also play a role in Yoga (*Yogasūtra* 1,33) in calming the consciousness.

6.  *avidyāsmitārāgadveṣābhiniveṣāḥ pañca kleśāḥ.* (Yogasūtra 2,3)
    अविद्यास्मितारागद्वेषाभिनिवेषाः पञ्च क्लेशाः ||
    Explanation: *pañca* = "five"

7.  *ānvīkṣikītrayīvārttādaṇḍanitayo vidyāḥ.* (After Kauṭilīya-arthaśāstra 1.2.1.)
    आन्वीक्षिकीत्रयीवार्त्तादण्डनितयो विद्याः ||
