# Exercise 16

## Exercise

A) Convert the following expressions into Tatpuruṣa compounds and translate them:

1. देवस्य पुरुषः (The man of the god) -> **devapuruṣaḥ**
2. गुणवती ब्राह्मणी (The virtuous Brahmin woman) -> **guṇavadbrāhmaṇī**
3. सत्यवान्ब्राह्मणः (The truthful Brahmin) -> **satyavadbrāhmaṇaḥ**
4. पशुमन्तो जनाः (The people possessing cattle) -> **paśumajjannāḥ**
5. शिवen रक्षिता बाला (The girl protected by Shiva) -> **śivarakṣitabālā**
6. रामेण पीतं जलम् (The water drunk by Rama) -> **rāmapītajalam**
7. देवानां स्तुतिः (The praise of the gods) -> **devastutiḥ**

B) Dissolve the compounds in the following sentences and translate:

1. पुण्यवद्वैश्यपुत्रो देवेन्द्रलोकं गच्छति ।
   (पुण्यवान्वैश्यस्य पुत्रो देवानामिन्द्रस्य लोकं गच्छति ।)
   The virtuous son of the Vaiśya goes to the world of the Indra [king] of the gods.
2. पुण्यकरणं स्वर्गमार्गः ।
   (पुण्यस्य करणं स्वर्गस्य मार्गः ।)
   Doing meritorious deeds is the path to heaven.
3. द्विजदासा इति शूद्रा उच्यन्ते ।
   (द्विजानां दासा इति शूद्रा उच्यन्ते ।)
   Śūdras are called "servants of the Twice-born".

## Declension Patterns (Review)

Memorize these paradigms! They are the foundation of Sanskrit grammar.

### 1. Masculine a-stems (nara)
| Case | Singular | Plural |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Nom. | naraḥ | narāḥ |
| Acc. | naram | narān |
| Inst. | narena | naraiḥ |
| Gen. | narasya | narāṇām |

### 2. Neuter a-stems (phala)
| Case | Singular | Plural |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Nom. | phalam | phalāni |
| Acc. | phalam | phalāni |
| Inst. | phalena | phalaiḥ |
| Gen. | phalasya | phalānām |

[... etc. for other patterns like kṣatriyā, ari, mati, guru, dhenu, devī ...]

## Philosophical Texts

Translate and dissolve the compounds:

1. योगश्चित्तवृत्तिनिरोधः ॥योगसूत्र १.२॥
   (योगः चित्तस्य वृत्तेः निरोधः)
   Yoga is the cessation (*nirodha*) of the fluctuations (*vṛtti*) of the mind (*citta*).

2. स्वधर्मो ब्राह्मणस्याध्ययनमध्यापनं यजनं याजनं दानं प्रतिग्रहश्च ॥
   The specific duty (*svadharma*) of a Brahmin is: Vedic study, teaching the Veda, sacrificing for oneself, sacrificing for others, giving [alms], and receiving [gifts].
