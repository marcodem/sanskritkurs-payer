# Exercise 20

## Exercise

A) Translate and dissolve the compounds in Sanskrit:

1. इन्द्रशत्र्वनार्या देवेन्द्रेण जीयन्ते ॥१॥
   (इन्द्रः शत्रुः येषां ते अनार्याः देवानाम् इन्द्रेण जीयन्ते ।)
   The King of the Gods conquers the non-Aryans, whose enemy is Indra.
2. शूरबलक्षत्रिययोधः शूरपुत्रमिच्छति ॥२॥
   (शूरस्य बलं यस्य सः क्षत्रियः एव योधः शूरं पुत्रम् इच्छति ॥)
   The Kṣatriya warrior, possessing the strength of a hero, desires a heroic son.
3. सुदुर्गममार्गेण स्वर्गं गम्यते । सुगमस्तु नरकमार्गः ॥३॥
   (सुदुर्गमेन मार्गेण स्वर्गं गम्यते । सुगमः तु नरकस्य मार्गः ॥)
   Heaven is reached by a very difficult path; but the path to hell is easy.
4. मृतपुत्रब्राह्मणो रोदिति ॥४॥
   (मृतः पुत्रः यस्य सः ब्राह्मणो रोदिति ॥)
   The Brahmin woman whose son has died cries.

## Bahuvrīhi Compound Analysis

Dissolve these compounds as Bahuvrīhi (possessive), Dvandva (coordinative), or Tatpuruṣa (determinative):

1. **indraśatravaḥ**: (indraḥ śatruḥ yeṣām) Those whose enemy is Indra.
2. **duṣkulāyāḥ**: (duṣṭaṃ kulaṃ yasyāḥ) Of her, whose family is wicked.
3. **jātimātrasya**: (jātiḥ eva mātraṃ yasya) Of him who is only [a member of his] caste [by birth].
4. **prāptodakāḥ**: (prāptam udakaṃ yaiḥ) Those who have reached the water.
5. **mṛtaputraḥ**: (mṛtaḥ putraḥ yasya) He whose son is dead.
6. **vītamohaḥ**: (vītaḥ mohaḥ yasya) He whose delusion has departed.
7. **vigate-nayanam**: (vigataṃ nayanaṃ yasya) Blind (one whose eyes are gone).
8. **labdhadhanānām**: (labdhaṃ dhanaṃ yeṣām) Of those who have obtained wealth.
9. **sakalpaḥ**: (saḥ kalpaḥ yeṣām) Having the same ritual/rules.

## Philosophical Nuggets

### 1. Yoga Sūtra (1.33)
मैत्रीकरुणामुदितोपेक्षाणां सुखदुःखपुण्यापुण्यविषयाणां भावनतश्चित्तप्रसादनम् ॥
"The clarification of the mind (*citta-prasādanam*) comes from cultivating friendliness (*maitrī*), compassion (*karuṇā*), joy (*muditā*), and equanimity (*upekṣā*) toward happiness, suffering, virtue, and vice."

### 2. Sāṃkhya Sūtra (1.1)
त्रिविधदुःखात्यन्तनिवृत्तिरत्यन्तपुरुषार्थः ॥
"The ultimate goal of man is the total cessation of the threefold suffering."
*(Threefold suffering: from oneself, from objects, from the gods/nature.)*

### 3. Nyāya Sūtra (1.21-22)
बाधनालक्षणं दुःखम् । तदत्यन्तविमोक्षो ऽपवर्गः ॥
"Suffering is characterized by affliction. Liberation (*apavarga*) is the absolute release from it."
