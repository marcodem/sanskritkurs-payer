# Lesson 14

## 14.1. Expressing the Relationship between Nouns: The Genitive (ṣaṣṭhī f. = षष्टी = sixth case ending)

To express the relationship between two nouns, the genitive (ṣaṣṭī f. "sixth case ending") is employed. The genitive differs from all other cases in that—with few exceptions—it does not serve to further specify the action expressed by the verb, but rather to express the relationship between persons or things denoted by nouns. The genitive frequently answers the question: Whose?

Examples:

> कवेः पुत्रः "The poet's son"
> 
> धन्स्य लोभः "Greed for wealth"
	
> नगरस्यार्धम् "Half of the city"
> 
> रामस्य कृतम् "The / A deed of Rāma"

The standard word order is:

Determining word in the genitive — Substantive being further specified in another case

Genitive and PPP:

In the case of PPPs, the genitive may stand in place of the instrumental (tṛtīyā) to denote the agent (kartṛ). In the construction using the instrumental, the PPP is regarded as a passive verbal form (where the element in the instrumental denotes the agent); in the construction using the genitive, the PPP is regarded as a noun or adjective (where the element in the genitive is not strictly considered the agent).

According to Pāṇini 2.3.67, in a PPP, the genitive is used if the PPP carries a present-tense meaning (see above), whereas the instrumental is used if the PPP carries a past-tense meaning:

रामस्येष्टं फलम् "The fruit desired by Rāma = The fruit that Rāma currently desires"

रामेणेष्टं फलम् "The fruit that Rāma has desired"

Naturally, the genitive is used with a neuter singular PPP when it is employed as a verbal abstract—that is, as a noun.

## 14.2. Formation of Genitive Forms (ṣāṣṭhī f. = षष्ठी)

For verbal stems involving stem gradation, the genitive is formed from the weak stem.

Genitive of stems ending in a consonant:

Following a consonant, the regular genitive endings are:

*   Singular: -as
*   Plural: -ām

Morphological Identity:

![](/images/lekt1401.jpg)

With the exception of all masculine and neuter stems ending in -a and the pronouns, the form of the genitive singular is identical to the form of the ablative (pañcamī, "fifth case ending") singular!

![](/images/lekt1401.jpg)

Note that for stems ending in a consonant, the ablative and genitive singular are identical to the accusative plural of masculine and feminine stems!

## 14.3. Further Remarks on the Use of the Genitive (षष्ठी)

Contrary to the fundamental rule stated above, the genitive is employed to express the object of certain verbs, for instance, with verbs of remembering:

> देवानां स्मरति "He remembers the gods"

In all such verbs, however, the object may also appear in the accusative:

> देवान्स्मरति "He remembers the gods"
