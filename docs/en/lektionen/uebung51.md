# Exercise 51

1. Kauṭilīya Arthaśāstra 1, 3, 9–12: Duties of the Ashramas (āśramadharma)

गृहस्तस्य स्वधर्माजीवस्तुल्यैरसमानार्षिभिर्वैवाह्यमृतुगामित्वं देवपित्रातिथिपूजा भृत्येषु त्यागः शेषभोजनं च ।९।  
ब्रह्मचारिणः स्वाध्यायो ऽग्निकार्याभिषेकौ भैक्षाव्रतित्वमाचार्ये प्राणान्तिकी वृत्तिस्तदभावे गुरुपुत्रे सब्रह्मचारिणि वा ।१०।  
वानप्रस्थस्य ब्रह्मचर्यं भूमौ शय्या जाटाजिनधारणमग्निहोत्राभिषेकौ देवतापित्रतिथिपूजा वन्यश्चाहारः ।११।  
प्रव्राजकस्य जितेन्द्रियत्वमनारम्भो निष्किंचनत्वं सङ्गत्यागो भैक्षाव्रतमनेकत्रारण्ये च वासो बाह्याभ्यन्तरं च शौचम् ॥१२॥

> A householder (gṛhastha) should:
> 
> *   have a livelihood corresponding to his specific dharma
> *   enter into a marriage alliance with equals, but those having a different Ṛṣi (= from a different Gotra)
> *   have sexual intercourse during the fertile periods of the wife
> *   honor gods, ancestors, and guests
> *   be generous toward his dependents
> *   and eat what remains.
> 
> A celibate Vedic student (brahmacārin) should:
> 
> *   study the Veda
> *   ritually maintain the fire
> *   perform ablutions
> *   keep the vow of living on alms-food
> *   stay with the Ācārya and care for him until his death, or with the son of the master, or with a fellow student.
> 
> A forest dweller (vānaprastha) should:
> 
> *   live celibately
> *   lie on the ground
> *   wear matted hair and an antelope skin
> *   perform fire sacrifices and ablutions
> *   honor gods, ancestors, and guests
> *   feed on forest products.
> 
> A homeless wandering ascetic (pravrājaka) should:
> 
> *   conquer his senses
> *   undertake nothing (anārambha)
> *   be without any possession
> *   give up all attachment
> *   keep the vow of living on alms-food
> *   dwell in many places in the wilderness
> *   be pure internally and externally.

2. Kauṭilīya Arthaśāstra 1, 3, 16–17: On the necessity of respecting the Varṇāśramadharma

तस्मात्स्वधर्मं भूतानाम्  
राजा न व्यभिचारयेत् ।  
स्वधर्मं संदधानो हि  
प्रेत्य चेह च नन्दति ॥१६॥  
व्यवस्थितार्यमर्यादः  
कृतवर्णाश्रमस्थितिः ।  
त्रय्याभिरक्षितो लोकः  
प्रसीदति न सीदति ॥१७॥

> Therefore, the king should not let creatures deviate from their specific dharma. For he who fulfills his specific dharma rejoices after death and in this life. When the noble boundary is firmly established for the noble ones, when the classes (varṇa) and stages of life (āśrama) are made constant, then the world protected by the three Vedas prospers and does not perish.

3. Bāṇa (7th cent. AD): Kādambarī, ed. K.P. Parab, 1896, p. 65ff.: Considerations of the parrot Vaiśampāyana on the hunter's (śabara) life:

आसीच्च मे मनसि -- अहो मोहप्रायमेतेषां जीवितं साधुजनगर्हितं च चरितम् । तथा हि । पुरुषपिशितोपहारे धर्मबुद्धिः , अहारः साधुजनगर्हितो मधुमांसादिः , श्रमो मृगया , शास्त्रं शिवारुतम् , समुपदेष्टारः सद्सतां कौशिकाः , प्रज्ञा शकुनिज्ञानम् , परिचिताः श्वानः , राज्यं शून्यास्वटवीषु , आपानकमुत्सवः , मित्राणि क्रुरकर्मसाधनानि धनूंषि , सहाया विषदिग्धमुखा भुजंगा इव सायकाः , गीतमुत्सादकारि मुग्धमृगाणाम् , कलत्राणि बन्दीगृहीताः परयोषितः , क्रूरात्मभिः शार्दूलैः सह संवासः , पशुरुधिरेण देवतार्चनम् , मांसेन बलिकर्म , चौर्येण जीवनम् , भूषणानि भुजंगमणयः , वनकरिमदैरङ्गरागः , यस्मिन्नेव कानने निवसन्ति तदेवोत्ख्यातमूलमशेषतः कुर्वत इति चिन्तयत्येव मयि शबरसेनापतिः समुपाविशत् ॥

> And it became conscious to me: "Oh! Their [the hunters'] life consists mainly of delusion, and their conduct is blamed by the good. Namely thus: they consider the offering of human flesh as true religion; their food consists of honey-wine, meat, and the like, which is blamed by the good; their effort is hunting; their textbook is the howling of jackals; owls are their teachers of good and evil; their wisdom is bird-lore; their intimates are dogs; their kingdom is in empty forests; their festival is a drinking bout; their friends are bows that perform cruel deeds; their companions are arrows whose tips are smeared with poison like snakes; their song brings destruction to the confused wild game; their wives are the captured young women of others; they dwell together with cruel tigers; they worship the deities with animal blood; they offer meat as sacrificial cakes; they live by robbery; their ornaments are snake-gems; they rub their limbs with the ichor of wild elephants; every forest in which they settle, they uproot completely." While I was reflecting thus, the general of the Śabaras entered before me.
