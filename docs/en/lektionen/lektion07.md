# Lesson 7

### Weekly Verse

धर्मो जयति नधर्मः  
सत्यं जयति नानृतम् |  
क्षमा जयति न क्रोधो  
देवो जयति नासुरः ||

*dharmo jayati nādharmaḥ*  
*satyaṃ jayati nānṛtam |*  
*kṣamā jayati na krodho*  
*devo jayati nāsuraḥ ||*

Righteousness triumphs, not unrighteousness;  
Truth triumphs, not untruth;  
Forbearance triumphs, not anger;  
God triumphs, not the anti-god.

## 7.1. The Verbal Sentence with a Direct Object

| Schema: (Agent = *kartṛ* m. = कर्तृ) - direct object (*karman* n. = कर्मन्) - Verb |
| :--- |

e.g.,
*   *rāmaḥ phalaṃ khādati* = रामः फलं खादति = "Rāma eats (chews) a fruit."
*   *brāhmaṇo devaṃ yajati* = ब्राह्मणो देवं यजति = "The (a) Brahmin venerates a god with a sacrifice (on behalf of someone else)."

| If the verb is in the *Parasmaipada* or *Ātmanepada*, the direct object (*karman* n. = कर्मन्) generally stands in the **Accusative** (objective case, *dvitīyā* f. = द्वितीया = "second case-ending"). |
| :--- |

The forms *enam* एनम् etc. belong to the stem **enad = एनद्**, which forms endings only in certain cases. In the cases where forms of this stem occur, they are used instead of the forms of *etad* एतद् and *idam* इदम् when the object has already been mentioned in what preceded. e.g., *ayaṃ devaḥ, enaṃ yajante* = अयं देवः | एनं यजन्ते || = "He is a god. They sacrifice to him (venerate him)."

### 7.2.2. Use of the Accusative (*dvitīyā* f. = द्वितीया = "second case-ending")

The Accusative (*dvitīyā* f. = द्वितीया) designates:

1.  **In a non-passive verbal sentence**: the direct object (*karman* n. = कर्मन्) of the action expressed by the verb:
    e.g.,
    *   *devaṃ yajati* = देवं यजति = "He venerates a god with a sacrifice."
    *   *phalam āpnoti* = फलमाप्नोति = "He attains the fruit (e.g., of his action)."
2.  **With verbs of motion**: verbs expressing going, movement, leading, or similar towards something; here the accusative designates the goal of the movement:
    e.g.,
    *   *narakaṃ gacchati* = नरकं गच्छति = "He goes to hell."

Further uses of the Accusative will be covered later.

## 7.3. Sandhi of Final *-n*

Final *-n*:

1.  **Before voiced palatal and retroflex stops and before *ś-***: is replaced by the nasal corresponding to these sounds:
    *   *-n* before *j-, jh-, ñ-, ś-* → **-ñ**
    *   *-n* before *ḍ-, ḍh-, ṇ-, ṣ-* → **-ṇ**
    e.g., *kṣatriyān + jayati* » *kṣatriyāñ jayati* = क्षत्रियाञ्जयति = "He defeats the Kṣatriyas."
    Initial *ś-* is then usually replaced by *ch-*:
    e.g., *gurūn + śṛṇoti* » *gurūñ chṛṇoti* (besides *gurūñ śṛṇoti*) = गुरूञ्छृणोति (गुरूञ्शृणोति) = "He hears the teachers." / "He listens to the teachers."
2.  **Before initial *l-***: is replaced by **-l** with a nasal sign (written as **-ल्** with *anunāsika* **ँ**):
    e.g., *paśūn + labhate* » *paśūṃl labhate* = पशूल्ँलभते = "He obtains livestock."
3.  **Before initial voiceless palatals, retroflexes, or dentals**: is replaced by *anusvāra* + the sibilant corresponding to these sounds:
    *   *-n* before *c-, ch-* → **-ṃś**
    *   *-n* before *ṭ-, ṭh-* → **-ṃṣ**
    *   *-n* before *t-, th-* → **-ṃs**
    e.g., *devān + ca* » *devāṃś ca* = देवांश्च = "and the gods (Acc.)"
    e.g., *devān + tu* » *devāṃs tu* = देवांस्तु = "but the gods (Acc.)"
4.  Except after a short vowel (see later), final *-n* remains unchanged in all other cases.

## 7.4. The Neuter (*napuṃsaka* n. = नपुंसक)

In the neuter, the forms for the **Nominative** (*prathamā*) and **Accusative** (*dvitīyā*) are identical.

### 7.4.1. Neuter of *-a* Stems

| Nominative / Accusative singular ending: **-m** |
| :--- |
e.g., *phala* n. = फल = "fruit": Nom. / Acc. sg. *phalam* = फलम्

| Nominative / Accusative plural ending: **-āni** |
| :--- |
e.g., *phala* n. = फल: Nom. / Acc. pl. *phalāni* = फलानि

## 7.5. Athematic Present Classes

The 5th present class forms a so-called **athematic present stem**, meaning the present stem does not end in the "thematic vowel" *-a* as it does in the thematic present classes (1st, 4th, 6th, 10th class).

Athematic present classes have **stem gradation**, meaning there are two forms of the present stem:
1.  **Strong stem**: full grade (rarely lengthened grade).
2.  **Weak stem**: zero grade.

The **strong stem** is found in:
1.  Indicative Singular Parasmaipada of the Present (*laṭ*) and Imperfect (*laṅ*).
2.  All first persons of the Imperative (*loṭ*) Parasmaipada and Ātmanepada.
3.  The third person singular Imperative (*loṭ*) Parasmaipada.

All other forms take the **weak present stem**.

### 7.5.1. Primary Endings of the 3rd Person Plural with Athematic Stems

For athematic present stems, the primary endings of the 3rd person plural are:
*   Parasmaipada: **-anti**
*   Ātmanepada: **-ate**

### 7.5.2. The 5th Present Class (*svādi* = स्वादि = "su etc.")

*   **Strong stem**: (usually) zero-grade root (as listed) + **-no-**
*   **Weak stem**: (usually) zero-grade root (as listed) + **-nu-**

Before vocalic endings, *-nu-* is replaced by **-nv-** for roots ending in a vowel; for roots ending in a consonant, *-nu-* is replaced by **-nuv-** before vocalic endings.

Examples:

| Root | Strong Stem | Weak Stem | Weak Stem before Vowel |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **āp** 5 P आप् "to reach, attain" | *āp-no*<br>3rd sg. P. *āpnoti* आप्नोति | *āp-nu* | *āp-nuv*<br>3rd pl. P. *āpnuvanti* आप्नुवन्ति |
| **aś** 5 Ā अश् "to reach, attain" | | *aś-nu*<br>3rd sg. Ā. *aśnute* अश्नुते | *aś-nuv*<br>3rd pl. Ā. *aśnuvate* अश्नुवते |
| **su** 5 U सु "to press out" | *su-no*<br>3rd sg. P. *sunoti* सुनोति | *su-nu*<br>3rd sg. Ā. *sunute* सुनुते | *su-nv*<br>3rd pl. P. *sunvanti* सुन्वन्ति<br>3rd pl. Ā. *sunvate* सुन्वते |

Note: **śru** 5 P श्रु "to hear"
*   Strong stem: *śṛ-ṇo* (3rd sg. P. *śṛṇoti* शृणोति)
*   Weak stem: *śṛ-ṇu*
*   Weak stem before vowel: *śṛ-ṇv* (3rd pl. P. *śṛṇvanti* शृण्वन्ति)
(The *ṇ* instead of *n* is due to regular word sandhi, see later).

## 7.6. Vocabulary

Learn the following words:

*   **aś** 5 Ā (*aśnute*) अश् अश्नुते : to reach, to arrive at, to attain.
*   **āp** 5 P (*āpnoti*) आप् आप्नोति : to reach, to attain.
*   **kup** 4 P (*kupyati*) कुप् कुप्यति : to be angry.
*   **krudh** 4 P (*krudhyati*) क्रुध् क्रुध्यति : to be angry.
*   **khād** 1 P (*khādati*) खाद् खादति : to chew, to eat.
*   **śru** 5 P (*śṛṇoti*) श्रु शृणोति : to hear (something: Accusative, someone: Genitive or Accusative; about: Accusative; from someone: Genitive, Ablative, Instrumental).
*   **su** 5 U (*sunoti*) सु सुनोति : to press out.
*   **soma** m. सोम : the soma-drink, Soma; moon. (The plant from which Soma was pressed is still debated).
    ![](/images/lekt0701.jpg)
    Fig.: Was this the Vedic Soma plant? Fly agaric (*Amanita muscaria*).
*   **phala** n. फल : fruit (also in a figurative sense: [karmic] fruit of an action).
*   **nṛtya** n. नृत्य : dance.
*   **svarga** m. स्वर्ग : heaven.
*   **naraka** m. नरक : hell.
*   **aṅga** n. अङ्ग : limb of the body, constituent part; also = *vedāṅga* = वेदाङ्ग.
*   **gam** 1 P (*gacchati*) गम् गच्छति : to go (listed in the first class, but is actually formed with the suffix *-ccha-*: root zero-grade *gm* → *ga-ccha-ti*).
