# Lesson 18

## 1. Subhāṣitam

> नास्ति कामसमो व्याधिर् — No malady is comparable to desire;
> नास्ति मोहसमो रिपुः । — No adversary to delusion;
> नास्ति क्रोधसमो वह्निर् — No conflagration to anger;
> नास्ति ज्ञानसमं सुखम् ॥ — No bliss to knowledge.

## 18.2. Determinative Compounds (तत्पुरुष) with Adverbial Prefixes

As an adverbial (indeclinable) prefix, the following may be used:

*   Adverbs
*   Prepositions
*   Particles
*   Other indeclinables

> Such *Tatpuruṣa* usually belong to the class of *Nityasamāsa* = नित्यसमास (m. "permanent compound"), i.e., compounds that cannot be decomposed at all, or can only be decomposed by means of words not present within the compound itself.

Examples:

अति "beyond/furthermore":

> अतिगुरु 3: exceedingly heavy, exceedingly weighty
> 
> अतिपुरुष m.: outstanding man, superman, hero
> 
> अतिस्तुति f.: excessive praise
> 
> अत्युक्ति f.: excessive speech, exaggeration

The most important adverbial prefixes are *a-/an-*, *su-*, and *dus-*.

अ (before a consonant), अन् (before a vowel): un-: stands in the compound in place of न "not".

Examples:

> अनृत n. = नर्तम् (= न + ऋतम्): untruth, falsehood
> 
> अकृत 3 = न कृत 3: not done, undone
> 
> अब्रआह्मणी f. = न ब्राह्मणी : a non-Brahmin woman
> 
> अदेव m. = न देवः : a non-god, non-deity

सु "well, good"; during the decomposition of the compound, commentators replace it with an adjective meaning "good" (e.g., सुष्टु 3, शोभन 3).

 Examples:

> सुकवि m.: a good poet
> 
> सुकृत n.: a good deed
> 
> सुखादित 3: well-chewed
> 
> सुदुःख n.: great suffering/distress

दुस् "bad, ill" (note Sandhi!).

Examples:

> दुर्नय m.: bad leadership, bad conduct
> 
> दुष्करण n.: bad deed, difficult deed

The following rule applies:

Sound change of *-s* in compounds

Contrary to sentence Sandhi, in the final syllable of the prefix in compounds:

\-s after -i- or -u- before an unvoiced guttural or labial $\rightarrow$ -ṣ

Therefore: दुष्करण

### 18.2.1. Compounds of the type सुकर / दुष्कर

Compounds of the type सुकर / दुष्कर primarily carry the meaning of passive possibility:

Examples:

> सुकर 3: easy to do
> 
> सुगम 3: easy to go/accessible
> 
> दुर्गम 3: difficult to go/inaccessible
> 
> सुदुर्गम 3: very difficult to go/extremely inaccessible

### 18.2.2. Meanings of nañ-Tatpuruṣa (a- / an-)

*Tatpuruṣa* with *a- / an-* can possess the following meanings:

1.  Similarity (तत्सादृश्यम्):  
    अब्राह्मणः = a Kṣatriya or Vaiśya who wears the sacred thread (यज्ञोपवीत n.) and thus resembles a Brahmin without being one
2.  Absence, non-existence (तदभावः):  
    अज्ञानम् = ignorance (absence of knowledge)
3.  Alterity/Difference (तदन्यत्वम्):  
    अपटम् = something that is not cloth/garment (पट m: fabric, garment)
4.  Smallness (तदल्पता):  
    अनुदरम् = a small belly (a "non-belly")
5.  Disapproval/Impropriety (अप्राशास्त्यम्):  
    अकालः = untimely/unseasonable
6.  Enmity/Opposition (निरोधः):  
    असुरः = anti-god (according to the false etymology *a-sura*)

The following verse summarizes these meanings:

> तत्सादृश्यमभावश्च  
> तदन्यत्वं तदल्पता ।  
> अप्राशास्त्यं निरोधश्च  
> नजर्थाः षत्प्रकीर्तिताः ॥
> 
> The six meanings of *nañ* (= a-/an-) are known as ....

## 18.3. Verbal Compounds

> उपसर्गेण धात्वर्थो  
> बलादन्यत्र नीयते ।  
> गङ्गासलिलमाधुर्यं  
> सागरेण यथाम्भसा ॥

Candrakīrti: Prasannapadā, S. 2, ll. 14f.

Through a prefix, the meaning of a root is forcibly altered, just as the sweetness of the Ganges water is [altered] by seawater.

In Sanskrit, verbs can be combined with prefixes (उपसर्ग m.). In such cases, sentence sandhi (Satzsandhi) usually applies. Prefixes can, in certain instances, significantly modify the meaning of the root, such that in many cases, the meaning of a root combined with a prefix must be learned separately. Verbs with a prefix may possess a different conjugation class (P, Ā) than the simple root. Multiple prefixes may be placed before a root simultaneously.

Some important prefixes are:

upa "to, towards, against"

Examples:

> upagam 1 upagacchati : to go towards, to approach
> 
> upadiś 6 upadiśati : to point out, to instruct, to advise
> 
> upapad 4 Ā upapadate : to attain, to reach
> 
> > PPP upapanna 3: endowed with (Instrumental)
> 
> upalabh 1 Ā upalabhate : to perceive, to obtain

pra "forward, forth"

Examples:

> prāp (pra-āp) 5 prāpnoti : to attain, to reach
> 
> pradiś 6 pradiśati : to show
> 
> prabudh 4 Ā prabudhyate : to awaken, to realize
> 
> prabhū 1 prabhavati : to emerge, to protrude, to have power over (Gen., Loc., Dat.)
> 
> pravac 2 pravakti PPP prokta (pra + ukta) : to declare, to communicate, to utter
> 
> pravad 1 pravadati : to utter, to designate as, to declare as
> 
> prastu 2 prastauti : to praise highly, to extol, to bring up, to begin

vi "apart, away, asunder, -de"

Examples:

> vigam 1 vigacchati : to depart, to pass away, to vanish
> 
> viji 1 Ā (!) vijayate : to conquer
> 
> vimuc 6 vimuncati : to release, to liberate
> 
> vivad 1 vivadati : to dispute, to argue, to narrate
> 
> vismṛ 1 vismarati : to forget
> 
> vihan 2 vihanti : to smash, to destroy, to annihilate

sam "together, with"

Examples:

> samas 2 Ā samāste : to sit together, to reside, to dwell
> 
> sami 2 sameti : to come together, to unite
> 
> sangam 1 Ā (!) sangacchate : to come together, to meet (amicably or hostilely), to have sexual intercourse with (Acc.)
> 
> sanjan 4 Ā saṃjāyate : to be born, to arise
> 
> > PPP saṃjāta 3: born, arisen, become
> 
> sambudh 4 Ā sambudhyate : to fully awaken (to the truth)
> 
> sampad 4 Ā sampadyate : to be granted to someone, to succeed
> 
> > PPP sampanna 3: endowed with (Instrumental)

The root kṛ "to do" also exhibits the form skṛ when combined with the prefixes sam, upa, apa, and pari.

Example:

> sam-kṛ 8 saṃskaroiti : to prepare, to consecrate for sacrifice, to sanctify
> 
> > PPP saṃskṛta 3: prepared for sacrifice; saṃskṛta n.: Sanskrit: the language prepared/refined for sacrifice; counterpart prākṛta 3: common, ordinary; prākṛta n.: common language, Prakrit (designation for the vernaculars and lingua francas related to Sanskrit)

### 18.3.1. Nominal Formations from Verbal Compounds

Nouns can be formed from roots with prefixes by means of kṛt-suffixes.

Examples:

sam-kṛ + -a = saṃskāra m.: consecration, preparation; rites of passage = designation for the ceremonies that accompany the various stages of life from conception to death (cf. the excellent summary in Basha, Wonder S. 160–170!)
