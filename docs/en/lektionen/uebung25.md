## Sentences & Compounds

Translate and analyze the compounds:

1. गुर्वादेशाद्रामो ग्रामान्नगरं गत्वा साधुगृहं प्रविश्य साधुमुपस्थायालं क्रोधेनेति वक्ति ॥१॥
   Following the teacher's instruction (*gurv-ādeśāt*), Rāma went from the village to the city, entered the holy man's house (*sādhu-gṛham*), stood before him, and said: "Enough of anger!" (*alaṃ krodhena*).
2. क्षत्रिया जनाञ्छत्रुभ्यो रक्षितुमर्हन्तीति क्षत्रियधर्मः ॥३॥
   It is the Kṣatriya duty (*kṣatriya-dharmaḥ*) that Kṣatriyas should protect the people from enemies (*śatrubhyaḥ*).
3. बुद्ध्याश्चार्हन्तश्च दुःखान्मुक्ताः । मुञ्चन्ती बुद्धिर्हि तैः प्राप्ता ॥६॥
   The Buddhas and Arhants are liberated from suffering (*duḥkhāt*). They have indeed attained the liberating insight.
4. विष्णुर्भक्तान्मरणात्पाति ॥११॥
   Viṣṇu protects his devotees from death (*maraṇāt*).
5. लोकादधिको हरिः ॥१७॥
   Hari (Viṣṇu) is greater than the world (*lokāt adhikaḥ*).

## Subhāṣitas

1. यतो यतो निवर्तते / ततस्ततो विमुच्यते । / निवर्तनाद्धि सर्वतो / न वेत्ति दुःखमण्वपि ॥१॥
   "From whatever one detaches oneself, from that one is liberated. By detaching from everything, one knows no suffering, not even as small as an atom."

2. मानाद्वा यदि वा लोभात् / क्रोधाद्वा यदि वा भयात् । / यो न्यायमन्यथा ब्रूते / स याति नरकं नरः ॥२॥
   "Whether out of pride, greed, anger, or fear—the man who gives a false judgment goes to hell."

3. भवन्ति नरकाः पापात् / पापं दारिद्र्यसंभवम् । / दारिद्र्यमप्रदानेन ॥३॥
   "Hells arise from sin; sin arises from poverty; poverty arises from not giving [charity]."
