# Lesson 42

## 42.1. Stems in *-ṛ*

### 42.1.1. Stems with Extended Grade in the Strong Stem

Most masculine nouns in *-ṛ* follow this pattern, specifically **agent nouns** formed with the suffix **-tṛ**. It also includes these kinship terms:
*   **naptṛ** m.: "grandson"
*   **bhartṛ** m.: "husband" (lit. "supporter, maintainer")

**Formation Rules:**
*   **Strong Stem**: *-ār*
*   **Weak Stem**:
    *   Before vowels: *-r*
    *   Before consonants: *-ṛ*

#### Declension of *dātṛ* m. ("giver")
| Case | Singular | Plural |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nom.** | *dātā* | *dātāras* |
| **Acc.** | *dātāram* | *dātṝn* |
| **Instr.** | *dātrā* | *dātṛbhis* |
| **Dat.** | *dātre* | *dātṛbhyas* |
| **Abl.** | *dātus* | *dātṛbhyas* |
| **Gen.** | *dātus* | *dātṝṇām* |
| **Loc.** | *dātari* | *dātṛṣu* |

#### Feminine Nouns
*   **svasṛ** f. ("sister") is declined like *dātṛ*, with one exception:
    *   **Acc. pl.** is **svasṝs**.

### 42.1.2. Primary (*kṛt*) Suffix *-tṛ*

The very common suffix **-tṛ** forms agent nouns (designating the *kartṛ*) from almost any root or causative stem.

**Formation:**
*   High-grade (*guṇa*) root + **-tṛ**
*   High-grade root / Causative stem + **-i-** + **-tṛ**

**Examples:**
*   **kartṛ** m.: "doer, agent"
*   **jetṛ** m.: "victor, winner"
*   **dhātṛ** m.: "creator"
*   **rakṣitṛ** m.: "protector"
*   **bodhayitṛ** m.: "one who awakens"
    ![](/images/lekt4201.jpg)
    Fig.: *ayaṃ bodhayitā* (This is the awakener)

**Feminine Formation:**
The feminine of agent nouns in *-tṛ* is formed in **-trī** (declined like *devī*), e.g., **kartrī** f. ("female doer").

### 42.1.3. Stems with High Grade in the Strong Stem

This pattern is followed by the following specific kinship terms:
*   **pitṛ** m.: "father"
*   **mātṛ** f.: "mother"
*   **duhitṛ** f.: "daughter"
*   **bhrātṛ** m.: "brother"
*   **devṛ** m.: "husband's brother"
*   **yātṛ** f.: "husband's brother's wife"
*   **nanāndṛ** f.: "husband's sister"

**Formation Rules:**
*   **Strong Stem**: *-ar*
*   **Weak Stem**:
    *   Before vowels: *-r*
    *   Before consonants: *-ṛ*

#### Declension Comparison:
| Case | Masculine (*pitṛ* "father") | Feminine (*mātṛ* "mother") |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| | **Sing. / Plur.** | **Sing. / Plur.** |
| **Nom.** | *pitā / pitaras* | *mātā / mātaras* |
| **Acc.** | *pitaram / pitṝn* | *mātaram / mātṝs* |
| | (Rest like *dātṛ*) | (Rest like *dātṛ*) |

### 42.1.4. Stems in *-ṛ* in Compounds

As the first member of a compound, nouns in *-ṛ* always use their weak stem:
*   Before consonants: **-ṛ**
*   Before vowels: **-r**

*   **bāṇa** m.: arrow, target.

*   **āṭopa** m.: pride, vanity.

*   **śaṃs** 1P (*śaṃsati*, PPP *śasta*): to praise, command, recite.

## 42.4. Translation Exercise: The Wedding of Sītā (*sītāvivāhaḥ*)

Once there was a king named Janaka in Mithilā. He had a daughter named Sītā, who was incomparable in beauty and character. Many princes, wishing to marry her, sent messengers to Janaka.

Janaka, however, wished to give her to a Kṣatriya prince endowed with heroic strength. Thus, he determined that she was to be won (*kretavyām*) by heroism. In his presence was a very heavy bow. Whoever could lift this bow and fit an arrow to it would marry his daughter—so Janaka promised.

Hearing this promise, hundreds of princes came. But not one of them was able even to move that bow. It is known that even Rāvaṇa, the lord of Laṅkā, approached with pride but returned in shame.

Seeing all the princes return, Janaka was lost in thought: "Who will be my daughter's husband?" Meanwhile, Śrī Rāma, the son of Daśaratha, the lord of Ayodhyā, was brought there by Viśvāmitra along with Lakṣmaṇa. By the word of the great sage Viśvāmitra, Śrī Rāma lifted the bow with ease, and as he was fitting an arrow to it, the bow broke into two.

![](/images/lekt4208.jpg)

"Excellent, excellent!" the people praised Rāma's heroism. King Janaka's heart was delighted. Then, having brought Daśaratha and others, he performed the wedding ceremony of Sītā and Rāma with great magnificence.

**Explanatory Notes:**
*   **Sītā** f.: Daughter of King Janaka. She appeared from the furrow (*sītā*) when the king was plowing the field.
    ![](/images/lekt4205.jpg)
*   **Mithilā** f.: Capital of Videha.
    ![](/images/lekt4206.jpg)
*   **Janaka** m.: King of Videha.
*   **Dhanus** n.: Bow.
*   **Rāvaṇa** m.: Ruler of Laṅkā and the Rākṣasas.
    ![](/images/lekt4207.jpg)
*   **Daśaratha** m.: King of Kosala; father of Rāma and Lakṣmaṇa.
*   **Viśvāmitra** m.: A sage (*ṛṣi*) who trained Rāma and Lakṣmaṇa.
