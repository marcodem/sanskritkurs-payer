# Lesson 21

## 21.1. Further Present Stems in *-nt*

### 21.1.1. Present Participle (*laṭ* Participle) Parasmaipada

This participle is a nominal formation from the present stem. It is an adjective that indicates that someone or something is doing what is expressed by the verbal root (+ preverb) while something else is happening. It can also express a continuous state.

Examples:
*   "While the father reads, he smokes" = "the reading father smokes."
*   "a being (= real, true) friend."

## 21.2. Sandhi for Final Nasals

After a short vowel, final nasals—except *-m*—are doubled before an initial vowel.

e.g.,
*   *jayan + ariḥ* → *jayannariḥ* "the victorious enemy"

## 21.3. Polite Forms of Address

In Sanskrit, one can address someone in the 2nd person singular without being impolite. However, to be polite, one can use a noun meaning "venerable" or similar, and put the verb in the 3rd person singular or plural, or use a passive construction. The levels of politeness in address are roughly:
2nd sg. → 2nd pl. → 3rd sg. with noun → 3rd pl. with noun.

The most important of these polite words is **bhavant**, fem.: **bhavatī**. It corresponds to the formal "you" (German *Sie*).

This *bhavant* is a contraction of *bhagavant*; its declension follows the nouns in *-vant* (see [Lesson 13](lektion13.md)). It is to be distinguished from the present participle P of *bhū* (to become), which is also *bhavant*: the Nom. sg. masc. of *bhavant* ("you") is *bhavān*, while that of the participle is *bhavan*.

Examples:
*   *kiṃ bhavān karoti?* = *kiṃ bhavatā kriyate?* = "What are you doing?"

More polite:
*   *kiṃ bhavantaḥ kurvanti?* = *kiṃ bhavadbhiḥ kriyate?*

Feminine:
*   *kiṃ bhavatī karoti?* = *kiṃ bhavatyā kriyate?*
*   *kiṃ bhavatyaḥ kurvanti?* = *kiṃ bhavatībhiḥ kriyate?*

Other words used similarly:
*   **ārya** (f.: **āryā**) "Noble one." e.g., *yadārya icchati* "What you wish."
*   **mahābhāga** "the one whose portion/luck is great = distinguished one." Often used by women when addressing or speaking about men of good standing. Very common in modern spoken Sanskrit.

To express not only politeness but also veneration, one uses **atrabhavant** for someone present or nearby, and **tatrabhavant** for someone absent or distant. These can be translated as "Your Excellence," "Your Reverence," etc.:
*   *kimatrabhavatyatrabhavatāṃ bhāryā?* = "Is the distinguished lady [present here] your wife?"
*   *kiṃ tatrabhavatāṃ kuśalavṛttam?* (in a letter or phone call) = "Are you well?"

## 21.4. Vocabulary

*   **bhaj** 1 U (*bhajati*, Pass. *bhajyate*, PPP *bhakta*): to allot/bestow something to someone (Acc.); to love, honor, venerate.
    From this:
    *   **bhakti** f.: devotion, loyalty, love (in a religious context: love and respect for a personal god).
    *   **bhāga** m.: portion, part.
    *   **bhaga** m.: (good) portion, luck, well-being, dignity.
    *   **bhagavant** 3: possessing luck/dignity (epithet of Viṣṇu - Kṛṣṇa).

*   **bhikṣ** 1 Ā (*bhikṣate*, Pass. *bhikṣyate*, PPP *bhikṣita*): to beg (originally a desiderative of *bhaj*: to wish to partake).
    From this:
    *   **bhikṣu** m.: beggar, monk.

*   **duṣ** 4 P (*duṣyati*, Pass. *duṣyate*, PPP *duṣṭa*): to spoil (intransitive), to go bad, to be ruined.
    *   **doṣa** m.: fault, defect.
*   **pac** 1 U (*pacati*, Pass. *pacyate*, no PPP, instead *pakva* 3: cooked, done): to cook, roast, bake (transitive).
