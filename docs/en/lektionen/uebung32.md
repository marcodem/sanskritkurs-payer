B) Translate and resolve the compounds in Sanskrit:

1. आसीत्क्षत्रिय उपपन्नो गुणैरिष्टै रूपवान् । स जनेन्द्राग्रे ऽतिष्ठत् । स देवानयजतारीनजयज्जनानपान्महापुण्यमकरोत् । तस्मान्मृत्वा देवलोके पुनर्भवमलभत ॥१॥ जनस्येन्द्रस्याग्रे । महत्पुण्यम् । देवानां लोके । Once there was a prince who had all desirable virtues, of beautiful form. He stood out among all human princes. He sacrificed to the gods, defeated the enemies, guarded the people, and performed meritorious deeds; therefore, after his death, he was reborn in a world of gods.

2. ब्राह्मणो महानगरे ऽवसत् । स पुत्रमागमय्यावक् । ब्राह्मणपुत्रो वेदं गुरावधीयीतेति । तच्छ्रुत्वा स पुत्रो ऽध्ययनाय गुरुमैत् । गुरुगृहे प्रविश्य गुरुमुपातिष्ठद्गुरुश्च तं पुत्रम् ब्राह्मणमपृच्छत् । ततस्तेन पुत्रेणान्नमादयत् ॥२॥ ब्राह्मणस्य पुत्रः । गुरोर्गृहे । A Brahmin lived in a large city. He sent for his son and spoke: "A Brahmin's son should study the Veda with a master." Upon these words, the son went to a master to study. He entered the master's house and stood respectfully before the master. The master inquired about the Brahmin. Then he gave that son food to eat.

3. राम आचर्यमुपसंगम्य वचनमब्रवीत् ॥३॥ Rāma approached the teacher and spoke the words.

4. ब्राह्मणा वेदमध्यैयत चाध्यापयंश्च देवांश्चायजन्नयजन्त च क्षत्रियाः श्रुतिमध्यैयत जनानरक्षन्महीमभुञ्जन्देवानयजन्त वैश्या वेदमध्यैयत देवानयजन्ताक्रीणन्व्यक्रीणत च द्विजदासास्तु शूद्रा आसन् ॥४॥ द्विजानां दासाः । Brahmins studied and taught the Veda, and sacrificed to the gods as patrons and on behalf of others. Kṣatriyas studied the revelation (Sruti), protected the people, enjoyed the earth, and sacrificed to the gods as patrons. Vaiśyas studied the Veda, sacrificed to the gods as patrons, bought and sold. Śūdras however were bondservants of the twice-born.

5. बुद्धपुत्राः सत्यमजानन्दुःखमरुन्धन्मोक्षं प्राप्नुवन् । बुद्धपुत्र इति बुद्धमार्गभिक्षुरुच्यते ॥५॥ बुद्धस्य पुत्राः । Buddha's sons knew the truth, brought suffering to a standstill, and attained liberation. A "son of Buddha" is a monk on the path of Buddha.

1.) After the son is born, the Brahmin woman sends a servant to the Brahmin. The Brahmin lets this servant enter the house and then asks about the son. The servant says that the son is well. When he has heard that, the Brahmin becomes happy.

पुत्रे जाते ब्राह्मणी दासं ब्राह्मणं गमयति । ब्राह्मणस्तं दासं गृहं प्रवेश्य पुत्रं पृच्छति । सुभगः पुत्र इति दासो वक्ति । तच्छ्रुत्वा ब्राह्मणो सुखतां गच्छति ॥१॥

2.) The Saint has endured the evil done to (him).

साधुना कृतं पापं सोढम् ॥२॥

3.) Morality is the man's ornament.

शीलं नरस्य भूषणम् ॥३॥

4.) The powerful warriors have gone to the Brahmin village.

बलवद्योधा ब्राह्मणग्रामं गताः ॥४॥

5.) The girl cries.

बाला रोदिति ॥५॥

6.) There is no disease equal to lust, there is no enemy like confusion, there is no fire like anger, there is no happiness like knowledge.

> नास्ति कामसमो व्याधिर्नास्ति मोहसमो रिपुः ।  
> नास्ति क्रोधसमो वह्निर्नास्ति ज्ञानसमं सुखम् ॥६॥

7.) A man whom the goddess protects is happy.

यं नरं देवी रक्षति स सुखवान' ॥७॥

8.) With whichever wind a cloud releases water (वारि n.), with that same wind a scholar moves his umbrella.

> येन येन च वातेन वारिदो वारिं मुञ्चति ।  
> तेन तेन च वातेन छत्रं वहति पण्डितः ॥८॥

9.) There are no fruitful activities of classes, stages of life, etc.

नैव सन्ति वर्णाश्रमादीनां फलदायिकाः क्रियाः ॥९॥

10) The cycle of rebirths has no beginning.

अनादिकालिकः संसारः ॥१०॥

11) It is time to devote oneself to food.

कालो भोजनं सेवितुम् ॥११॥

12) Welcome to the queen.

स्वागतं देव्यै ॥१२॥

13) For the sake of heaven, people perform meritorious deeds.

स्वर्गेभ्यो नराः पुण्यं कुर्वते ॥१३॥

14) A man who, out of arrogance, greed, anger, or fear, speaks a judgment falsely, goes to a hell.

> मानाद्वा यदि वा लोभात्क्रोधाद्वा यदि वा भयाट् ।  
> यो न्यायमन्यथा ब्रूते स याति नरकं नरः ॥१४॥

15) Rāma went from the village to the city at the teacher's instruction, entered the holy man's house, stood respectfully before the saint, and speaks: "Desist from anger!"

गुर्वादेशाद्रामो ग्रामान्नगरं गत्वा साधुगृहं प्रविश्य साधुमुपस्थायालं क्रोधेनेति वक्ति ॥१५॥

16) Always (let his) connection be with those who have grown in the sciences, so that his education/good behavior may grow. (This) because education/good behavior has the connection with such ones as its root.

नित्यो विद्यावृद्धसंयोगो विनयवृद्ध्यर्थम् । तन्मूलत्वाद्विनयस्य ॥१६॥

17) While the teacher stands, the boy must not sit.

गुरौ तिष्ठति बाल आसितुम् नार्हति ॥१७॥

18) There is no better refuge than Rāma.

रामान्नास्ति परायणं प्रतरम् ॥१८॥

19) Viṣṇumitra has Rāma send Govinda to the village.

विष्णुमित्रो रामेण गोविन्दं ग्रामं गमयति ॥१९॥

20) He has Devadatta cook rice.

स देवदत्तेनौदनं पाचयति ॥२०॥

21) It is the Dharma of the Arians that Brahmin sons should study the sections of the Veda and Smṛti repeatedly.

ब्राह्मणपुत्रा वेदाध्यायांश्च स्मृत्यध्यायांश्च पुनः पुनरधीयीरन्नित्यार्यधर्मः ॥२१॥

22) The teacher taught the boys the Veda and then went into the house.

गुरुर्बालानवेदमधाप्य गृहं गतः ॥२२॥

23) Which amulet has protected the girl?

कया रक्षिकया बाला रक्षिता ॥२३॥

24) Truth is the light of the world.

सत्यं लोकस्य दीपः ॥२४॥

25) To whom do these houses belong?

केषामिमानi गृहाणि ॥२५॥

26) The Dharma of all is: non-injury, truth, purity, lack of envy, non-malice, and patience.

सर्वेषां धर्मो ऽहिंसा सत्यं शौचमनसूयानृशंस्यं क्षमा च ॥२६॥

27) The Kṣatriyas, who have defeated the enemies, sit in the house.

जितशत्रुक्षत्रिया गृह आसते ॥२७॥

28) She is a (real) wife who speaks lovingly; he however is a (true) son who lives. He lives who possesses good qualities; he lives who possesses Dharma.

> सा भार्या या प्रियं ब्रूते  
> स पुत्रो यस्तु जीवति ।  
> स जीवति गुणो यस्य  
> धर्मो यस्य स जीवति ॥२८॥

29) The prince of gods defeats the non-Arians, who are enemies of Indra. (Passive)

इन्द्रशत्र्वनार्या देवेन्द्रेण जीयन्ते ॥२९॥

30) Yoga of action consists of austerity (tapas n.), recitation (of Veda), and devotion to the Lord. It serves the manifestation of meditative absorption and the weakening of the kleśas (afflictions).

तपःस्वाध्यायेश्वरप्रणिधानानि क्रियायोगः । समाधिभावनार्थः क्लेशतनूकरनार्थश्च ॥३०॥

31) Intake of food, sleep, fear, and mating: this is a commonality of people with animals. In Dharma indeed lies their additional specialty. Bereft of Dharma, they are equal to animals.

> आहारनिद्राभयमैथुनं च सामान्यमेतत्पशुभिर्नराणाम् ।  
> धर्मो हि तेषामधिको विशेषो धमेण हीनाः पशुभिः समानाः ॥३१॥

32) People are born to die.

मरणाय जना जायन्ते ॥३२॥

33) Hells exist because of evil. Evil has poverty as its origin. Poverty arises through not giving.

> भवन्ति नरकाः पापात्पापं दारिद्र्यसंभवम् ।  
> दारिद्र्यमप्रदानेन ॥३३॥

34) It is the Dharma of the Kṣatriyas that the Kṣatriyas should protect the people from the enemies.

क्षत्रिया जनाञ्छत्रुभ्यो रक्षितुमर्हन्तीति क्षत्रियधर्मः ॥३४॥

35) Therefore, the three sciences have the staff (of governance) as their root. Governance, which has education/discipline as its root, brings gain and secure possession to living beings.

तस्मद्दण्डमूलास्तिस्रो विद्याः । विनयमूलो दण्डः प्राणभृतां योगक्षेमावहः ॥३५॥

36) Bad people do not listen when the teacher speaks about the Dharma.

धर्मं वदति गुरौ दुर्जना न शृण्वन्ति ॥३६॥

37) To that Rāma be reverence!

रामाय तस्मै नमः ॥३७॥

38) The noble Hari is my way/goal, who sent his enemies to a heaven, made his own people know the meaning of the Veda, gave the gods the food of immortality (amṛta) to eat, taught the creator (vidhi) the Veda, and set the earth firmly in the water.

> शत्रूनगमयत्स्वर्गं वेदार्थं स्वानवेदयत् ।  
> आशयच्चामृतं देवानवेदमध्यापयद्विधिम् ।  
> आशयत्सलिले पृथ्वीं यः स मे श्रीहरिर्गतिः ॥३८॥

39) Viṣṇu shows himself to his devotees.

विष्णुर्भक्तान्दर्शयते ॥३९॥

40) Governance that is not applied brings about the law of the fishes (the strong eating the weak).

अप्रणीतो दण्डो मात्स्यन्यायमुद्भावयति ॥४०॥

41) He who possesses riches has friends; he who possesses riches has relatives; he who possesses riches is a man in the world; he who possesses riches is indeed a scholar.

> यस्यार्थास्तस्य मित्राणि यस्यार्थास्तस्य बान्धवाः ।  
> यस्यार्थाः स पुमांल्लोके यस्यार्थाः स हि पण्डितः ॥४१॥

मृतम् दहन्नग्निः सतीमपि दहति ॥४२॥

अन्नं पक्त्वा ब्राह्मणदास्यत्ति ॥४३॥

44) Now it's enough!

अलं क्षमया ॥४४॥

45) This fruit is enough for him to eat.

इदं फलमलं तस्य खादनाय ॥४५॥

46) The innermost temple shrine is a house for the image of the god.

देवप्रतिमायै गृहं गर्भगृहम् ॥४६॥

47) A thief is freed from theft by punishment or by release. But if the king does not punish him, he receives the guilt of the thief.

> शासनाद्वा विमोक्षाद्वा स्तेनः स्तेयाfविमुच्यते ।  
> अशासित्वा तु तं राजा स्तेनस्याप्नोति किल्बिषम् ॥४७॥

48) Because he made a mistake in the sacrifice, the Brahmin is not worthy to receive riches.

कृतयज्ञदोषत्वाद्ब्राह्मणो धनं लब्धुं नार्हति ॥४८॥

49) When the initiation ceremony has taken place, he should acquire the Veda and philosophy from learned ones, economics from department heads.

वृत्तोपनयनस्त्रयीमान्वीक्षिकीं च शिष्टेभ्यो वार्त्तामध्यक्षेभ्य् उपयुञ्जीत ॥५९॥

50) Vaiśyadharma is that Vaiśyas should live by buying and selling. Since it is so, Vaiśya sons buy and sell.

क्रयेण च विक्रयेण च वैश्या जीवेयुरिति वैश्यधर्मः । एवं सति वैश्यपुत्राः क्रीणन्ति विक्रीणते च ॥५०॥

51) One should speak the truth, one should speak pleasant things; one should not speak an unpleasant truth and one should also not speak a pleasant untruth. This is the eternal Dharma.

> सत्यं ब्रूयात्प्रियं ब्रूयान्न ब्रूयात्सत्यमप्रियम् ।  
> प्रियं च नानृतं ब्रूयादेष धर्मः सनातनः ॥५१॥

52) Goodbye! See you again!

पुनर्दर्शनाय ॥५२॥

Once an old man, traveling to another village, became tired on the path. Therefore, he went to the foot of a mango tree standing nearby to rest. On this tree, there were ripe fruits. The old man felt a longing for them. But he could not climb the tree and reach them. Fortunately, some monkeys were on that tree, eating fruits. Seeing them, the old man felt great joy. What did he do? He took some stones, aimed at the monkeys, and threw them. The monkeys became angry, plucked some fruits, and threw them at the old man. The old man joyfully took them and went to his desired destination. O, the cleverness of the old man! (from: Saṃskṛtabālādarśa)

आदाय Absolutive of आ-दा (3rd present class) "to take"
