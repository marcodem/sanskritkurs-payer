# Lesson 53 (Vacation Lesson)

## 53.1. The Dual (*dvivacana*) of Nouns

The Dual (*dvivacana*) is used to designate exactly "two":
*   **aśvinau**: "the two Ashvins."
*   **hastau**: "the two hands" (of an individual).
*   **pādau**: "the two feet" (of a human or animal).

Sometimes the Dual denotes a masculine and a feminine member of the same class:
*   **pitarau**: "father and mother" = "parents."

Words meaning "a pair" (e.g., *yuga* n., *dvandva* n., *dvaya* n.) are always used in the Singular unless two or more pairs are meant.
*   **bāhudvayam**: "a pair of arms."

Fig.: *mārjārayugam* (A pair of cats).

## 53.2. Noun Dual Endings

| Case | Masculine / Feminine | Neuter |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Nom. / Acc. / Voc.** | **-au** | **-ī** |
| **Instr. / Dat. / Abl.** | **-bhyām** | **-bhyām** |
| **Gen. / Loc.** | **-os** | **-os** |

For nouns with stem gradation, the Nom./Acc./Voc. Dual M/F uses the **strong stem**.

## 53.3. Dual of Consonant Stems

### 53.3.1. Stems without Gradation
*   **satyavāc** 3 ("truth-speaking"): Nom. *satyavācau* (M/F), *satyavācī* (N); Instr. *satyavāgbhyām*.
*   **balin** 3 ("strong"): Nom. *balinau* (M), *balinī* (N); Instr. *balibhyām*.
*   **sumanas** 3 ("benevolent"): Nom. *sumanasau* (M/F), *sumanasī* (N); Instr. *sumanobhyām*.
*   **havis** n. ("oblation"): Nom. *haviṣī*; Instr. *havirbhyām*.

### 53.3.2. Stems with Gradation
*   **bharant** 3 ("bearing"): Nom. *bharantau* (M), *bharantī* (N); Instr. *bharadbhyām*. ![](/images/lekt5305.jpg)
*   **mahal** 3 ("great"): Nom. *mahāntau* (M), *mahatī* (N); Instr. *mahadbhyām*.
*   **ātman** m. ("self"): Nom. *ātmānau*; Instr. *ātmabhyām*.
*   **rājan** m. ("king"): Nom. *rājānau*; Instr. *rājabhyām*; Gen./Loc. **rājños**.
*   **nāman** n. ("name"): Nom. *nāmnī* / *nāmānī*; Instr. *nāmabhyām*.

## 53.5. *Dualdvandva* Compounds

Dual Dvandvas represent two items as a pair.
*   **arthadharmau**: "Profit and Duty."
*   **pitāputrau**: "Father and Son." (Note the Nom. sg. form of the first member). ![](/images/lekt5308.jpg)
*   **mātāpitarau**: "Mother and Father."
*   **mitrāvaruṇau**: "Mitra and Varuna." (Note the vowel lengthening).

## 53.7. Pronominal Adjectives
The following are declined like *yad* (using Dual forms when applicable):
*   **anya** (another), **anyatara** (one of two), **itara** (the other).
*   **ubha** (both) — used **only** in the Dual.
*   **ubhaya** (both) — has no Dual; used in Sg. and Pl.

## 53.8. Irregular Comparison (*-īyas / -iṣṭha*)

While *-tara* and *-tama* are added to the stem, *-īyas* (Comparative) and *-iṣṭha* (Superlative) are added directly to the **root**.
*   **Guru** (heavy) → **garīyas** (heavier), **gariṣṭha** (heaviest).
*   **Prasashya** (praiseworthy) → **śreyas** (better), **śreṣṭha** (best).
*   **Antika** (near) → **nedīyas**, **nediṣṭha**.
*   **Yuvan** (young) → **yavīyas**, **yaviṣṭha**.
*   **Bahal** (much) → **bhūyas**, **bhūyiṣṭha**.

## 53.9. Declension of *-īyas* Stems

Comparative adjectives on *-īyas* (e.g., *garīyas*) have a specific declension:
*   Masc. Nom. sg. **garīyān**, Akk. sg. **garīyāṃsam**, Nom./Akk. du. **garīyāṃsau**, Instr. pl. **garīyobhis**.
*   Neut. Nom./Akk. sg. **garīyas**, pl. **garīyāṃsi**.
*   Fem. **garīyasī** (declines like *devī*).

## 53.10. Metrics (*Chandaḥ*)

Metric determination is essential for aesthetic, critical, and chronological analysis of Sanskrit literature.

### 53.10.3. Quantity
Syllables are either **laghu** (light: ˘) or **guru** (heavy: ˉ).
A syllable is **heavy** if:
1. It contains a long vowel.
2. It has an *anusvāra*.
3. It has a *visarga*.
4. It is followed by a consonant cluster.
5. It is at the end of a *pāda* (verse-quarter).

### 53.10.4. The Epic *Śloka* (Anuṣṭubh)
The most common Sanskrit meter. It consists of two half-verses of 16 syllables each, further divided into four *pādas* of 8 syllables.
**General Rule for the Second Half of a *Pāda* (Pathyā):**
*   Pādas a & c (odd): **˘ ˉ ˉ ̽**
*   Pādas b & d (even): **˘ ˉ ˘ ̽**
*(Syllables 5, 6, 7 are critical: 5 is usually light, 6 heavy).*
