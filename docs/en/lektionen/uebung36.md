D) Translate:

पुत्रे जाते सुगतः कुलं धनं च तत्याजागाराच्चानगर्यं प्रवव्राज । बुद्ध्यार्यसत्यानि प्रज्ञाय प्रज्ञया च दुःखान्मुक्तो मोक्तुकामार्यजनान्बोधयामासेति भिक्षव आहुः ॥१॥

When a son was born to him, the Sugata (one who has gone well) left his family and wealth and went from home into homelessness. Having realized the Noble Truths with his intellect and been freed from suffering through insight, he awakened the noble people who desired liberation—so say the monks.

ब्राह्मणा महादेवयज्ञायाग्निं चिक्यिरे । ब्राह्मणेष्विन्द्रादिदेवान्स्तुवत्स्वग्निर्यज्ञान्नमाश । एवं यज्ञेन ब्राह्मणा महादेवैरादयां चक्रुस्तांश्च स्तोत्रानि श्रावयां बभूवुः ॥२॥

The Brahmins built a fire for the sacrifice to the Great God. While the Brahmins were praising Indra and other gods, the sacrificial fire consumed the food. Thus, through the sacrifice, the Brahmins caused the great gods to eat and made them hear songs of praise.

रक्षितधर्मक्षत्रिययोधा महानगरं जेतुकामाञ्छत्रून्विजिग्युर्न तु जघ्नुः ॥३॥

The Kṣatriya warriors who guarded the Dharma defeated the enemies who desired to conquer the large city, but they did not kill them.

अधीतवेदद्विजो द्विजधर्मं वेद ॥४॥

A twice-born who has studied the Veda knows the Dharma of the twice-born.

विद्ययैव जीवितुं न शक्यते । य एवं विदुर्नाधीयीरन् ॥५॥

One cannot live by science alone. Those who know this should not study.

स साधुर्दुर्जनपापलोभमतिमीक्षां चक्रे ॥६॥

That holy man saw the evil, greedy mind of the wicked people.

ब्राह्मणीभिः स्वान्नानि पेचिरे ॥७॥

The Brahmin women cooked their own foods.

Translate:

1\. Mahābhārata 13.7.25-26

> येन प्रीणति पितरं  
> तेन प्रीतः प्रजापतिः ।  
> प्रीणति मातरं yeन  
> पृथिवी तेन पूजिता ।  
> येन प्रीणात्युपाध्यायं  
> तेन स्याद्ब्रह्म पूजितम् ।  
> सर्वे तस्यादृता धर्मा  
> यस्यैते त्रय आदृताः ।  
> अनादृतास्तु यस्यैते  
> सर्वास्तस्याफलाः क्रियाः ॥

By whatever one pleases his father, the Creator (Prajāpati) is pleased thereby. By whatever one pleases his mother, the Earth is worshiped thereby. By whatever one pleases his teacher, Brahman is worshiped thereby. All duties are respected for him for whom these three are respected. But for him for whom these are not respected, all actions are fruitless.

2\. Manusmṛti 4.154 on right behavior toward elders:

> अभिवादयेद्वृद्धांश्च दद्याच्चैवासनं स्वकम् ।  
> कृताञ्जलिरुपासीत गच्छतः पृष्ठतो ऽन्वियात् ॥

One should greet the aged, give them one's own seat, and serve them with folded hands; one should follow behind them when they go.

3\. Manusmṛti 2.98: Who is a "jitendriya" (one who has conquered the senses):

> श्रुत्वा स्पृष्ट्वा च दृष्ट्वा च भुक्त्वा घ्रात्वा च यो नरः ।  
> न हृष्यति ग्लायति वा स विज्ञेयो जितेन्द्रियः ॥

When a man does not rejoice or grieve after hearing, touching, seeing, eating, or smelling, he is to be known as one who has conquered his senses.

4\. Manusmṛti 2.110 on the right behavior of a Brahmin:

> नापृष्टः कस्यचिद्ब्रूयान्न चान्यायेन पृच्छतः ।  
> जानन्नपि हि मेधावी जडवल्लोक आचरेत् ॥

Unasked he should not speak to anyone, nor to one who asks improperly. Even though he knows, a wise man should behave in the world like a simpleton.
