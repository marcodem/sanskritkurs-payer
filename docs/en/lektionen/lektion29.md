# Lesson 29

## 29.1. Use of Parasmaipada and Ātmanepada in the Causative

The Causative is, with few exceptions, **Ubhayapada**.

Rules for the use of **Ātmanepada** in the causative:
The Ātmanepada is used:
1. When the fruit of the caused action benefits the instigator:
   > *rāmaḥ kaṭaṃ kārayate* = "Rāma has a mat made for himself (in his own interest)."
2. When the causative of transitive verbs is used reflexively ("to show oneself," "to let oneself be heard," etc.), or when the object of the simple verb is the agent (*kartṛ*) of the causative:
   > *viṣṇur bhaktān darśayate* = "Viṣṇu shows himself to his devotees."
   (Result: *bhaktā viṣṇuṃ paśyanti* = "The devotees see Viṣṇu.")

These rules generally correspond to the regular rules for the use of Ātmanepada in Ubhayapada verbs.

## 29.2. Vocabulary

**Preverbs:**
*   **ud-**: up, upwards, out.
*   **pari-**: around, about (place, time), throughout.

*   **bhū + pari** 1P (*paribhavati*): to encompass, master, defeat; to neglect, despise.
*   **avajñāna** n.: neglect, contempt.
*   **gupta** 3: protected, guarded.
*   **gṛhastha** 3: being in the house; m.: householder (one in the 2nd *āśrama*).
*   **gras** 1Ā (*grasate*, Fut. *grasiṣyate*, Pass. *grasyate*, Kaus. *grāsayati*, PPP *grasta*, Inf. *grasitum*): to consume, devour.
*   **tīkṣṇa** 3: sharp, pointed, severe, intense, sharp-tongued.
*   **nyāya** m.: norm, rule, right way; method, logic.
*   **parivrājaka** m.: wanderer, wandering monk, pilgrim.

*   **matsya** m.: fish.
    *   **mātsya** 3: pertaining to fish.

*   **śuci** 3: shining, bright, pure; m.: purity.
*   **pūj** 10P (*pūjayati*): to honor, venerate.

## 29.3. Translation Exercise for Review

Translate literally into good English and memorize the Sanskrit texts:

1. **Definition of *avidyā*:**
   *anityāśuciduḥkhānātmasu nityaśucisukhātmakhyātir avidyā || Yoga Sūtra 2.5 ||*
   Explanation: *ātmasu* = Loc. sg. of *ātman* m. "soul; the Absolute realized in the individual."

2. **Kauṭilīya Arthaśāstra 1.4 on the right use of *daṇḍa*:**
   *tīkṣṇadaṇḍo bhūtānāmudvejanīyo bhavati | 8 | mṛdudaṇḍaḥ paribhuūyate | 9 | yathārhadaṇḍaḥ pūjyate | 10 | suvijñātaprṇīto hi daṇḍaḥ prajā dharmārthakāmair yojayati | 11 | duṣpraṇītaḥ kāmakrodhābhyām avajñānādvā vānaprasthaparivrājakān api kopayati, kimaṅga punar gṛhasthān | 12 | apraṇītastu mātsyanāyam udbhāvayati | 13 | balīyān abalaṃ hi grasate daṇḍadharābhāve | 14 | sa tena guptaḥ prabhavatīti | 15 |*

   > *caturvarṇāśramo loko*
   > *rājñā daṇḍena pālitaḥ |*
   > *svadharmakarmābhirato*
   > *vartate sveṣu vartmasu || 16 ||*

![](/images/lekt2902.jpg)
Fig.: *mātsyanāyaḥ* (The law of the fishes).

**Explanations:**
- (8) *udvejanīya* 3: "something/someone to be shuddered at."
- (11) *vijñāta* 3: "known," "understood."
- (11) *yojayati* (Kaus. of *yuj*): "to yoke, connect with, unite with."
- (12) *kāmakrodhābhyām*: Instr./Dat./Abl. dual masc. of *kāmakrodha*.
- (12) *kimaṅga*: "how much more."
- (14) *balīyān*: Nom. sg. masc. of *balīyas* 3 "stronger."
- (16) *catur*: "four" in compounds.
- (16) *rājñā*: Instr. sg. masc. of *rājan* m. "king."
- (16) *sveṣu*: Loc. pl. masc./neut. of *sva* 3 "own."
- (16) *vartmasu*: Loc. pl. neut. of *vartman* n. "path, track."
