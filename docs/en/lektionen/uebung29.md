# Exercise 29

## Exercise: Philosophical Texts

Translate the following passages and memorize the key terms.

### 1. Definition of Avidyā (Yoga Sūtra 2.5)

अनित्याशुचिदुःखानात्मसु नित्यशुचिसुखात्मख्यातिरविद्या ॥
"Ignorance (*avidyā*) is the perception of the eternal, the pure, the joyful, and the Self (*Atman*) in what is non-eternal, impure, painful, and non-Self."

### 2. The Right Use of the Rod (Arthashastra 1.4)

तीक्ष्णदण्डो भूतानामुद्वेजनीयो भवति । मृदुदण्डः परिभूयते । यथार्हदण्डः पूज्यते । सुविज्ञातप्रणीतो हि दण्डः प्रजा धर्मार्थकामैर्योजयति । ... अप्रणीतस्तु मात्स्यन्यायमुद्भावयति । बलीयानबलं हि ग्रसते दण्डधराभावे । स तेन गुप्तः प्रभवतीति ।
"A harsh rule (*tīkṣṇa-daṇḍaḥ*) makes beings tremble. A lax rule is despised. A just rule is revered. Rule (*daṇḍa*) guided by wisdom unites the subjects with Dharma, prosperity, and joy. ... When rule is absent, the 'Law of the Fish' (*mātsya-nyāya*) arises—for the stronger devours the weak in the absence of a rod-bearer. Protected by the rod-bearer, the weak one thrives."

> चतुर्वर्णाश्रमो लोको / राज्ञा दण्डेन पालितः । / स्वधर्मकर्माभिरतो / वर्तते स्वेषु वर्त्मसु ॥
> "The world, with its four castes (*varna*) and stages of life (*āśrama*), protected by the King through the rod (*daṇḍa*), delights in activity according to its own Dharma and custom, and moves along its own paths."

## Key Terms

* **Avidyā**: Ignorance; the root cause of suffering.
* **Daṇḍa**: Lit. "rod"; governance, punishment, political power.
* **Mātsya-nyāya**: "Law of the Fish"; a state of anarchy where the strong eat the weak.
* **Catur-varṇāśrama-lokaḥ**: The world structured by four castes and four life-stages.
